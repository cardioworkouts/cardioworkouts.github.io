Should You Do Cardio Before Working Out

Written by Luffy Sep 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

When it comes to working out, there are always questions about the most effective way to do it. One question that comes up often is whether or not cardio should be done before strength training. In this blog post, we will explore the pros and cons of doing cardio before working out and help you decide if it's right for you.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance between cardio and strength training. For some, doing cardio before lifting weights can lead to fatigue and decreased performance during their strength training session. Others believe that doing cardio first can help to warm up the body and prevent injury during strength training.

The answer to whether or not you should do cardio before working out ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. Let's explore some of the factors to consider when making this decision.

Overall, the decision to do cardio before working out will depend on your specific fitness goals and needs. If you are looking to improve cardiovascular fitness, doing cardio before strength training may be the best option for you. However, if you are focused on building strength and muscle, you may want to save your energy for lifting weights and do cardio after your strength training session.

Personal Experience with Cardio Before Working Out

As someone who enjoys both cardio and strength training, I have experimented with doing cardio before and after my strength training sessions. I have found that doing cardio before lifting weights helps me to feel more energized and focused during my strength training session. However, I also feel more fatigued and find it harder to lift heavy weights.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio Before Working Out

There are both pros and cons to doing cardio before working out. Some of the benefits of doing cardio first include:

  • Increased cardiovascular fitness
  • Improved endurance
  • Reduced risk of injury during strength training

However, there are also some drawbacks to doing cardio before strength training, such as:

  • Decreased energy for lifting weights
  • Increased risk of injury during cardio due to fatigue
  • Reduced muscle activation during strength training

Cardio Before Working Out and Weight Loss

If weight loss is your primary fitness goal, doing cardio before strength training may be beneficial. Cardio is a great way to burn calories and increase your heart rate, which can help to improve weight loss. However, it's important to remember that weight loss is ultimately determined by your overall diet and calorie intake.

How to Incorporate Cardio Before Working Out

If you decide to do cardio before strength training, it's important to warm up properly and gradually increase the intensity of your cardio workout. Start with a 5-10 minute warm-up, such as a light jog or brisk walk, and gradually increase the intensity over time. Be sure to listen to your body and stop if you feel fatigued or experience any pain or discomfort.

My Personal Preference

For me, the best approach is to do a short cardio warm-up before lifting weights, such as a 5-10 minute jog or brisk walk on the treadmill. This helps to get my heart rate up and prepare my body for the workout ahead. After my strength training session, I like to do a longer cardio workout, such as a 30-minute run or cycle, to improve my overall cardiovascular fitness.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you are focused on building strength and muscle, it may be better to save your energy for lifting weights and do cardio after your strength training session. However, if you are looking to improve cardiovascular fitness, doing cardio before strength training may be the best option for you.

Q: Can doing cardio before lifting weights help prevent injury?

A: Yes, doing cardio before strength training can help to warm up the body and prevent injury during your lifting session. However, it's important to warm up properly and gradually increase the intensity of your cardio workout.

Q: Will doing cardio before lifting weights decrease my strength gains?

A: There is some evidence to suggest that doing cardio before lifting weights may reduce muscle activation and strength gains. However, the impact of cardio on strength training is not well understood and may vary depending on the individual.

Q: How long should I do cardio before lifting weights?

A: It's best to start with a 5-10 minute warm-up and gradually increase the intensity of your cardio workout. Be sure to listen to your body and stop if you feel fatigued or experience any pain or discomfort.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before Working Out

Ultimately, the decision to do cardio before working out depends on your specific fitness goals and needs. If you are looking to improve cardiovascular fitness, doing cardio before strength training may be the best option for you. However, if you are focused on building strength and muscle, you may want to save your energy for lifting weights and do cardio after your strength training session. Remember to warm up properly and listen to your body to prevent injury and get the most out of your workouts.
