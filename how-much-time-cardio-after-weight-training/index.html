How Much Time Should You Do Cardio After Weight Training

Written by Petter Nov 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who loves hitting the gym and working out but is confused about how much time you should do cardio after weight training? You're not alone! It's a common question among fitness enthusiasts, and in this article, we'll dive deep into the topic and provide you with some useful insights.

After an intense weight training session, your body is in a state of high metabolism, and your muscles are craving nutrients to recover and grow. At the same time, cardio can help you burn calories and improve your cardiovascular health. But how much time should you spend on cardio after weight training?

The general recommendation is to do 20-30 minutes of cardio after weight training, but this can vary depending on your fitness goals and schedule. If you have limited time, you can do a quick 10-minute cardio session to cool down and stretch your muscles. On the other hand, if your goal is to lose weight, you may want to increase the duration or intensity of your cardio workout.

My Personal Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I love hitting the gym and working out. I usually spend 45-60 minutes on weight training, followed by 30 minutes of cardio. However, on days when I'm short on time, I do a quick 10-minute cardio session to cool down and stretch my muscles. It's important to listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

How Cardio After Weight Training Affects Your Body

Cardio after weight training can have a positive impact on your body. It can help you burn more calories, increase your endurance, and improve your cardiovascular health. However, if you overdo it or do not give your body enough time to recover, it can lead to muscle breakdown and fatigue. It's essential to strike a balance between weight training and cardio and give your body enough time to rest and recover.

The Best Time to Do Cardio After Weight Training

The best time to do cardio after weight training is immediately after your weight training session. This is because your body is already in a state of high metabolism, and doing cardio at this time can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health. However, if you prefer doing cardio before weight training, make sure to warm up properly and keep the intensity low to avoid muscle fatigue.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Training into Your Workout Routine

If you're looking to incorporate cardio after weight training into your workout routine, here are some tips:

  • Start with a low to moderate intensity cardio workout.
  • Gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio workout over time.
  • Try different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming, to keep your workout interesting and challenging.
  • Listen to your body and adjust your workout routine accordingly.

Common Questions About How Much Time Cardio After Weight Training

1. Should I do cardio before or after weight training?

It's recommended to do cardio after weight training as your body is already in a state of high metabolism. However, if you prefer doing cardio before weight training, make sure to warm up properly and keep the intensity low to avoid muscle fatigue.

2. How much time should I do cardio after weight training?

The general recommendation is to do 20-30 minutes of cardio after weight training, but this can vary depending on your fitness goals and schedule.

3. Can doing too much cardio after weight training be harmful?

Yes, doing too much cardio after weight training can lead to muscle breakdown and fatigue. It's essential to strike a balance between weight training and cardio and give your body enough time to rest and recover.

4. Can I skip cardio after weight training?

Yes, you can skip cardio after weight training if you're short on time or don't feel like doing it. However, incorporating cardio into your workout routine can have several health benefits and help you achieve your fitness goals.

Conclusion of How Much Time Cardio After Weight Training

In conclusion, cardio after weight training can have several health benefits, but it's essential to strike a balance between weight training and cardio and give your body enough time to rest and recover. The general recommendation is to do 20-30 minutes of cardio after weight training, but this can vary depending on your fitness goals and schedule. Listen to your body, adjust your workout routine accordingly, and incorporate cardio into your workout routine for better health and fitness.
