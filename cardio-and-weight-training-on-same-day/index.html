Cardio And Weight Training On Same Day A Beginner s Guide

Written by Frank Nov 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who wants to make the most out of your workout routine but doesn't have enough time in a day? Do you feel confused about whether you should do cardio or weight training first? If yes, then you have come to the right place.

Combining cardio and weight training on the same day can be daunting for many, but it's an effective way to get a full-body workout. However, doing both in a single session can be challenging, and it's understandable to have some concerns.

The primary target of cardio and weight training on the same day is to maximize your fitness gains while saving time. By doing both workouts in a single session, you can improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and build muscle mass simultaneously.

In this article, we will discuss the benefits of combining cardio and weight training on the same day, the best time to do it, and tips to ensure you get the most out of your workout routine.

Cardio and Weight Training on Same Day: Benefits

One of the most significant advantages of combining cardio and weight training is that it helps you burn more calories than doing either workout alone. Cardio exercises help you burn calories and fat, while weight training increases your metabolism, leading to more calorie burn throughout the day.

Another benefit is that you can improve your cardiovascular health by doing cardio exercises, which can help you perform better during weight training. Furthermore, weight training can help you build lean muscle mass, which can improve your body composition and overall strength.

However, it's essential to keep in mind that combining both workouts can be challenging, especially for beginners. It's crucial to start slow and gradually increase the intensity to avoid injury or burnout.

Cardio and Weight Training on Same Day: Best Time

The best time to do cardio and weight training on the same day depends on your fitness goals and personal preference. However, many fitness experts recommend doing weight training first, followed by cardio.

Doing weight training first can help you focus on building strength and muscle mass, which requires more energy and focus than cardio exercises. Furthermore, weight training can increase your heart rate and prepare your body for cardio exercises. After weight training, you can do cardio exercises to burn calories and improve your cardiovascular health.

Tips for Cardio and Weight Training on Same Day

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your cardio and weight training workout:

  • Start with a warm-up to prepare your body for the workout routine.
  • Choose exercises that work different muscle groups to avoid overtraining.
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts to avoid burnout or injury.
  • Stay hydrated throughout the workout to maintain optimal performance.
  • Listen to your body and take rest days as needed to avoid overtraining.

How to Combine Cardio and Weight Training on Same Day

Here's an example workout routine that combines cardio and weight training:

  1. Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio (jogging, cycling, etc.)
  2. Weight training: 30-45 minutes of resistance training (squats, lunges, push-ups, etc.)
  3. Cardio: 20-30 minutes of moderate to high-intensity cardio (running, cycling, rowing, etc.)
  4. Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of stretching and light cardio

Question and Answer

Q: Is it okay to do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, it's okay to do cardio and weight training on the same day. In fact, it's an effective way to get a full-body workout and save time.

Q: Does cardio before weight training burn more fat?

A: It's a common misconception that doing cardio before weight training burns more fat. However, it's not true. The order of the workout doesn't affect fat burn. What matters is the intensity and duration of the workout.

Q: How often should I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and personal preference. However, most fitness experts recommend doing cardio and weight training 3-4 times a week.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day if you're a beginner. However, it's crucial to start slow and gradually increase the intensity to avoid injury or burnout.

Conclusion of Cardio and Weight Training on Same Day

Combining cardio and weight training on the same day can be an effective way to get a full-body workout and save time. However, it's essential to start slow and gradually increase the intensity to avoid injury or burnout. By following the tips and workout routine mentioned above, you can get the most out of your cardio and weight training routine.
