Should I Do Cardio Right After Lifting The Truth Behind This Fitness Myth

Written by Bardi Jul 13, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you one of those people who is always wondering whether you should do cardio right after lifting? If so, you're not alone! Many fitness enthusiasts are confused about this topic and are looking for answers. In this post, we'll give you the lowdown on whether or not you should do cardio right after lifting.

Let's face it, there are a lot of myths and misconceptions when it comes to fitness. And the question of whether you should do cardio right after lifting is a prime example. Some people swear by it, while others avoid it like the plague. So, what's the truth?

The short answer is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer. It really depends on your fitness goals and what you're trying to accomplish. That being said, there are some general guidelines that can help you decide whether or not to do cardio right after lifting.

So, should you do cardio right after lifting? The answer is...it depends. If your goal is to build muscle mass and gain strength, then doing cardio right after lifting may not be the best idea. This is because cardio can deplete your energy stores and make it harder to lift heavy weights. On the other hand, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, then doing cardio right after lifting can be a great way to get in some extra calorie-burning exercise.

The Experience of Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

Personally, I've tried doing cardio right after lifting and I've found that it can be challenging. When I'm lifting weights, I like to give it my all and really push myself. But when I transition to cardio, I find that my energy levels are depleted and I'm not able to perform at my best. That being said, I've also found that doing cardio after lifting can be a great way to improve my overall fitness and help me reach my weight loss goals.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

One of the main benefits of doing cardio right after lifting is that it can help you burn more calories. When you lift weights, your body continues to burn calories even after you've stopped working out. This is known as the afterburn effect. By doing cardio right after lifting, you can take advantage of this effect and burn even more calories. Additionally, doing cardio after lifting can help improve your cardiovascular fitness and endurance.

The Science Behind Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

So, why is it that doing cardio right after lifting can be so challenging? The answer lies in the way your body uses energy. When you lift weights, your body primarily uses carbohydrates for fuel. However, when you do cardio, your body switches to using fat for fuel. This means that if you do cardio right after lifting, your body may not have enough carbohydrates available to fuel your workout, which can lead to fatigue and decreased performance.

How to Do Cardio Right After Lifting

If you do decide to do cardio right after lifting, there are a few things you can do to make it easier on your body. First, make sure you're properly fueling your body before your workout. This means eating a balanced meal that includes carbohydrates and protein. Additionally, make sure you're staying hydrated throughout your workout. Finally, start with a low-intensity cardio workout and gradually work your way up as your body becomes more accustomed to the added stress.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio Right After Lifting

So, should you do cardio right after lifting? As we've seen, the answer is not a simple yes or no. It really depends on your fitness goals and what you're trying to accomplish. However, if you do decide to do cardio right after lifting, be sure to listen to your body and take it slow. With time and practice, you'll be able to find the right balance between lifting and cardio that works best for you.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle mass and gain strength, then lifting should be your priority. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness or lose weight, then doing cardio before lifting can be a good way to warm up and get your heart rate up.

Q: How long should I wait after lifting to do cardio?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after lifting before doing cardio. This will give your body time to recover and replenish its energy stores.

Q: Can doing cardio after lifting help me burn more fat?

A: Yes, doing cardio after lifting can help you burn more fat. When you lift weights, your body primarily uses carbohydrates for fuel. However, when you do cardio, your body switches to using fat for fuel. So, by doing cardio after lifting, you can take advantage of this effect and burn even more fat.

Q: Should I do cardio every day?

A: It's generally recommended to do cardio at least three times a week for optimal health and fitness. However, the exact frequency will depend on your fitness goals and current fitness level.
