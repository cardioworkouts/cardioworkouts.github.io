How Much Cardio A Day For Beginners

Written by Aprilia Nov 10, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you a beginner who wants to incorporate cardio into your workout routine but unsure of how much cardio to do? You're not alone. Many beginners struggle with finding the right amount of cardio that works for them.

The question of how much cardio a day for beginners can be tricky to answer because it depends on various factors such as age, fitness level, goals, and overall health. However, there are some general guidelines that can help you determine the appropriate amount of cardio for your fitness level.

The American Heart Association recommends that adults engage in at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week. For beginners, it's best to start with a lower amount of cardio and gradually increase the duration and intensity over time.

To summarize, as a beginner, it's recommended to start with 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio at least three times per week and gradually work your way up to 150 minutes per week.

My Personal Experience with How Much Cardio a Day for Beginners

When I first started my fitness journey, I was intimidated by the thought of doing cardio. However, I knew that it was essential for my overall health, so I started with 20 minutes of brisk walking on the treadmill three times per week. As I progressed, I gradually increased my cardio duration and intensity to 45 minutes of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) four times per week.

Tips for How Much Cardio a Day for Beginners

1. Start slow and gradually increase your cardio duration and intensity over time.

2. Mix up your cardio routine to prevent boredom and challenge your body.

3. Listen to your body and take rest days when needed.

4. Stay hydrated before, during, and after your cardio sessions.

The Benefits of Cardio for Beginners

Cardio has numerous benefits for beginners, including:

- Improving heart health and circulation

- Boosting energy levels

- Reducing stress and anxiety

- Burning calories and aiding in weight loss

- Increasing endurance and stamina

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Workout Routine

Here are some tips for incorporating cardio into your workout routine:

- Choose cardio activities that you enjoy, such as running, cycling, swimming, or dancing.

- Schedule your cardio sessions at a time that works best for you, whether it's in the morning or after work.

- Track your progress and celebrate your achievements to stay motivated.

FAQs about How Much Cardio a Day for Beginners

Q: Can I do cardio every day as a beginner?

A: It's not recommended to do cardio every day as a beginner. It's important to give your body time to rest and recover between cardio sessions.

Q: How long should I do cardio as a beginner?

A: As a beginner, aim for 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio, at least three times per week, and gradually increase the duration and intensity over time.

Q: What is the best time to do cardio?

A: The best time to do cardio is whenever it works best for you. Some people prefer to do it in the morning, while others prefer to do it after work.

Q: What are some beginner-friendly cardio exercises?

A: Some beginner-friendly cardio exercises include walking, cycling, swimming, dancing, and low-impact aerobics.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio a Day for Beginners

As a beginner, it's essential to start slow and gradually increase the duration and intensity of your cardio workouts. Remember to listen to your body, mix up your cardio routine, and celebrate your achievements to stay motivated. With time and consistency, you'll be able to achieve your fitness goals and reap the many benefits of cardio.
