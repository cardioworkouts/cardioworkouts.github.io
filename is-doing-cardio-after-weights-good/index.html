Is Doing Cardio After Weights Good The Ultimate Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those fitness enthusiasts who have always wondered if doing cardio after weights is good for you? Well, you are not alone. Many people have the same question and are confused about the right way to go about it. In this guide, we will discuss the pros and cons of doing cardio after weights and help you make an informed decision.

The Pain Points Related to Doing Cardio after Weights

When it comes to working out, everyone has different goals. Some people want to build muscle, while others want to lose weight. However, one thing that is common among all gym-goers is the desire to get the most out of their workout. But sometimes, doing too much can be counterproductive. Many people make the mistake of doing cardio after weights, thinking that it will help them burn more calories. However, this can lead to fatigue, decreased performance, and even injury.

Is Doing Cardio after Weights Good?

The short answer is yes, doing cardio after weights is good for you. However, there are a few things you need to keep in mind. First, it is essential to understand that doing weights before cardio can help you burn more fat. This is because lifting weights depletes your glycogen stores, which means your body will burn more fat during cardio. Second, doing cardio after weights can help you improve your cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn more calories.

So, if your main goal is to burn fat, doing weights before cardio is the way to go. But if you want to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance, doing cardio after weights is an excellent option.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio after Weights

Now that we have established that doing cardio after weights is good for you let's take a look at the pros and cons.

Pros:

- Improves cardiovascular health
- Increases endurance
- Burns more calories
- Helps reduce stress
- Can be a great way to cool down after lifting weights

Cons:

- Can lead to fatigue
- Can decrease performance
- Can increase the risk of injury if not done correctly
- May not be the best option if your main goal is to build muscle

How to Do Cardio after Weights

So, you have decided to do cardio after weights. Great! Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your workout.

1. Warm-up:

Before you start doing cardio, make sure you warm up your muscles. This will help prevent injury and prepare your body for the workout ahead.

2. Keep it low intensity:

When doing cardio after weights, it's best to keep the intensity low. This will help prevent fatigue and ensure that you can complete your workout without decreasing performance.

3. Don't overdo it:

It's easy to get carried away when doing cardio after weights. However, it's important to listen to your body and not overdo it. If you feel fatigued or dizzy, take a break.

4. Stay hydrated:

Make sure you drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout. This will help prevent dehydration and ensure that you can perform at your best.

FAQs about Doing Cardio after Weights

Q1. How long should I wait to do cardio after lifting weights?

A1. It's best to wait at least 30 minutes after lifting weights before doing cardio.

Q2. Should I do cardio before or after weights?

A2. If your main goal is to burn fat, it's best to do weights before cardio. However, if you want to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance, doing cardio after weights is an excellent option.

Q3. How often should I do cardio after weights?

A3. It's best to do cardio after weights two to three times a week.

Q4. What type of cardio is best after weights?

A4. Low-intensity cardio such as walking, jogging, or cycling is best after weights. This will help prevent fatigue and ensure that you can complete your workout without decreasing performance.

Conclusion of Doing Cardio after Weights

Doing cardio after weights is an excellent way to improve your cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn more calories. However, it's important to keep in mind that doing too much can be counterproductive. Make sure you listen to your body, stay hydrated, and don't overdo it. By following these tips, you can get the most out of your workout and achieve your fitness goals.
