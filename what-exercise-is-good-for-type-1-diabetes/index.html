What Exercise Is Good For Type 1 Diabetes

Written by Daniel Nov 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Diabetes is a condition that affects millions of people worldwide. Type 1 diabetes is an autoimmune disorder that requires careful management of blood glucose levels. Exercise is an essential part of diabetes management, but what exercise is good for type 1 diabetes can be a confusing topic. In this article, we will explore the benefits of exercise for type 1 diabetes and what types of exercise are recommended.

Managing type 1 diabetes can be challenging, and exercise can sometimes make it more difficult. People with type 1 diabetes need to be careful when exercising to avoid hypoglycemia (low blood sugar) or hyperglycemia (high blood sugar). Additionally, some people may experience other complications such as diabetic neuropathy or retinopathy, which can make exercise more challenging.

Regular exercise is essential for people with type 1 diabetes as it can help with blood glucose control, improve cardiovascular health, and reduce the risk of complications. However, the type and intensity of exercise need to be tailored to the individual's needs and medical history.

In summary, what exercise is good for type 1 diabetes depends on the individual's medical history, fitness level, and personal preferences. Some people may prefer low-impact activities such as swimming or cycling, while others may enjoy high-intensity workouts such as running or weightlifting.

Cardiovascular Exercise

Cardiovascular exercise is an excellent choice for people with type 1 diabetes as it can help improve blood glucose control, reduce the risk of cardiovascular disease, and improve overall fitness. Personally, I enjoy going for a brisk walk or jog, cycling, or using an elliptical machine.

Strength Training

Strength training is also beneficial for people with type 1 diabetes as it can help increase muscle mass, improve insulin sensitivity, and reduce the risk of osteoporosis. Personally, I enjoy using free weights or resistance bands, but it's important to start with light weights and proper form to avoid injury.

Yoga and Pilates

Yoga and Pilates are low-impact activities that can help improve flexibility, balance, and reduce stress levels. These types of exercise are great for people with type 1 diabetes who may experience diabetic neuropathy or other complications that make high-impact exercise challenging.

Tips for Exercising with Type 1 Diabetes

1. Check your blood glucose levels before and after exercise

2. Always carry a source of fast-acting carbohydrates with you, such as glucose tablets or juice

3. Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after exercise

4. Wear appropriate footwear and clothing to avoid injury and manage body temperature

Question and Answer

Q: Can exercise help me reduce my insulin dosage?

A: Yes, regular exercise can help improve insulin sensitivity, which may reduce the amount of insulin you need to take. However, it's essential to work with your healthcare provider to adjust your medication as needed.


Q: How often should I exercise?

A: The American Diabetes Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week, spread over at least three days per week, with no more than two consecutive days without exercise.


Q: Can I exercise if I have diabetic neuropathy?

A: Yes, low-impact activities such as swimming, cycling, and yoga can be beneficial for people with diabetic neuropathy. However, it's important to work with your healthcare provider to determine the best type and intensity of exercise for your individual needs.


Q: Is it safe to exercise if I have retinopathy?

A: It depends on the severity of your retinopathy. It's important to work with your healthcare provider to determine the best type and intensity of exercise for your individual needs.

Conclusion of What Exercise is Good for Type 1 Diabetes

Regular exercise is an essential part of diabetes management for people with type 1 diabetes. Cardiovascular exercise, strength training, yoga, and Pilates are all excellent options, but the type and intensity of exercise should be tailored to the individual's needs and medical history. By following the tips for exercising with type 1 diabetes and working with your healthcare provider, you can safely and effectively incorporate exercise into your diabetes management plan.
