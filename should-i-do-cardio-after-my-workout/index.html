Should I Do Cardio After My Workout

Written by Luffy Jun 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who hit the gym regularly and spend hours sweating out to achieve your desired body goals? Well, if you are, then you must have wondered at some point in your fitness journey if doing cardio after your workout is necessary. The answer to the question is not as straightforward as you may think, and it can often leave gym-goers in a state of confusion. In this article, we will explore the pros and cons of doing cardio after your workout and help you decide whether it is the best approach for you.

The post-workout period is crucial for muscle recovery and growth, and you don't want to hinder that process by overexerting your body. Doing cardio after your workout can be beneficial for burning extra calories and fat, but it can also lead to fatigue and delayed muscle recovery. It's essential to consider your fitness goals and body needs before deciding whether to do cardio after a workout.

If your primary goal is to lose weight, doing cardio after your workout can be an effective way to burn extra calories and fat. However, if you're focusing on building muscle mass, cardio after a workout can be counterproductive as it can lead to muscle fatigue and hinder muscle growth. It's essential to understand your body's requirements and find the right balance between cardio and strength training.

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio after your workout is subjective and depends on your fitness goals and body needs. If you're looking to lose weight, doing cardio after your workout is a good option. However, if you're trying to build muscle mass, it's best to avoid cardio after your workout as it can interfere with muscle recovery and growth.

My Personal Experience

As someone who has gone through various fitness journeys, I have tried both approaches of doing cardio before and after my workout. When my primary focus was to lose weight, doing cardio after my workout helped me achieve my fitness goals faster. However, when I switched my focus to building muscle mass, I realized that doing cardio after my workout was hindering my muscle growth, and it was best to avoid it.

Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio After Your Workout

Pros:

- Burning extra calories and fat

- Increased endurance and stamina

- Boosts cardiovascular health

Cons:

- Can lead to muscle fatigue and hinder muscle recovery

- Interferes with muscle growth

- Increases the risk of injury

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate cardio into your workout routine, it's essential to find the right balance between cardio and strength training. One way to do this is by doing cardio on days when you're not doing strength training. You can also try doing cardio before your workout to warm up your body and increase your heart rate. However, make sure to keep the intensity low to avoid fatiguing your muscles before your workout.

FAQs

Q1. Is it better to do cardio before or after a workout?

A1. It depends on your fitness goals. If your primary focus is to lose weight, doing cardio after your workout is a good option. However, if you're trying to build muscle mass, it's best to avoid cardio after your workout as it can interfere with muscle recovery and growth.

Q2. How much cardio should I do after a workout?

A2. The amount of cardio you should do after your workout depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're a beginner, start with 10-15 minutes of low-intensity cardio and gradually increase the duration and intensity as your fitness level improves.

Q3. Can doing cardio after a workout cause injury?

A3. Yes, doing cardio after a workout can increase the risk of injury if you overexert your muscles. Make sure to keep the intensity low and avoid high-impact exercises that can strain your joints and muscles.

Q4. Is it necessary to do cardio after a workout?

A4. No, it's not necessary to do cardio after your workout. It depends on your fitness goals and body needs. If you're looking to lose weight, doing cardio after your workout can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle mass, it's best to avoid cardio after your workout.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio After My Workout

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio after your workout depends on your fitness goals and body needs. If you're looking to lose weight, doing cardio after your workout can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle mass, it's best to avoid cardio after your workout. Remember to find the right balance between cardio and strength training and listen to your body to avoid injury and fatigue.
