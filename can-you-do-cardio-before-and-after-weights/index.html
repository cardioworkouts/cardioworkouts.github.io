Can You Do Cardio Before And After Weights

Written by Oliver Nov 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

If you're someone who enjoys working out, you've probably wondered whether it's better to do cardio before or after weights. While there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question, there are some things you should consider before deciding when to do your cardio workout.

The Pain Points of Can You Do Cardio Before and After Weights

One of the main concerns people have when it comes to doing cardio before or after weights is whether it will impact their performance in either activity. Another concern is whether one approach is more effective for weight loss or muscle gain.

Answering Can You Do Cardio Before and After Weights

The short answer is yes, you can do cardio before or after weights. The best approach depends on your specific fitness goals and preferences. If your goal is to build muscle, you should focus on strength training and do cardio after weights. If you're primarily interested in weight loss, you can do cardio before or after weights, but doing it before may help you burn more calories.

Ultimately, the best approach is the one that works best for you and helps you stick to your fitness routine. Experiment with different approaches and see what feels best.

Main Points of Can You Do Cardio Before and After Weights

In this article, we'll explore the pros and cons of doing cardio before and after weights, as well as some tips for incorporating both into your fitness routine. We'll also discuss how to balance cardio and weight training for optimal results.

Cardio Before Weights: My Personal Experience

When I first started working out, I would always do cardio before weights. I thought that doing cardio first would help me warm up and get my heart rate up before lifting. However, I quickly realized that this approach wasn't ideal for building muscle.

While doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and improve your endurance, it can also deplete your glycogen stores, which can impact your strength and muscle-building potential. I found that I was too tired after cardio to lift as heavy as I wanted to, which meant I wasn't seeing the gains I wanted.

Cardio After Weights: My Personal Experience

After realizing that doing cardio before weights wasn't working for me, I switched to doing cardio after weights. I found that this approach allowed me to lift heavier and focus more on building muscle, while still getting the cardio benefits I wanted.

While doing cardio after weights may not burn as many calories as doing it before, it can still be effective for weight loss. Plus, doing cardio after weights can help you burn more fat, as your body will use fat as fuel when your glycogen stores are depleted.

The Benefits of Cardio Before Weights

While doing cardio before weights may not be ideal for building muscle, it does have some benefits. For one, doing cardio first can help you burn more calories during your workout. It can also improve your endurance, which can help you lift heavier weights for longer periods of time.

Additionally, doing cardio before weights can help you warm up and get your heart rate up, which can make your weight training more effective. Just be sure to keep your cardio session relatively short and low-intensity, so you don't deplete your glycogen stores too much.

The Benefits of Cardio After Weights

While doing cardio after weights may not be as effective for burning calories during your workout, it can still be effective for weight loss. Doing cardio after weights can help you burn more fat, as your body will use fat as fuel when your glycogen stores are depleted.

Additionally, doing cardio after weights can help you recover faster from your weight training session. Cardio can help increase blood flow to your muscles, which can help reduce soreness and promote healing.

How to Balance Cardio and Weight Training

The key to balancing cardio and weight training is to find the right mix for your goals and fitness level. If you're primarily interested in building muscle, you should focus on strength training and do cardio after weights. If you're primarily interested in weight loss, you can do cardio before or after weights, but doing it before may help you burn more calories.

It's also important to vary your cardio and weight training workouts to prevent boredom and plateauing. Try different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming, and mix up your weight training routine with different exercises and rep ranges.

Conclusion of Can You Do Cardio Before and After Weights

Ultimately, the best approach to doing cardio before or after weights depends on your specific fitness goals and preferences. Experiment with different approaches and find what works best for you. Just be sure to vary your workouts and keep things interesting to prevent boredom and plateauing.
