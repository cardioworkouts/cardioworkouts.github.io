Should You Lift Weights Or Do Cardio First

Written by Eveline Jun 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether you should lift weights or do cardio first during your workout routine? It's a common dilemma that many fitness enthusiasts face, but it's important to understand the benefits and drawbacks of each option before making a decision.

Pain Points Related to Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First

One of the biggest pain points related to should you lift weights or do cardio first is the fear of not maximizing your workout. Many people are concerned that if they do cardio first, they won't have the energy or strength to lift weights effectively. On the other hand, if they lift weights first, they worry that they won't have enough energy left for cardio.

Additionally, some people want to lose weight and wonder which option is more effective for weight loss. Others want to build muscle and are unsure which approach will help them achieve their goals.

Answer to Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First

The answer to should you lift weights or do cardio first ultimately depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build strength and muscle, lifting weights first is the better option. This is because you need to have maximum energy and focus to perform weightlifting exercises effectively. If you do cardio beforehand, you'll be too tired to lift weights properly.

On the other hand, if your goal is to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting weights may be more beneficial. This is because cardio helps elevate your heart rate and burn calories, making it an effective way to kickstart your weight loss journey. Doing cardio first also helps to warm up your body and loosen up your muscles, making it easier to perform weightlifting exercises.

Summary of Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First?

Ultimately, whether you should lift weights or do cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle, lift weights first. If you want to lose weight, do cardio first. It's important to remember that there's no one-size-fits-all solution, so experiment with both options and see what works best for you.

Building Muscle with Lifting Weights First

When I started my fitness journey, my goal was to build muscle and get stronger. I found that lifting weights first was the best approach for me. By starting with weightlifting exercises, I was able to focus and use all my energy on building muscle. I also found that I had more energy and motivation to push myself harder during my weightlifting workouts.

Another benefit of lifting weights first is that it helps to burn more calories after your workout. This is because weightlifting exercises create an afterburn effect, which means your body continues to burn calories even after you finish your workout. This can help you achieve your weight loss goals more quickly.

Losing Weight with Cardio First

When I wanted to lose weight, I switched up my routine and started doing cardio first. I found that doing cardio before lifting weights helped me burn more calories and lose weight more quickly. I also noticed that I had more energy to perform weightlifting exercises after doing cardio.

Another benefit of doing cardio first is that it helps to improve your cardiovascular health. Cardiovascular exercises help to strengthen your heart and lungs, which can improve your overall health and fitness level.

More Details on Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First

If you're still unsure about whether you should lift weights or do cardio first, consider the following factors:

  • Your fitness goals
  • Your current fitness level
  • Your energy levels
  • Your workout routine
  • Your personal preferences

It's important to listen to your body and choose the option that feels best for you. If you find that doing cardio first leaves you too tired to lift weights, try switching up your routine and lifting weights first. Alternatively, if lifting weights first doesn't feel right, try doing cardio first and see if that helps you achieve your goals more effectively.

How to Incorporate Both Cardio and Weightlifting into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate both cardio and weightlifting into your workout routine, consider doing them on separate days or at different times of the day. This can help you maximize your energy and focus on each type of exercise separately.

Alternatively, you can try doing a combination of cardio and weightlifting during the same workout. For example, you could do a quick cardio warm-up, followed by weightlifting exercises, and then finish with a longer cardio session. This can help you achieve both your muscle-building and weight-loss goals simultaneously.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio or lift weights first for building muscle?
A: If your goal is to build muscle, lifting weights first is the better option. This is because you need maximum energy and focus to perform weightlifting exercises effectively.

Q: Can doing cardio before lifting weights help me lose weight?
A: Yes, doing cardio before lifting weights can help you burn more calories and lose weight more quickly. However, the best approach ultimately depends on your fitness goals.

Q: Should I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?
A: It's up to you. You can do them on separate days or at different times of the day, or you can do a combination of both during the same workout.

Q: Is there a specific order I should follow for cardio and weightlifting?
A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle, lift weights first. If you want to lose weight, do cardio first.

Conclusion of Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First

When it comes to should you lift weights or do cardio first, there's no one-size-fits-all solution. It ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. Experiment with both options and see what works best for you. Remember to listen to your body and do what feels right.
