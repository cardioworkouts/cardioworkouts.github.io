Is It Better To Do Cardio Or Weights First For Weight Loss

Written by Bardi Jul 28, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking to lose weight but unsure of where to start? Perhaps you’ve heard conflicting advice about whether to do cardio or weights first for weight loss. It can be overwhelming to navigate through the different opinions and information out there, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this article, we will explore the benefits and drawbacks of both approaches and help you make an informed decision about which one is best for you.

The Pain Points

One of the biggest pain points for those trying to lose weight is figuring out how to maximize their time in the gym. Many people are strapped for time and want to make the most of their workouts, but are unsure of how to structure them to achieve their weight loss goals. There is also a lot of misinformation out there about whether to do cardio or weights first, which can be confusing and overwhelming.

Answering the Target

So, is it better to do cardio or weights first for weight loss? The answer is: it depends on your goals. Both cardio and strength training can help you lose weight, but they do so in different ways. Cardio burns more calories during the workout, while strength training helps you build muscle and increase your metabolism, which helps you burn more calories throughout the day.

Summary of Main Points

When it comes to weight loss, both cardio and weights can be effective. The best approach depends on your individual goals and preferences. Cardio burns more calories during the workout, while strength training helps you build muscle and increase your metabolism. Ultimately, the key to weight loss is creating a calorie deficit, which can be achieved through a combination of diet and exercise.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio First

Personally, I find it beneficial to start my workouts with cardio. It helps me get my heart rate up and mentally prepares me for my strength training. It also ensures that I get my cardio in, as sometimes I can be tempted to skip it if I leave it until after my weights. Additionally, studies have shown that doing cardio before weights can lead to greater fat loss and improved endurance.

The Benefits of Doing Weights First

On the other hand, some people prefer to start with weights. This approach can help you build muscle and increase your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day. Additionally, if you prioritize strength training, it may be more beneficial to do it first when you have the most energy and focus.

Cardio vs. Weights: Which Burns More Calories?

When it comes to burning calories, cardio is the winner. Depending on the intensity and duration of your workout, you can burn anywhere from 200 to 500 calories during a cardio session. However, strength training can also burn calories and help you lose weight in the long run by increasing your metabolism.

How to Incorporate Both Into Your Workouts

If you want to incorporate both cardio and strength training into your workouts, there are a few ways to do so. One approach is to alternate between cardio and weights on different days. Another option is to do a combination of both in one session, either by doing cardio first and then weights, or by doing circuits that incorporate both.

Personal Experience with Both Approaches

Personally, I have tried both approaches and have found that doing cardio first works best for me. I like to get it out of the way and it helps me mentally prepare for my strength training. However, I know people who prefer to start with weights and have had great success with that approach as well. It's all about finding what works for you and sticking with it.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you lose weight with cardio alone?

A: Yes, you can lose weight with cardio alone. However, strength training can help you build muscle and increase your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

Q: How many times a week should I do cardio and weights?

A: It depends on your goals and fitness level, but aim for at least three days of cardio and two days of strength training per week.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weights?

A: It depends on your goals and preferences. Cardio before weights can lead to greater fat loss, while weights before cardio can help you build muscle and increase your metabolism.

Q: Do I need to lift heavy weights to lose weight?

A: No, you don't need to lift heavy weights to lose weight. You can still build muscle and lose weight with lighter weights and higher reps.

Conclusion of Is it Better to Do Cardio or Weights First for Weight Loss

Ultimately, the best approach to weight loss depends on your individual goals, preferences, and lifestyle. Both cardio and strength training can be effective for weight loss, but they do so in different ways. Experiment with different approaches and find what works best for you, and remember that creating a calorie deficit through a combination of diet and exercise is the key to successful weight loss.
