Is It Okay To Do Cardio After Strength Training

Written by Frank Aug 23, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who enjoys both strength training and cardio exercises? If so, you may be wondering whether it's okay to do cardio after strength training. This is a common question among fitness enthusiasts, and it can be challenging to find a clear answer. In this post, we'll explore the topic in depth and provide you with the information you need to make an informed decision.

Many people are concerned about whether doing cardio after strength training can hinder their results. They worry that cardio will deplete their energy levels and make it harder to lift weights, leading to a less effective workout. Additionally, some people believe that doing cardio after strength training can interfere with muscle growth and recovery.

The short answer is that it's generally safe to do cardio after strength training. In fact, some studies have shown that doing cardio after lifting weights can actually enhance recovery and improve overall fitness. However, the best approach may depend on your individual goals and fitness level.

If you're someone who's primarily focused on building muscle mass and strength, you may want to prioritize strength training over cardio. This is because cardio exercises can burn calories and reduce the energy reserves you need for lifting weights. On the other hand, if you're trying to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, incorporating cardio after strength training can be an effective way to achieve your goals.

My Personal Experience

As a personal trainer, I often recommend that my clients incorporate cardio after strength training. I've found that this approach can be effective for improving overall fitness and helping clients reach their goals. I've also seen many clients who have successfully built muscle mass and strength while doing cardio after lifting weights.

However, it's important to note that the best approach may depend on your individual fitness level and goals. If you're just starting out with strength training, it may be best to focus on building your strength before incorporating cardio. Similarly, if you have a lot of weight to lose, you may want to prioritize cardio over strength training.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Strength Training

As mentioned earlier, there are several benefits to doing cardio after strength training. One of the main advantages is that it can help improve overall fitness and cardiovascular health. Additionally, research has shown that doing cardio after lifting weights can enhance recovery and reduce muscle soreness. This can help you recover faster between workouts and improve your overall performance.

Another benefit of doing cardio after strength training is that it can help you burn more calories. When you do cardio after lifting weights, your body is already in a fat-burning state. This means that you're more likely to burn fat during your cardio workout, which can help you achieve your weight loss goals.

Tips for Incorporating Cardio After Strength Training

If you're interested in incorporating cardio after strength training, there are a few tips you should keep in mind. First, it's important to start with a light cardio workout and gradually increase the intensity over time. This will help you avoid overtraining and reduce the risk of injury.

Second, it's important to choose a type of cardio that complements your strength training routine. For example, if you're doing a lot of lower body exercises, you may want to choose a cardio exercise that focuses on your upper body, such as rowing or swimming.

How to Determine the Best Approach for You

If you're still unsure about whether it's okay to do cardio after strength training, the best approach is to experiment with different routines and see what works best for you. Try doing cardio after strength training for a few weeks and see how you feel. If you notice that you're too tired or sore to lift weights effectively, you may want to switch up your routine.

Ultimately, the most important thing is to listen to your body and prioritize your individual fitness goals. With the right approach, you can achieve your fitness goals while still enjoying your workouts.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: There is no right or wrong answer to this question. Some people prefer to do cardio before lifting weights to warm up their muscles, while others prefer to do it afterward to burn more calories. Ultimately, the best approach will depend on your individual goals and fitness level.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after strength training?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after lifting weights before doing cardio. This will give your body time to recover and replenish its energy reserves.

Q: Can doing cardio after strength training help me lose weight?

A: Yes, doing cardio after strength training can be an effective way to burn calories and lose weight. This is because your body is already in a fat-burning state after lifting weights, which can help you burn more fat during your cardio workout.

Q: How often should I do cardio after strength training?

A: This will depend on your individual fitness goals and schedule. Some people may choose to do cardio after every strength training workout, while others may only do it once or twice a week. It's important to find a routine that works for you and your lifestyle.

Conclusion of Is It Okay to Do Cardio After Strength Training?

In conclusion, it's generally safe to do cardio after strength training. In fact, it can be an effective way to improve overall fitness, enhance recovery, and achieve your fitness goals. However, the best approach will depend on your individual goals and fitness level. By experimenting with different routines and listening to your body, you can find a workout routine that works best for you.
