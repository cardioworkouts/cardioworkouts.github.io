How To Get The Most Out Of Good Cardio Interval Workouts

Written by Eveline Jul 04, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of endless hours on the treadmill or stationary bike with little to no results? Good cardio interval workouts might be the answer you're looking for. Not only do they save time, but they also provide an effective way to burn fat and increase cardiovascular endurance.

Cardio interval training is a type of workout that alternates between periods of high-intensity exercise and recovery periods. This type of training can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, swimming, and more. The goal is to push your body to its limits during the high-intensity periods, followed by a period of rest or low-intensity exercise.

Some good cardio interval workouts include high-intensity interval training (HIIT), Tabata, and fartlek training. HIIT involves short bursts of all-out effort followed by periods of recovery. Tabata is similar, but the intervals are shorter and more intense. Fartlek training is less structured and involves alternating between periods of high and low intensity at random intervals.

The benefits of good cardio interval workouts are numerous. They can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time, improve your cardiovascular health, and increase your endurance. They can also help you break through weight loss plateaus and improve your overall fitness level.

High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

One of the most popular good cardio interval workouts is HIIT. This type of workout involves short, intense bursts of exercise followed by periods of rest. For example, you could sprint for 30 seconds, followed by 30 seconds of rest or low-intensity exercise. Repeat this cycle for 10-20 minutes, depending on your fitness level.

Personally, I've seen great results from incorporating HIIT into my workout routine. It's challenging, but the results are worth it. I've noticed an increase in my endurance and have been able to push myself harder during my other workouts.

Tabata

Tabata is a type of HIIT that involves 20 seconds of all-out effort followed by 10 seconds of rest, repeated for a total of 4 minutes. This may sound easy, but the short intervals make it incredibly challenging. You can do Tabata with a variety of exercises, including push-ups, squats, and burpees.

I've tried Tabata a few times, and it definitely lives up to its reputation as one of the toughest good cardio interval workouts. It's a great way to challenge yourself and push your limits.

Fartlek Training

Fartlek training is a less structured form of interval training that involves alternating between periods of high and low intensity at random intervals. For example, you could run at a moderate pace for a few minutes, then sprint for 30 seconds, followed by a recovery period. Repeat this cycle for 20-30 minutes.

I've used fartlek training as a way to mix up my cardio routine and prevent boredom. It's a fun way to challenge yourself and keep your body guessing.

Tips for Good Cardio Interval Workouts

1. Start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your intervals.

2. Incorporate a variety of exercises to prevent boredom and challenge your body.

3. Keep track of your progress and adjust your intervals as needed.

4. Don't forget to warm up and cool down before and after your workout.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do good cardio interval workouts?

A: It's recommended to do cardio interval training 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between.

Q: Can I do cardio interval training if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, but it's important to start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your intervals. It's also a good idea to consult with a trainer or healthcare professional before starting a new workout routine.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do good cardio interval workouts?

A: No, you can do cardio interval training with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, and bodyweight exercises.

Q: Can cardio interval training help me lose weight?

A: Yes, cardio interval training can help you burn more calories and lose weight. It's important to combine cardio interval training with a healthy diet for best results.

Conclusion of Good Cardio Interval Workouts

Good cardio interval workouts are an effective way to burn fat, increase cardiovascular endurance, and improve your overall fitness level. HIIT, Tabata, and fartlek training are all great options to incorporate into your workout routine. Remember to start slow, gradually increase the intensity and duration of your intervals, and consult with a trainer or healthcare professional before starting a new workout routine.
