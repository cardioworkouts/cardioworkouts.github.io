How Long Does It Take To Get Good Cardio

Written by William Sep 12, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of feeling out of shape and looking for a way to get fit? Cardiovascular exercise is a great way to improve your health and fitness, but how long does it take to get good cardio? In this article, we will explore the answer to this question and provide tips on how to improve your cardiovascular fitness.

Many people struggle with sticking to a consistent exercise routine, and one of the reasons for this is the lack of immediate results. When it comes to cardio, it can be discouraging to feel like you're not making progress. This can lead to giving up before seeing any real improvements.

How Long Does It Take to Get Good Cardio?

The answer to this question is not straightforward, as it depends on various factors such as your starting fitness level, exercise routine, and diet. However, it generally takes around six to eight weeks of consistent cardiovascular exercise to see noticeable improvements in your fitness level.

During this time, you may experience some muscle soreness and fatigue, but this is a sign that your body is adapting to the new exercise routine. It's essential to listen to your body and rest when needed to avoid injury.

To improve your cardiovascular fitness, you should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. This can include activities such as running, cycling, swimming, or brisk walking.

My Personal Experience

When I started my fitness journey, I was out of shape and struggled to run for more than a few minutes. However, I stuck to a consistent exercise routine of running three times a week for six weeks. By the end of the six weeks, I could run for 30 minutes straight without stopping. It was a small victory, but it felt like a significant accomplishment.

Consistency is key when it comes to improving your cardiovascular fitness. It's important to find an exercise routine that you enjoy and can stick to long-term. This will help you stay motivated and see long-term results.

Tips for Improving Cardiovascular Fitness

Here are some tips to help you improve your cardiovascular fitness:

1. Mix Up Your Routine

Doing the same exercise routine every day can become boring and lead to a lack of motivation. Mix things up by trying different activities such as swimming, cycling, or hiking. This will keep your workouts exciting and challenging.

2. Increase Intensity

Once you feel comfortable with your exercise routine, gradually increase the intensity to challenge your body. This can include increasing the speed or incline on a treadmill or adding resistance training to your routine.

Tracking Your Progress

Tracking your progress is an essential part of improving your cardiovascular fitness. It can be motivating to see how far you've come and help you set new goals. You can track your progress by keeping a workout journal, using a fitness app, or investing in a fitness tracker.

Conclusion of How Long Does It Take to Get Good Cardio

Improving your cardiovascular fitness takes time and consistency. It's essential to find an exercise routine that you enjoy and can stick to long-term. By following the tips outlined in this article and staying committed to your fitness goals, you can improve your cardiovascular fitness and overall health.

Question and Answer

Q. How often should I do cardio to see improvements?

A. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week to see improvements in your cardiovascular fitness.

Q. Is it better to do cardio before or after weight lifting?

A. It's generally better to do cardio after weight lifting, as weight lifting can deplete your energy levels. However, it ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preference.

Q. How long should a cardio session be?

A. Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per session, but you can gradually increase this as your fitness level improves.

Q. Can I do cardio every day?

A. Yes, you can do cardio every day, but it's important to listen to your body and rest when needed to avoid injury. It's recommended to have at least one rest day per week.
