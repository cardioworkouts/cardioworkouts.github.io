How Often Should We Do Cardio Exercise

Written by William Oct 01, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Cardio exercise is an essential part of any fitness routine, but many people struggle with the question of how often they should do it. Without a clear answer, it can be challenging to create a workout plan that meets your fitness goals while still being safe and effective. In this post, we'll explore the science behind cardio exercise and provide some guidance on how often you should do it.

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to cardio exercise is the lack of clarity around how often to do it. Many people wonder if they should do cardio every day, or if a few times a week is enough. The truth is that there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. The amount of cardio you should do depends on a variety of factors, including your fitness level, goals, and overall health.

So, how often should you do cardio exercise? The American Heart Association recommends that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity per week. This equates to about 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise five days a week or 25 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise three days a week. However, this is just a general guideline, and you may need to adjust your cardio routine based on your individual needs.

To summarize, the general recommendation for how often you should do cardio exercise is at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity per week. However, this can vary depending on your fitness level, goals, and overall health.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Exercise

Before we dive deeper into how often you should do cardio exercise, let's take a moment to explore some of the benefits of this type of workout. Cardio exercise, also known as aerobic exercise, is any activity that gets your heart rate up and increases your breathing rate. Some of the benefits of cardio exercise include:

Improved cardiovascular health: Cardio exercise strengthens your heart and lungs, reducing your risk of heart disease and other cardiovascular conditions.

Weight loss: Cardio exercise can help you burn calories and lose weight, especially when combined with a healthy diet.

Reduced stress: Cardio exercise releases endorphins, which can improve your mood and reduce stress levels.

Better sleep: Regular cardio exercise can help you fall asleep faster and sleep more deeply.

Personal Experience with How Often to Do Cardio Exercise

As a personal trainer, I've worked with clients who have varying cardio needs. One client, for example, was a seasoned runner who wanted to improve her race times. For her, I recommended doing cardio exercise six days a week, with a mix of moderate and high-intensity workouts. Another client, who was just starting on her fitness journey, needed a more gradual approach. For her, I recommended doing cardio exercise three days a week, with a focus on low-impact activities like walking and swimming.

It's important to remember that everyone's needs are different when it comes to cardio exercise. Some people may need to do more or less than the recommended guidelines based on their goals and fitness level. It's always a good idea to consult with a fitness professional to create a cardio routine that is safe and effective for you.

How Often Should We Do Cardio Exercise for Weight Loss?

If weight loss is your primary goal, you may be wondering if you need to do more cardio than the recommended guidelines. While cardio exercise can certainly help you lose weight, it's important to remember that weight loss is not just about cardio. A balanced diet and strength training are also crucial components of a healthy weight loss plan.

That being said, if weight loss is your primary goal, you may want to consider doing more cardio than the recommended guidelines. Aim for at least 300 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, or 150 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise. This may sound like a lot, but you can break it up into shorter workouts throughout the day or week.

Question and Answer

Q: Can you do cardio exercise every day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio exercise every day, but it's important to vary the intensity and duration of your workouts to avoid overtraining and injury.

Q: How long should a cardio workout be?

A: A cardio workout should be at least 20-30 minutes long to be effective, but you can break it up into shorter sessions throughout the day if needed.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It's generally recommended to do cardio after strength training, as this allows you to focus on building strength and muscle first.

Q: How do I know if I'm doing enough cardio?

A: Pay attention to how you feel during and after your workouts. If you feel energized and refreshed, you're probably doing enough cardio. If you feel fatigued or sore, you may need to adjust your routine.

Conclusion of How Often Should We Do Cardio Exercise

Cardio exercise is an important part of any fitness routine, but how often you should do it depends on a variety of factors, including your fitness level, goals, and overall health. The general recommendation is at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic activity or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic activity per week, but you may need to adjust your routine based on your individual needs. Remember to listen to your body and consult with a fitness professional if you have any questions or concerns about your cardio routine.
