What Is The Difference Between Aerobic And Strength Training

Written by Oliver Jun 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to get fit but confused about the different types of exercises? Do you wonder which one to choose between aerobic and strength training? This blog post will help you understand the difference between these two types of exercises and which one is best suited for you.

Aerobic and strength training are the two most popular types of exercises, but they are very different from each other. Aerobic exercise is any activity that increases your heart rate and respiration, while strength training is any exercise that involves muscle contraction against resistance.

In simple terms, aerobic exercise is a cardio workout that focuses on your heart and lungs, while strength training is a muscle workout that focuses on your body's strength and size.

To summarize, the main difference between aerobic and strength training is that aerobic exercise focuses on cardiovascular fitness, while strength training focuses on building muscle strength and size.

Aerobic Training

When I started my fitness journey, I was overweight and struggled to walk up a flight of stairs without getting out of breath. I started with aerobic exercises, such as running, cycling, and swimming. These exercises helped me increase my endurance, lose weight, and improve my overall fitness level.

Aerobic exercises rely on your body's ability to use oxygen to produce energy. This type of exercise increases your heart rate, breathing rate, and blood circulation, improving oxygen delivery to your muscles. Aerobic exercises can be performed at low, moderate, or high intensity, depending on your fitness level.

Strength Training

After I lost weight and improved my fitness level, I decided to start strength training. I wanted to build muscle and increase my body's strength. Strength training involves exercises that use resistance to stimulate muscle growth and improve strength.

Strength training can be done using free weights, weight machines, resistance bands, or your own body weight. This type of exercise can help increase bone density, improve posture and balance, and reduce the risk of injury.

Which One is Better?

Both aerobic and strength training have their own benefits, and the best one for you depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular fitness, lose weight, or reduce the risk of chronic disease, aerobic exercise is the way to go. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle, improve your body composition, or increase your strength, strength training is the way to go.

Tips for Getting Started

If you are new to exercise, start with low-impact aerobic exercises, such as walking or cycling. Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts as your fitness level improves.

For strength training, start with light weights or resistance bands and focus on proper form. Gradually increase the weight or resistance as your muscles get stronger.

Conclusion

Both aerobic and strength training are important for overall fitness and health. The key is to choose the one that best fits your fitness goals and preferences. Whether you prefer running, weightlifting, or a combination of both, make sure to include exercise as part of your daily routine for a healthier and happier life.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do both aerobic and strength training in one workout?

A: Yes, you can combine both types of exercises in one workout. This is called a circuit training workout, where you alternate between aerobic and strength exercises with short rest periods in between.

Q: How often should I do aerobic and strength training?

A: It is recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity aerobic exercise per week, and strength train all major muscle groups at least twice a week.

Q: Will strength training make me bulky?

A: No, strength training will not make you bulky unless that is your goal. Women, in particular, do not have enough testosterone to build large muscles, so they are more likely to tone and strengthen their muscles instead.

Q: Can aerobic exercise improve my mental health?

A: Yes, aerobic exercise has been shown to improve mental health by reducing stress, anxiety, and depression and improving mood and cognitive function.

Conclusion of What is the Difference Between Aerobic and Strength Training

In conclusion, aerobic and strength training are two different types of exercises that offer unique benefits for overall fitness and health. Whether you choose to do aerobic or strength training or a combination of both, consistency and proper form are key to achieving your fitness goals.
