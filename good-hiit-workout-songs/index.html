The Ultimate Guide To Good Hiit Workout Songs

Written by Aprilia Jul 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of working out to the same old playlist? Do you find yourself losing motivation halfway through your HIIT workout? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people struggle with finding the right songs to keep them energized and motivated during their HIIT workouts. In this guide, we'll explore the best HIIT workout songs and how they can help you achieve your fitness goals.

High-Intensity Interval Training, or HIIT for short, is a popular workout technique that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. HIIT workouts are designed to maximize calorie burn and improve cardiovascular fitness in a short amount of time. However, the intensity of these workouts can be challenging, and having the right music can make a significant difference in your performance.

The primary target of good HIIT workout songs is to keep you motivated and energized throughout your workout. These songs should have a fast tempo, inspiring lyrics, and a strong beat that matches the rhythm of your workout. Some of the best HIIT workout songs include "Can't Hold Us" by Macklemore & Ryan Lewis, "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott, and "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor.

In summary, good HIIT workout songs are essential to keep you motivated and energized during your workout. These songs should have a fast tempo, inspiring lyrics, and a strong beat that matches the rhythm of your workout.

The Benefits of Good HIIT Workout Songs

Good HIIT workout songs offer several benefits that can help you achieve your fitness goals. Firstly, music has been shown to improve performance and endurance during exercise. When you listen to music, it can distract you from the discomfort of exercise and help you maintain a consistent pace. Secondly, music can also elevate your mood and reduce stress levels, making your workout more enjoyable. Finally, good HIIT workout songs can provide a sense of community and motivation, especially when you're working out with others.

Personally, I've found that good HIIT workout songs have helped me push through challenging workouts and achieve my fitness goals. One of my favorite HIIT workout songs is "Stronger" by Kanye West. The beat of the song matches the rhythm of my workout perfectly, and the lyrics inspire me to keep going even when I feel like giving up.

The Best HIIT Workout Songs

Now that we've established the benefits of good HIIT workout songs let's dive into some of the best songs to add to your playlist:

1. "Can't Hold Us" by Macklemore & Ryan Lewis

This song has a fast tempo and an infectious beat that will keep you moving throughout your workout. The lyrics are also inspiring and motivational, making it a great addition to your HIIT workout playlist.

2. "Lose Control" by Missy Elliott

This classic hip-hop song has a high-energy beat that will get your heart pumping. The lyrics are also empowering and will help you push through even the toughest workouts.

3. "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

This iconic song from the Rocky movies is a timeless classic that will never go out of style. The powerful lyrics and driving beat will keep you motivated and energized throughout your entire workout.

Personally, I love to mix up my HIIT workout playlist with a variety of genres and artists. Some other great HIIT workout songs include "Jump" by Van Halen, "All I Do is Win" by DJ Khaled, and "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars.

FAQs About Good HIIT Workout Songs

Q: How do I choose the right HIIT workout songs?

A: The best way to choose the right HIIT workout songs is to find songs that have a fast tempo, inspiring lyrics, and a strong beat that matches the rhythm of your workout.

Q: Can listening to music improve my performance during HIIT workouts?

A: Yes, listening to music can improve your performance and endurance during exercise by distracting you from the discomfort of exercise and helping you maintain a consistent pace.

Q: How many songs should I add to my HIIT workout playlist?

A: It's recommended that you have at least 30-60 minutes of music on your HIIT workout playlist. This will give you enough variety to keep you motivated and energized throughout your entire workout.

Q: Can I use the same HIIT workout playlist every time I workout?

A: While using the same HIIT workout playlist every time you workout may be convenient, it's recommended that you mix up your playlist to keep things fresh and exciting. This will help prevent boredom and keep you motivated to achieve your fitness goals.

Conclusion of Good HIIT Workout Songs

In conclusion, good HIIT workout songs are an essential part of any successful workout routine. They can improve your performance, elevate your mood, and provide a sense of community and motivation. By choosing the right songs for your playlist, you'll be able to push through even the toughest workouts and achieve your fitness goals.
