How Many Minutes Of Cardio After Strength Training

Written by Bardi Sep 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to maximize the benefits of your workout routine? Strength training is a great way to build muscle and increase overall fitness, but many people wonder how much cardio they should do after lifting weights. If you're one of those people, you're in the right place.

The answer to how many minutes of cardio after strength training varies depending on your fitness goals and overall health. However, a general rule of thumb is to do at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after your strength training session.

Incorporating cardio after strength training can help improve cardiovascular health, burn fat, and increase endurance. It also helps to prevent injury and reduce muscle soreness by aiding in the removal of lactic acid from the muscles.

To summarize, doing cardio after strength training can provide numerous benefits to your overall fitness routine. Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after your strength training session to maximize these benefits.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training: Personal Experience

As someone who has been doing strength training for a few years now, I have found that incorporating cardio after my workouts has helped me see better results. I typically do 30 minutes of cardio on the elliptical after my strength training sessions.

The first few times I did cardio after strength training, I was extremely sore the next day. However, as I continued to do it regularly, I found that the soreness subsided and I felt more energized throughout the day.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training: The Benefits

Aside from the benefits mentioned earlier, doing cardio after strength training can also help improve mental health by reducing stress and anxiety levels. It can also increase metabolism and aid in weight loss.

Another benefit of doing cardio after strength training is that it helps to improve overall fitness levels. By combining strength training and cardio, you can work on both muscular and cardiovascular endurance, which can help improve performance in other areas of life.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training: The Science Behind It

According to a study published in the Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research, doing cardio after strength training can increase the amount of fat burned during exercise. This is because strength training depletes glycogen stores in the muscles, which forces the body to use fat as an energy source during cardio.

Additionally, doing cardio after strength training can also help to improve insulin sensitivity, which can aid in weight loss and reduce the risk of developing type 2 diabetes.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training: How to Incorporate It Into Your Routine

If you're new to incorporating cardio into your strength training routine, start small and gradually increase the amount of time you spend doing cardio. You can also try different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, or swimming, to keep things interesting.

It's also important to make sure you're fueling your body properly before and after your workouts. This means eating a balanced diet that includes carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats.

How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training: Final Thoughts

Overall, doing cardio after strength training can provide numerous benefits to your overall fitness routine. Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after your strength training session to maximize these benefits. Of course, it's important to listen to your body and adjust accordingly based on your individual needs and goals.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio before strength training?

A: It's generally recommended to do strength training before cardio, as cardio can deplete energy stores needed for lifting weights.

Q: How often should I do cardio after strength training?

A: This depends on your fitness goals and overall health. Aim for at least 2-3 times per week, but adjust as needed based on your individual needs.

Q: What type of cardio is best after strength training?

A: This varies depending on personal preference and fitness level. Some good options include running, cycling, swimming, or using the elliptical.

Q: What should I eat after doing cardio and strength training?

A: It's important to refuel your body after exercising with a balanced meal that includes carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. Good options include a chicken and vegetable stir-fry with brown rice or a spinach and feta omelette with whole-grain toast.

Conclusion of How Many Minutes of Cardio After Strength Training

Doing cardio after strength training can provide numerous benefits to your overall fitness routine. Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after your strength training session to maximize these benefits. Remember to listen to your body and adjust accordingly based on your individual needs and goals.
