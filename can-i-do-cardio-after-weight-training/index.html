Can I Do Cardio After Weight Training The Ultimate Guide

Written by William Nov 08, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym but is confused about whether you can do cardio after weight training? Do you want to know what the best way to maximize your workout results is? If yes, then you're in the right place! In this guide, we'll answer all your questions about whether you can do cardio after weight training and what the best practices are for doing so.

Many people wonder if doing cardio after weight training is a good idea or not. Some worry that doing so can lead to muscle loss or hinder muscle growth. Others are not sure if it's the best way to maximize their workout results. The good news is that doing cardio after weight training is not only safe but can also offer several benefits.

If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn calories, doing cardio after weight training is an excellent option. It can also help you recover faster and reduce muscle soreness. However, it's essential to do it correctly and in the right order.

The key to doing cardio after weight training is to ensure that you don't compromise your weight training performance. Your weight training session should always come first. After you finish your weight training session, you can then move on to your cardio workout. Doing it in this order ensures that you have enough energy to perform your weight training exercises correctly.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Weight Training

Doing cardio after weight training can offer several benefits, including:

Improved cardiovascular health: Doing cardio after weight training can help improve your heart health by increasing blood flow, reducing blood pressure, and lowering cholesterol levels.

Increased endurance: Cardiovascular exercises can help improve your endurance, making it easier to perform weight training exercises for more extended periods.

Burns calories: Doing cardio after weight training can help you burn more calories and reduce body fat.

Faster recovery: Doing cardio after weight training can help reduce muscle soreness and speed up recovery time.

My Personal Experience with Doing Cardio After Weight Training

As a fitness enthusiast, I have been doing cardio after weight training for several years. I find that it helps me stay energized and focused throughout my workouts. I also noticed that doing cardio after weight training helped me improve my endurance and burn more calories. However, I always make sure to do it in the right order and not compromise my weight training performance.

The Best Cardio Exercises to Do After Weight Training

The best cardio exercises to do after weight training are those that are low-impact, such as jogging, elliptical, cycling, or swimming. These exercises help improve your cardiovascular health without putting too much stress on your joints or muscles.

The Best Time to Do Cardio After Weight Training

The best time to do cardio after weight training is immediately after your weight training session. Doing so ensures that you have enough energy to perform your weight training exercises correctly. It also helps you maximize your workout results by burning more calories and improving your cardiovascular health.

How Long Should Your Cardio Workout Be?

Your cardio workout should be around 20-30 minutes long. Doing so ensures that you get enough cardiovascular exercise without compromising your weight training performance. If you're new to cardio, you can start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase the time as you build endurance.

The Bottom Line

Doing cardio after weight training is an excellent way to improve your cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn calories. However, it's crucial to do it in the right order and ensure that you don't compromise your weight training performance. By following the tips in this guide, you can safely and effectively incorporate cardio into your workout routine.

Doing cardio after weight training is an excellent way to improve your cardiovascular health, increase endurance, and burn calories. By following the tips in this guide, you can safely and effectively incorporate cardio into your workout routine.
