Cardio Or Strength Training First

Written by Eveline Oct 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you confused about whether to start with cardio or strength training first in your workout routine? You are not alone. Many people struggle with this decision, which is why we have created this guide to help you make the best choice for your fitness goals.

Understanding the Target of Cardio or Strength Training First

The first thing you need to understand is the target of cardio or strength training. Cardio, also known as aerobic exercise, is aimed at increasing your heart rate and breathing rate to improve your cardiovascular health. Strength training, on the other hand, is aimed at building muscle mass, strength, and endurance.

Both types of exercises offer unique benefits, and the one you choose to do first will depend on your fitness goals.

Personal Experience with Cardio or Strength Training First

Personally, I prefer to start my workout with strength training first. This is because I want to build muscle mass and strength, which is my primary fitness goal. By starting with strength training, I am able to focus all my energy on lifting weights without being too tired from cardio.

However, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, you may want to start with cardio first. This will allow you to get your heart rate up and improve your endurance before moving on to strength training.

Tips for Cardio or Strength Training First

If you are still unsure about whether to start with cardio or strength training first, here are some tips to help you make the best decision:

1. Consider Your Fitness Goals

Think about what you want to achieve from your workout routine. If your primary goal is to build muscle mass and strength, start with strength training. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, start with cardio.

2. Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to how your body feels during your workout. If you feel too tired to do strength training after cardio, switch the order and start with strength training first. You don't want to risk injury by pushing yourself too hard.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Is it okay to do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, it is okay to do cardio and strength training on the same day. However, make sure to give yourself enough time to recover between exercises. It is recommended to take at least 24 hours of rest between strength training workouts.

Q: How long should I do cardio or strength training for?

A: It is recommended to do at least 30 minutes of cardio and 30 minutes of strength training per day. However, the duration may vary depending on your fitness goals and level of fitness.

Q: What are some examples of cardio exercises?

A: Some examples of cardio exercises include running, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope.

Q: What are some examples of strength training exercises?

A: Some examples of strength training exercises include weight lifting, push-ups, squats, and lunges.

Conclusion of Cardio or Strength Training First

In conclusion, whether to start with cardio or strength training first depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to build muscle mass and strength, start with strength training. If your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular health, start with cardio. However, make sure to listen to your body and adjust your routine accordingly. By following these tips, you can create a workout routine that best suits your needs and helps you achieve your fitness goals.
