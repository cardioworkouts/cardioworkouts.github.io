Is It Bad To Lift Weights After Cardio

Written by Daniel Oct 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who's always confused about whether you should lift weights before or after cardio? Are you worried if lifting weights after cardio will have any adverse effects on your body? If yes, then this article is for you!

Many fitness enthusiasts have this question in mind. They are unsure if lifting weights after cardio is a good idea or not. Most of them are concerned whether they will be able to lift weights with the same intensity after doing cardio. Others are worried about the potential injuries that may arise due to fatigue.

The answer to whether it is bad to lift weights after cardio is no. In fact, it is a good idea to lift weights after cardio. Doing cardio before lifting weights helps in warming up your body and prepares it for the workout ahead. It also helps in increasing your heart rate, which in turn improves blood flow to your muscles. This means your muscles will receive more oxygen and nutrients, which will help you in lifting weights with more intensity.

In summary, it is not bad to lift weights after cardio. In fact, it is a good idea to do so. Doing cardio before lifting weights helps in warming up your body and prepares it for the workout ahead. It also helps in increasing your heart rate, which in turn improves blood flow to your muscles.

Benefits of Lifting Weights After Cardio

Personally, I have found lifting weights after cardio to be very beneficial. It helps me in maintaining my heart rate, which means that I can lift weights with more intensity. Also, I find that I have more energy when I lift weights after cardio, as opposed to when I lift weights before cardio.

Another benefit of lifting weights after cardio is that it helps in burning more calories. When you do cardio before lifting weights, your body burns glycogen for energy. Once the glycogen is depleted, your body starts burning fat for energy. This means that if you lift weights after cardio, your body will burn more fat for energy, which will help you in losing weight.

Myths About Lifting Weights After Cardio

There are many myths about lifting weights after cardio. One of the most common myths is that it will make you tired and decrease your intensity. However, this is not true. Lifting weights after cardio will not make you tired, but it will actually increase your energy levels.

Another myth is that lifting weights after cardio will lead to muscle loss. This is also not true. In fact, lifting weights after cardio can help you in building muscle. When you lift weights after cardio, your muscles are already warmed up, which means that you can lift weights with more intensity. This results in more muscle growth.

Factors to Consider When Lifting Weights After Cardio

While lifting weights after cardio is not bad, there are a few factors to consider. Firstly, make sure that you do not overdo your cardio. Doing too much cardio can lead to fatigue, which can make you prone to injuries. Secondly, make sure that you take enough rest between your cardio and weightlifting sessions. This will help in preventing injuries and also give your body time to recover. Finally, make sure that you warm up properly before lifting weights. This will help in preventing injuries and also prepare your muscles for the workout ahead.

How to Warm Up Before Lifting Weights After Cardio

The best way to warm up before lifting weights after cardio is to do a few dynamic stretches. Dynamic stretches are stretches that involve movement. These stretches help in increasing your heart rate and also improve blood flow to your muscles. Some examples of dynamic stretches that you can do are leg swings, arm swings, and lunges.

Conclusion of Is it Bad to Lift Weights After Cardio

So, in conclusion, lifting weights after cardio is not bad. In fact, it is a good idea to do so. Doing cardio before lifting weights helps in warming up your body and prepares it for the workout ahead. It also helps in increasing your heart rate, which in turn improves blood flow to your muscles. However, make sure that you do not overdo your cardio, take enough rest between your cardio and weightlifting sessions, and warm up properly before lifting weights.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I lift weights after cardio every day?

A: It is not recommended to lift weights after cardio every day. Give your body enough time to rest and recover.

Q: How long should I wait after cardio to lift weights?

A: It is recommended to wait for at least 10-20 minutes after cardio to lift weights. This will give your body enough time to recover and also prevent injuries.

Q: Should I lift heavy or light weights after cardio?

A: This depends on your fitness level and goals. If you're a beginner, start with lighter weights and gradually increase the weight. If you're an experienced lifter, you can lift heavier weights.

Q: Can lifting weights after cardio help in losing weight?

A: Yes, lifting weights after cardio can help in losing weight. When you lift weights after cardio, your body burns more fat for energy, which helps in losing weight.
