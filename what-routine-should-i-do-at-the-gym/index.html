What Routine Should I Do At The Gym A Beginner s Guide

Written by Frank Jun 14, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you new to the gym and feeling overwhelmed with all the equipment and workout routines? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many beginners struggle with figuring out what workout routine is best for them. In this guide, we'll go over some tips and tricks to help you find the perfect routine for your fitness goals.

Why Finding the Right Routine is Important

Before we dive into the specifics of what routine you should do at the gym, let's talk about why finding the right routine is important. A well-planned workout routine can help you achieve your fitness goals faster, reduce the risk of injury, and keep you motivated to continue working out.

On the other hand, if you don't have a plan and just go to the gym without a set routine, you may find yourself wandering aimlessly and not making much progress. You may also be more prone to injury if you don't know how to use the equipment properly or push yourself too hard.

The Best Routine for Beginners

If you're a beginner, it's best to start with a full-body workout routine. This means working out all the major muscle groups in your body, including your legs, arms, chest, back, and core. This type of workout is great for building overall strength and improving your fitness level.

Here's an example of a full-body workout routine for beginners:

  • Squats
  • Lunges
  • Bench press
  • Bent-over rows
  • Shoulder press
  • Bicep curls
  • Tricep dips
  • Planks

Try doing each exercise for 3 sets of 10-12 reps. Rest for 1-2 minutes between each set.

The Importance of Cardio

While strength training is important, it's also essential to incorporate cardio into your workout routine. Cardio helps improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and increase endurance.

Some great cardio exercises to try include running, cycling, swimming, and rowing. Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, 5 days a week.

How to Create Your Own Routine

If you're not a beginner and want to create your own workout routine, here are some things to keep in mind:

  • Identify your fitness goals - Do you want to build muscle, lose weight, or increase endurance?
  • Choose exercises that target your goals - For example, if you want to build muscle, focus on strength training exercises like squats and deadlifts.
  • Include a variety of exercises - Don't just focus on one muscle group or type of exercise. Mix it up to keep things interesting and challenge your body.
  • Consider your schedule - Make sure your routine fits into your schedule and is sustainable for the long-term.

Tips for Sticking to Your Routine

Creating a workout routine is one thing, but sticking to it is another. Here are some tips to help you stay motivated and on track:

  • Find a workout buddy to keep you accountable.
  • Set realistic goals and track your progress.
  • Mix up your routine to prevent boredom.
  • Make sure you're getting enough rest and recovery time.

Q&A

Q: How often should I work out?

A: It's recommended to work out at least 3-4 times a week, but listen to your body and don't overdo it. It's important to have rest days as well.

Q: Should I do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It's best to do cardio after strength training, as strength training can deplete your energy levels.

Q: Can I do the same routine every day?

A: It's not recommended to do the same routine every day, as your body needs time to rest and recover. Mix up your routine and focus on different muscle groups on different days.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Aim for 1-2 minutes of rest between sets to allow your muscles to recover.

Conclusion

When it comes to figuring out what routine to do at the gym, it's important to identify your fitness goals, choose exercises that target those goals, and create a routine that's sustainable for the long-term. Remember to mix it up, stay motivated, and listen to your body. With the right routine, you'll be on your way to achieving your fitness goals in no time.
