How Much Cardio Should You Do After Strength Training

Written by Bardi Aug 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you feel confused about how much cardio you should do after strength training? Are you wondering if you should do cardio before or after your weight lifting sessions? You are not alone! Many people are confused about the right amount of cardio after strength training. Let's dive deeper into this topic and help you find the right answer.

The amount of cardio you should do after strength training depends on your fitness goals, the intensity of your weight lifting session, and your overall fitness level. While some people prefer to do cardio before weight lifting, others find it more beneficial to do it after. The key is to find the right balance that works best for your body and your fitness goals.

As a general rule of thumb, it is recommended to do at least 20 minutes of cardio after strength training. This helps to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance. However, if your primary goal is to build muscle mass and strength, you may want to limit your cardio to shorter durations or on separate days. Doing too much cardio after strength training can interfere with muscle recovery and growth.

In summary, the amount of cardio you should do after strength training depends on your fitness goals and the intensity of your weight lifting session. It is recommended to do at least 20 minutes of cardio, but you may need to adjust this based on your individual needs.

The Benefits of Doing Cardio After Strength Training

Personally, I have found that doing cardio after strength training helps me to burn more calories and improve my overall fitness level. I usually do 20-30 minutes of cardio on the treadmill or stationary bike after my weight lifting session. This helps me to cool down and stretch my muscles, while also boosting my cardiovascular endurance.

Research has shown that doing cardio after strength training can also help to improve your body's ability to burn fat. This is because weight lifting depletes your body's glycogen stores, which means that your body will burn more fat for energy during cardio.

How Much Cardio Should You Do After Strength Training for Weight Loss?

If your primary fitness goal is to lose weight, you may need to do more cardio after strength training. Aim for at least 30-40 minutes of moderate to high-intensity cardio after your weight lifting session. This will help you to burn more calories and boost your metabolism.

Types of Cardio You Can Do After Strength Training

There are many types of cardio you can do after strength training, such as running, cycling, rowing, or using an elliptical machine. Choose a form of cardio that you enjoy and that challenges your body. You can also mix up your cardio routine to prevent boredom and plateaus.

Tips for Doing Cardio After Strength Training

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your cardio after strength training:

  • Start with a 5-minute warm-up to get your heart rate up gradually
  • Choose a form of cardio that you enjoy and that challenges your body
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio over time
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with healthy foods
  • Stretch your muscles after your cardio session to prevent injury

Question and Answer

Q: Can doing too much cardio after strength training interfere with muscle growth?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio after strength training can interfere with muscle recovery and growth. It is recommended to limit your cardio to shorter durations or on separate days if your primary goal is to build muscle mass and strength.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight lifting?

A: This depends on your individual needs and fitness goals. Some people find it more beneficial to do cardio before weight lifting to warm up their muscles and increase their heart rate. Others prefer to do cardio after weight lifting to cool down and stretch their muscles.

Q: How long should I wait after strength training to do cardio?

A: It is recommended to wait at least 20-30 minutes after strength training to do cardio. This allows your body to cool down and recover before engaging in cardio exercises.

Q: How much cardio should I do after strength training for general fitness?

A: For general fitness, aim for at least 20-30 minutes of moderate to high-intensity cardio after your weight lifting session. This will help to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance.

Conclusion of How Much Cardio Should You Do After Strength Training

Doing cardio after strength training can help to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance, as well as help you to burn more calories and lose weight. However, the amount of cardio you should do after strength training depends on your individual needs and fitness goals. Aim for at least 20-30 minutes of cardio after strength training, and gradually increase the intensity and duration over time. Remember to listen to your body and adjust your cardio routine as needed.
