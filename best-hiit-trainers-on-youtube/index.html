The Best Hiit Trainers On Youtube A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Jul 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of boring workouts that don't challenge you? Do you want to push your fitness to the next level but don't know where to start? If you're looking for an intense, effective workout that will get your heart pumping and your muscles burning, HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) is the way to go. And luckily, you don't even need to leave your house to get a great HIIT workout. YouTube is home to some of the best HIIT trainers out there, and in this guide, we'll show you who they are and what they have to offer.

If you're new to HIIT, you might be wondering what all the fuss is about. Simply put, HIIT is a type of workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. The idea is to push your body to its limits, then give it a chance to recover before going all out again. This type of training has been shown to be incredibly effective at burning fat, building muscle, and improving cardiovascular health.

So, who are the best HIIT trainers on YouTube? There are countless options out there, but we've narrowed it down to a few of our favorites:

1. Fitness Blender

Fitness Blender is run by a husband-and-wife team, Daniel and Kelli, who have been creating HIIT workouts on YouTube for over a decade. They offer a huge variety of workouts, from beginner-friendly routines to more advanced challenges. Their videos are easy to follow and include modifications for different fitness levels.

2. The Body Coach TV

Joe Wicks, aka The Body Coach, is a well-known fitness guru in the UK. His YouTube channel is packed with high-energy, high-intensity workouts that are sure to get your heart rate up. He also offers nutrition advice and healthy recipe ideas to help you fuel your workouts.

3. POPSUGAR Fitness

POPSUGAR Fitness is a popular YouTube channel that offers a wide variety of workout videos, including plenty of HIIT options. Their trainers are engaging and motivating, and their workouts are always challenging but doable.

These are just a few of the many great HIIT trainers on YouTube. No matter which one you choose, you're sure to get a killer workout that will leave you feeling strong and accomplished.

Why I Love Fitness Blender

Personally, my favorite HIIT trainer on YouTube is Fitness Blender. I love their workouts because they're challenging but not impossible, and they always leave me feeling like I've accomplished something. Plus, I appreciate that they offer modifications for different fitness levels, so I can adjust the workouts to fit my needs.

Why I Love The Body Coach TV

Another great option is The Body Coach TV. I love Joe Wicks' energy and enthusiasm, and his workouts always leave me feeling like I've pushed myself to my limits. Plus, his nutrition advice is spot-on and has helped me make healthier choices overall.

The Benefits of HIIT Training

If you're still on the fence about trying HIIT, here are a few of the benefits you can expect:

-Increased fat burning

-Improved cardiovascular health

-Increased muscle growth and strength

-Boosted metabolism

-Shorter workouts (since HIIT is so intense, you don't need to spend as much time exercising to get results)

How to Get Started with HIIT

If you're new to HIIT, it's important to start slowly and build up your endurance over time. Try incorporating a few HIIT workouts into your routine each week, and gradually increase the intensity and duration as you get stronger. Remember to listen to your body and take breaks when you need them.

Conclusion of Best HIIT Trainers on YouTube

Whether you're a seasoned fitness enthusiast or a newbie looking to get in shape, HIIT is a great option for anyone who wants to challenge themselves and see results. And with so many great HIIT trainers on YouTube, there's no excuse not to give it a try. So grab your water bottle, put on your workout clothes, and get ready to sweat!

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do HIIT workouts?

A: It's generally recommended to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between to allow your body to recover.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do HIIT?

A: While some HIIT workouts may require equipment like dumbbells or resistance bands, many can be done with just your bodyweight. It's always a good idea to check the video description before starting a workout to see what equipment, if any, is needed.

Q: Can HIIT be dangerous?

A: As with any type of exercise, there is a risk of injury if you don't use proper form or push yourself too hard. It's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time. If you have any health concerns or injuries, it's always a good idea to check with your doctor before starting a new workout regimen.

Q: How long should a typical HIIT workout be?

A: HIIT workouts can vary in length depending on the trainer and the specific workout, but most are around 20-30 minutes long.
