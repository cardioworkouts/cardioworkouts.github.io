The Best Background Music For Workout Videos A Tutorial

Written by Daniel Jun 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of creating workout videos with background music that doesn't quite fit the vibe? Do you struggle with finding the right beats to keep your viewers motivated and engaged? Well, look no further! In this tutorial, we will explore the best background music for workout videos and how to use it to increase engagement and results.

When it comes to creating workout videos, the background music is just as important as the moves themselves. The right music can help set the tone and energy for the workout, while the wrong music can distract and disengage viewers. It's important to choose music that fits the workout style and keeps viewers motivated throughout.

The target of this tutorial is to provide guidance on the best background music for workout videos. We will explore the different types of music that work well for various workout styles, as well as tips for finding the perfect track. By the end of this tutorial, you will have the tools and knowledge necessary to create engaging and effective workout videos.

In summary, the best background music for workout videos is crucial for keeping viewers engaged and motivated. It's important to choose music that fits the workout style and energy, and to find tracks that keep viewers engaged throughout the video.

The Importance of BPM in Workout Music

One important factor to consider when choosing background music for workout videos is the BPM, or beats per minute. The BPM of a song can directly impact the intensity of a workout and the energy level of the viewers. For example, a high BPM can be great for cardio workouts, while a lower BPM may be better for yoga or stretching videos.

Personally, I have found that matching the BPM of the music to the workout intensity can greatly improve engagement and results. In my experience, using music with a BPM that is too high or too low for the workout can cause viewers to lose focus or become disengaged.

The Role of Genre in Workout Music

Another important factor to consider when choosing background music for workout videos is the genre of the music. Different genres can evoke different emotions and energies, which can impact the overall effectiveness of the workout video. For example, pop or hip-hop music may be great for high-energy workouts, while classical or instrumental music may be better for yoga or meditation videos.

Personally, I have found that using a variety of genres can keep viewers engaged and interested throughout the video. Switching up the genre and style of the music can also help to break up the monotony of the workout and keep viewers motivated.

Using Royalty-Free Music for Workout Videos

When creating workout videos, it's important to use royalty-free music to avoid copyright infringement. There are many websites that offer royalty-free music specifically for use in workout videos, such as AudioJungle, Epidemic Sound, and Artlist. These websites offer a wide variety of music styles and genres, as well as different licensing options to fit your needs.

Personally, I have found that using royalty-free music has made the process of creating workout videos much simpler and stress-free. It allows me to focus on the workout and the content of the video, rather than worrying about copyright issues or legalities.

How to Find the Perfect Track for Your Workout Video

When searching for the perfect background music for your workout video, there are a few key things to keep in mind. First, consider the workout style and intensity, and choose music that matches that energy level. Second, think about the genre and style of the music, and choose something that fits the overall vibe of the video. Finally, search for royalty-free music to avoid any legal issues or copyright infringements.

One helpful tip is to listen to a variety of tracks and create a playlist of potential options. Play the tracks alongside the workout video and see which ones fit the best. Don't be afraid to experiment and try out different styles and genres to find the perfect fit.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use any music I want for my workout video?
A: No, it's important to use royalty-free music to avoid copyright infringement. There are many websites that offer royalty-free music specifically for use in workout videos.

Q: What is the importance of BPM in workout music?
A: The BPM of a song can directly impact the intensity of a workout and the energy level of the viewers. It's important to choose music with a BPM that matches the workout intensity.

Q: What are some good genres for workout music?
A: Different genres can work well for different workout styles. Pop or hip-hop music may be great for high-energy workouts, while classical or instrumental music may be better for yoga or meditation videos.

Q: How can I find the perfect track for my workout video?
A: Consider the workout style and intensity, the genre and style of the music, and search for royalty-free options. Create a playlist of potential options and experiment to find the perfect fit.

Conclusion of Best Background Music for Workout Videos

In conclusion, the best background music for workout videos is crucial for keeping viewers engaged and motivated. When choosing music, consider the BPM, genre, and licensing options. Experiment with different tracks and styles to find the perfect fit for your workout video. By following these tips and tricks, you can create engaging and effective workout videos that keep viewers coming back for more.
