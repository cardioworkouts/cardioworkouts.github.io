Can You Gain Weight Doing Cardio The Truth Revealed

Written by Oliver Jun 14, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who is trying to gain weight but is also incorporating cardio into your workout routine? You might be wondering, can you gain weight doing cardio or will it hinder your progress? This is a common concern among fitness enthusiasts, and in this article, we will explore the truth behind this question.

Pain Points of Can You Gain Weight Doing Cardio

Many people who are trying to gain weight struggle with finding the right balance between cardio and strength training. Cardio is known for burning calories and fat, which is the opposite of what someone trying to gain weight wants. This can lead to confusion and frustration, making it difficult to achieve their desired results.

Answering the Target of Can You Gain Weight Doing Cardio

The simple answer to this question is yes, you can gain weight doing cardio. However, it's important to understand that the type of cardio and the way you approach it can make all the difference. While some forms of cardio might burn more calories than others, it's still possible to build muscle and gain weight through a balanced workout routine that includes cardio and strength training.

Summarizing the Main Points

In summary, it's possible to gain weight while doing cardio as long as you approach it in the right way. Balancing cardio with strength training and focusing on the type of cardio you do can help you achieve your weight gain goals. Let's dive deeper into this topic with some personal experiences and helpful tips.

Can You Gain Weight Doing Cardio? My Personal Experience

As someone who has struggled with finding the right balance between cardio and strength training, I can understand the confusion surrounding this topic. When I first started my fitness journey, I was solely focused on cardio and noticed that I was losing weight instead of gaining it. This made me realize that I needed to incorporate strength training into my routine and focus on the right type of cardio.

After some trial and error, I discovered that high-intensity interval training (HIIT) and weightlifting were the best types of cardio for my weight gain goals. HIIT allowed me to burn calories and build endurance while weightlifting helped me build muscle and increase my overall body weight. It's all about finding the right balance and approach that works for you.

The Best Cardio Exercises for Weight Gain

Not all cardio exercises are created equal when it comes to weight gain. While some might burn more calories, others can help you build muscle and increase your overall body weight. Here are some of the best cardio exercises for weight gain:

1. High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT)

HIIT is a type of cardio that involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest. This type of cardio is great for burning calories and building endurance, making it a great option for weight gain. It's important to note that HIIT should be done in moderation and not every day to avoid overtraining.

2. Weightlifting

While weightlifting might not be traditionally considered cardio, it can still provide a cardiovascular workout while also building muscle. Incorporating compound exercises like squats, deadlifts, and bench presses can help you build muscle and increase your overall body weight.

3. Cycling

Cycling is a low-impact form of cardio that can help you build endurance and burn calories. It's also a great option for those who might struggle with joint pain or injuries.

The Importance of Nutrition for Weight Gain

While cardio and strength training are important for weight gain, nutrition plays a crucial role in achieving your goals. Consuming a diet rich in protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats can help you build muscle and increase your overall body weight. It's important to consume enough calories to fuel your workouts and support your weight gain goals.

Frequently Asked Questions

1. Can you gain weight by doing cardio every day?

Doing cardio every day can lead to overtraining and burnout, making it difficult to achieve your weight gain goals. It's important to balance cardio with strength training and allow for rest days to avoid overtraining.

2. What is the best type of cardio for weight gain?

High-intensity interval training (HIIT), weightlifting, and cycling are all great options for weight gain. It's important to find the right balance and approach that works for you.

3. Should you do cardio before or after weightlifting?

It's recommended to do weightlifting before cardio to ensure that you have enough energy to complete your strength training workout. Doing cardio after weightlifting can also help with recovery and cooldown.

4. How many days a week should you do cardio for weight gain?

The number of days a week you should do cardio for weight gain depends on your fitness goals and current fitness level. It's recommended to start with 2-3 days a week and gradually increase as you build endurance and strength.

Conclusion of Can You Gain Weight Doing Cardio

So, can you gain weight doing cardio? The answer is yes, as long as you approach it in the right way. Balancing cardio with strength training and focusing on the right type of cardio can help you achieve your weight gain goals. Remember to also pay attention to your nutrition and consume enough calories to fuel your workouts and support your weight gain goals. With the right approach and mindset, you can achieve your desired results and reach your fitness goals.
