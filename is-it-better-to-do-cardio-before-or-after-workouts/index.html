Is It Better To Do Cardio Before Or After Workouts

Written by William Nov 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who have been working out for a while now but still can't figure out whether to do cardio before or after your workout session? Well, you're not alone. Many fitness enthusiasts struggle with this decision, and it can be quite confusing. You want to make the most out of your workout, but you also want to make sure that you're not overexerting your body. So, what's the best way to go about it?

The question of whether to do cardio before or after workouts is a common pain point for many fitness enthusiasts. People often wonder which approach will yield the best results and help them achieve their fitness goals faster. The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. It all depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences.

If your target is to lose weight or burn more calories, it's better to do cardio before your workout session. This is because cardio exercises increase your heart rate, warm up your body, and prepare you for the more intense workout to come. Additionally, doing cardio first ensures that you don't run out of energy during your workout. On the other hand, if your goal is to build muscle or improve your strength, it's better to do your weight training first and then do cardio after.

In summary, the decision to do cardio before or after workouts depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight or burn more calories, do cardio before your workout session. If you want to build muscle or improve your strength, do your weight training first and then do cardio after.

My Personal Experience

When I started my fitness journey, I was quite confused about whether to do cardio before or after my workout session. However, after some research and experimentation, I found that doing cardio before my workout session worked best for me. I felt more energized and ready to tackle my workout after a good cardio warm-up, and I also noticed that I burned more calories during my workout.

The Science of Cardio Before or After Workouts

The science behind the debate on whether to do cardio before or after workouts is quite simple. When you do cardio, your body burns calories and increases your heart rate, which prepares your body for the workout to come. On the other hand, when you do weight training, your body uses up energy, and your muscles get fatigued. Therefore, doing weight training first and then cardio after may lead to a more effective workout and better muscle gains.

Factors to Consider

While the science behind cardio before or after workouts is quite straightforward, there are some factors you need to consider before making a decision. These include:

  • Your fitness goals
  • Your personal preferences
  • Your fitness level
  • Your workout routine

Tips for Doing Cardio Before or After Workouts

Whether you decide to do cardio before or after your workout session, there are some tips you can follow to optimize your workout. These include:

  • Start slow and gradually increase your intensity
  • Don't overdo it and listen to your body
  • Hydrate properly before, during, and after your workout
  • Eat a balanced diet with sufficient protein and carbohydrates

FAQs

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: If your goal is to build muscle or improve your strength, it's better to do your weight training first and then do cardio after.

Q: Is it safe to do cardio and weight training in the same session?

A: Yes, it's safe to do cardio and weight training in the same session. However, it's essential to listen to your body and not overdo it.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after weight training?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after your weight training session before doing cardio.

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio every day, but it's essential to listen to your body and not overdo it. It's also essential to mix up your cardio routine to avoid boredom and prevent injuries.

Conclusion of Is It Better to Do Cardio Before or After Workouts

In conclusion, whether to do cardio before or after your workout session depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. If your goal is to lose weight or burn more calories, do cardio before your workout session. If your goal is to build muscle or improve your strength, do your weight training first and then do cardio after. Remember to start slow, gradually increase your intensity, and listen to your body to avoid injuries and optimize your workout.
