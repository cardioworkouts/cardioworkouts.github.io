Best Background Music For Fitness Video

Written by Eveline Sep 10, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a fitness trainer or do you enjoy making fitness videos? If so, you'll know that selecting the right background music can make or break your video. The perfect background music can help to motivate your audience and make them feel energized, while the wrong music can turn them off and make them lose interest. In this post, we'll explore the best background music for fitness video.

If you've ever watched a fitness video without background music, you'll know how boring it can be. The right music can help to set the tone for your workout and make it more enjoyable for your audience. However, choosing the wrong music can have the opposite effect and turn your audience off. You want to select music that is upbeat and motivational, but not distracting.

The target of best background music for fitness video is to create a motivational and uplifting atmosphere that will keep your audience engaged throughout the workout. The ideal music should be upbeat, with a tempo of around 120-140 beats per minute. This tempo has been shown to be the most effective for workouts, as it helps to synchronize your movements and keeps you motivated.

In summary, the best background music for fitness video should be upbeat, with a tempo of around 120-140 beats per minute. The music should be motivational and uplifting, but not distracting. It should help to set the tone for your workout and keep your audience engaged throughout.

Benefits of Using the Right Background Music for Fitness Video

As a fitness trainer or video creator, using the right background music can have a number of benefits. First and foremost, it can help to motivate your audience and keep them engaged throughout the workout. It can also help to set the tone for your video and make it more enjoyable to watch.

Personally, I've found that using the right background music can help to make my workouts more enjoyable and effective. When I'm listening to music that I love, I'm able to push myself harder and stay motivated for longer. It's also been shown that music can help to distract you from the discomfort of exercise, making it easier to push through the pain.

Choosing the Right Background Music for Your Fitness Video

When it comes to choosing the right background music for your fitness video, there are a few things to keep in mind. First and foremost, you want to select music that you love and that motivates you. This will help to keep you energized and engaged throughout the workout.

You also want to choose music that is appropriate for your audience. If you're creating a video for a high-intensity workout, you'll want to select music that is fast-paced and energetic. If you're creating a video for a more relaxed workout, you may want to choose music that is more soothing and calming.

Best Genres for Background Music for Fitness Video

While the best genre for background music for fitness video can vary depending on your personal preferences and the type of workout you're creating, some of the most popular genres include:

  • Electronic Dance Music (EDM)
  • Hip Hop
  • Rock
  • Pop
  • Instrumental

Each of these genres has its own unique benefits, so it's important to choose the one that works best for you and your audience.

Tips for Using Background Music in Your Fitness Video

When using background music in your fitness video, there are a few tips to keep in mind. First and foremost, make sure that the music is not too loud or distracting. You want it to be loud enough to motivate your audience, but not so loud that it drowns out your instructions.

You also want to make sure that the music is synced with your workout. If you're doing a high-intensity workout, you'll want to select music that is fast-paced and energetic. If you're doing a more relaxed workout, you may want to choose music that is slower and more soothing.

Personal Experience with Background Music for Fitness Video

As a fitness enthusiast, I've created a number of workout videos over the years. One thing that I've found to be incredibly important is selecting the right background music. When I'm able to find music that motivates me and keeps me energized, I'm able to push myself harder and get more out of my workouts.

One of my favorite genres of music to use for workouts is EDM. I find that the fast-paced beats and energetic melodies help to keep me motivated and engaged throughout the workout. However, I also enjoy using instrumental music for more relaxed workouts, as it helps to create a calming atmosphere.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I use copyrighted music in my fitness videos?

A: It's best to avoid using copyrighted music in your fitness videos, as this can lead to legal issues. Instead, you can use royalty-free music or create your own music to use in your videos.

Q: How can I find royalty-free music for my fitness videos?

A: There are a number of websites that offer royalty-free music, such as AudioJungle, Epidemic Sound, and PremiumBeat. You can also create your own music using software like GarageBand or Logic Pro.

Q: Should I use the same music for every workout video?

A: It's up to you whether you want to use the same music for every workout video or switch it up. However, using the same music can help to create a consistent brand and make it easier for your audience to recognize your videos.

Q: Can I use music with explicit lyrics in my fitness videos?

A: It's best to avoid using music with explicit lyrics in your fitness videos, as this can be offensive to some viewers. Instead, choose music that is appropriate for all ages and backgrounds.

Conclusion of Best Background Music for Fitness Video

Choosing the right background music for your fitness video can have a huge impact on the effectiveness of your workout. The ideal music should be upbeat, motivational, and uplifting, with a tempo of around 120-140 beats per minute. It should help to set the tone for your workout and keep your audience engaged throughout. By following the tips outlined in this post, you can create fitness videos that are both effective and enjoyable to watch.
