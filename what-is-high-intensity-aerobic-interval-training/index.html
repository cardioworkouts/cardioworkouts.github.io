What Is High Intensity Aerobic Interval Training

Written by Petter Jul 23, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to improve your cardiovascular fitness and burn more calories in a shorter amount of time? High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training (HIIT) might be the answer you've been searching for.

Pain Points of High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training

Many people find it challenging to fit a regular exercise routine into their busy schedule. Others struggle with boredom and lack of motivation when doing the same workout day after day. Some individuals may also have physical limitations that make traditional forms of exercise difficult or uncomfortable.

What is High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training?

High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training (HIIT) is a type of workout that alternates short periods of intense exercise with periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. These intervals can range from 10 seconds to several minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals.

The intense exercise periods should be challenging enough to push your heart rate up to at least 80% of its maximum capacity. This level of exertion may feel uncomfortable or even painful, but it's necessary to reap the benefits of HIIT.

HIIT can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, swimming, and bodyweight exercises. The key is to choose activities that you enjoy and that challenge you at your current fitness level.

Benefits of High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training

One of the main benefits of HIIT is that it can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time than traditional steady-state cardio workouts. This is because HIIT continues to burn calories even after your workout is over, thanks to a phenomenon known as excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC).

Other benefits of HIIT include improved cardiovascular fitness, increased endurance, and better insulin sensitivity. HIIT has also been shown to be effective for reducing body fat, especially in the abdominal area.

How to Incorporate HIIT into Your Routine

If you're new to HIIT, it's essential to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your intervals as you become more fit. A good starting point might be to do 30 seconds of high-intensity exercise followed by 60 seconds of low-intensity exercise, repeated for a total of 10-15 minutes.

As you become more comfortable with HIIT, you can increase the duration of your intervals or decrease the rest periods to make your workout more challenging. Just be sure to listen to your body and take breaks as needed to avoid injury or overexertion.

Tips for Success with HIIT

Here are a few tips to help you get the most out of your HIIT workout:

  • Choose exercises that you enjoy and that challenge you at your current fitness level.
  • Warm up before starting your HIIT workout to reduce the risk of injury.
  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your intervals.
  • Listen to your body and take breaks as needed to avoid overexertion.
  • Cool down and stretch after your HIIT workout to prevent muscle soreness and improve flexibility.

Personal Experience with HIIT

When I first started doing HIIT workouts, I was amazed at how challenging and effective they were. I had been doing steady-state cardio for years, but I had never pushed myself as hard as I did during a HIIT session.

At first, the high-intensity intervals were tough to get through, and I often found myself gasping for air and feeling like I couldn't go on. But as I continued to do HIIT workouts, I noticed that I was able to push myself harder and recover more quickly between intervals.

Now, I look forward to my HIIT workouts as a way to challenge myself and improve my fitness. I love the variety of exercises and the way that HIIT continues to burn calories long after I'm done working out.

Question and Answer

Q: What are some good exercises for HIIT?

A: Running, cycling, swimming, and bodyweight exercises like burpees and jump squats are all great options for HIIT.

Q: How often should I do HIIT?

A: It's best to start with 1-2 HIIT workouts per week and gradually increase to 3-4 as your fitness level improves.

Q: Can anyone do HIIT?

A: While HIIT can be modified to suit different fitness levels and physical limitations, it may not be suitable for everyone. It's always a good idea to consult with your doctor before starting a new exercise routine.

Q: How long should a HIIT workout be?

A: HIIT workouts can range from 10-30 minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of What is High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training

High-Intensity Aerobic Interval Training is a challenging and effective way to improve your cardiovascular fitness, burn calories, and reduce body fat. By incorporating HIIT into your exercise routine, you can enjoy a variety of exercises, push yourself to your limits, and see real results in a shorter amount of time.
