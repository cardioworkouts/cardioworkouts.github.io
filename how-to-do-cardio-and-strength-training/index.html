How To Do Cardio And Strength Training A Beginner s Guide

Written by Bardi Aug 09, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking to improve your fitness routine but not sure where to start? Incorporating both cardio and strength training into your workouts can have a huge impact on your overall health and well-being. In this post, we'll cover the basics of how to do cardio and strength training, so you can start seeing results and feeling great.

Why Cardio and Strength Training Are Important

Cardio and strength training are both essential components of a well-rounded fitness routine. Cardio, also known as aerobic exercise, helps improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, and reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes and heart disease. Strength training, on the other hand, focuses on building muscle mass and bone density, improving overall strength and posture, and boosting metabolism.

By combining both types of exercise, you can achieve a wide range of benefits, including:

  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased muscle strength and endurance
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases
  • Improved metabolism and weight management
  • Boosted energy levels and mood

How to Do Cardio and Strength Training

Now that you understand the importance of both cardio and strength training, let's dive into how to incorporate them into your fitness routine.

Cardio

Cardio can be any type of exercise that gets your heart rate up and makes you breathe harder. Some popular forms of cardio include:

  • Running
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Dancing
  • Rowing
  • Jumping rope

When starting out with cardio, it's important to choose an activity you enjoy and that fits your fitness level. Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio most days of the week, or 150 minutes per week. As you progress, you can increase the intensity or duration of your workouts.

Strength Training

Strength training typically involves using weights or resistance to build muscle and improve overall strength. Some common strength training exercises include:

  • Squats
  • Push-ups
  • Lunges
  • Bicep curls
  • Deadlifts
  • Planks

When starting out with strength training, it's important to start with lighter weights and focus on proper form to avoid injury. Aim for at least two strength training sessions per week, targeting all major muscle groups. As you progress, you can gradually increase the weight and intensity of your workouts.

Sample Cardio and Strength Training Workout

Here's an example of a beginner-friendly cardio and strength training workout:

  1. Warm-up: 5-10 minutes of light cardio (such as jogging or jumping jacks)
  2. Strength training: 3 sets of 10-12 reps of squats, push-ups, lunges, and bicep curls, with 30-60 seconds of rest between sets
  3. Cardio: 20-30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio (such as cycling or swimming)
  4. Cool-down: 5-10 minutes of stretching

Common Questions About Cardio and Strength Training

Q: How often should I do cardio and strength training?

A: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, and at least two strength training sessions per week.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, it's possible to do both types of exercise on the same day. Just be sure to give yourself enough time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Do I need expensive equipment to do strength training?

A: No, you can do many strength training exercises using just your body weight or simple equipment like dumbbells or resistance bands.

Q: How can I stay motivated to stick with my cardio and strength training routine?

A: Find a workout buddy, set realistic goals, and mix up your routine to keep things interesting.

Conclusion

Incorporating both cardio and strength training into your fitness routine can have a huge impact on your overall health and well-being. By following the tips outlined in this post, you can start seeing results and feeling great in no time.
