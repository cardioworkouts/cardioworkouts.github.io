Is Cardio Bad Before Lifting Weights Let s Find Out

Written by Frank Jun 04, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who hits the treadmill or bike before lifting weights? Or do you prefer to lift weights first and then do cardio? If you're confused about whether cardio is bad before lifting weights, then you're not alone. Many fitness enthusiasts have different opinions on this topic. In this blog post, we'll explore the pros and cons of doing cardio before lifting weights and give you a clearer understanding of this topic.

When it comes to working out, everyone has different preferences and goals. Some people prioritize burning calories and losing weight, while others aim to build muscle mass and strength. Depending on your fitness goals, doing cardio before lifting weights can have its advantages and disadvantages.

So, is cardio bad before lifting weights? The answer is no, not necessarily. If your main goal is to lose fat and get leaner, doing cardio before lifting weights can help you achieve that. Cardio exercises like running, cycling, or swimming can increase your heart rate, boost your metabolism, and burn calories. Doing cardio first can also help you warm up and prepare your body for the more intense weightlifting session that follows.

However, if your main goal is to build muscle and strength, doing cardio before lifting weights might not be the best idea. Cardio exercises can tire out your muscles and deplete your energy levels, making it harder to lift heavy weights and achieve your maximum potential. Plus, if you're doing high-intensity cardio like sprints or HIIT, it can increase your risk of injury and hinder your weightlifting performance.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Before Lifting Weights

As a personal trainer, I've experimented with different workout routines and tried doing cardio before lifting weights. In my experience, doing cardio before lifting weights can be beneficial, but it depends on your fitness goals and the type of cardio you're doing.

If your goal is to lose weight and get leaner, doing low-intensity cardio like walking or light cycling before lifting weights can help you burn calories and warm up your body. However, if you're trying to build muscle mass and strength, doing cardio before lifting weights can be counterproductive. I've noticed that my clients who do cardio after lifting weights tend to have better results in terms of muscle growth and strength gains.

The Pros and Cons of Doing Cardio Before Lifting Weights

Here's a summary of the pros and cons of doing cardio before lifting weights:

Pros:

  • Increases heart rate and metabolism
  • Burns calories and aids in weight loss
  • Helps warm up the body before weightlifting

Cons:

  • Tires out muscles and depletes energy levels
  • Can hinder weightlifting performance
  • Increases risk of injury, especially with high-intensity cardio

Should You Do Cardio Before or After Lifting Weights?

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals and preferences. If you're trying to lose weight and get leaner, doing cardio before lifting weights can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle and strength, doing cardio after lifting weights might be a better option.

Ultimately, the best workout routine is the one that you enjoy and that helps you achieve your fitness goals. If you prefer doing cardio before lifting weights, make sure to do it at a low to moderate intensity and give your body enough time to recover before weightlifting.

How to Warm Up Before Lifting Weights

If you decide to do cardio before lifting weights, it's important to warm up your muscles properly to prevent injury and improve performance. Here are some tips for warming up before weightlifting:

  • Start with a low-intensity cardio exercise like walking or light cycling for 5-10 minutes
  • Do some dynamic stretching exercises that target the muscles you'll be working on during your weightlifting session
  • Start with light weights and gradually increase the intensity as you warm up

My Final Thoughts on Cardio Before Lifting Weights

As with most fitness-related topics, there's no one-size-fits-all answer to whether cardio is bad before lifting weights. It all depends on your fitness goals and preferences. If you're trying to lose weight and get leaner, doing cardio before lifting weights can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle and strength, doing cardio after lifting weights might be a better option.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Will doing cardio before lifting weights make me lose muscle?

A: If you do high-intensity cardio before lifting weights, it can deplete your energy levels and make it harder to lift heavy weights, which can hinder your muscle growth. However, if you do low to moderate-intensity cardio, it shouldn't affect your muscle gains.

Q: How long should I wait after cardio before lifting weights?

A: It's recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after doing cardio before lifting weights to allow your body to recover and refuel. You can also do some light stretching or mobility exercises during this time to prepare your muscles for weightlifting.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weightlifting on the same day. However, it's important to give your body enough time to recover between the two workouts. You can do them in the same session, but make sure to do cardio before lifting weights and warm up properly to prevent injury.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to lose weight and get leaner, doing cardio before lifting weights can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle and strength, doing weights before cardio might be a better option.

Conclusion of Is Cardio Bad Before Lifting Weights

Doing cardio before lifting weights can have its advantages and disadvantages, depending on your fitness goals and preferences. If you're trying to lose weight and get leaner, doing cardio before lifting weights can be beneficial. However, if you're trying to build muscle and strength, doing cardio after lifting weights might be a better option. Ultimately, the best workout routine is the one that you enjoy and that helps you achieve your fitness goals.
