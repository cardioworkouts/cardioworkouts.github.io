Should You Do Cardio Or Strength Training To Lose Weight

Written by Luffy Jun 28, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Getting in shape and losing weight is a common goal for many people. However, with so many different exercises out there, it can be challenging to know where to start. One of the most common questions people have is whether they should focus on cardio or strength training to lose weight. If you're struggling to decide between the two, keep reading to find out which one is best for you.

When it comes to losing weight, both cardio and strength training can be effective. However, they work in different ways and have different benefits.

Cardio, also known as aerobic exercise, is any exercise that gets your heart rate up and increases your breathing. It includes activities like running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. Cardio is great for burning calories and increasing your endurance. It can also help reduce the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Strength training, on the other hand, is any exercise that uses resistance to strengthen your muscles. This includes activities like weightlifting, push-ups, and squats. Strength training is great for building muscle, which can help boost your metabolism and burn more calories throughout the day. It can also help improve bone health and reduce the risk of injury.

So, which one should you do to lose weight? The answer is both! Incorporating both cardio and strength training into your workout routine can help you lose weight, build muscle, and improve your overall health.

When it comes to weight loss, the most important factor is creating a calorie deficit. This means burning more calories than you consume. Cardio is great for burning calories during your workout, while strength training can help you build muscle and boost your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

To get the most out of your workout routine, aim to include both cardio and strength training exercises each week. This can be done by alternating between the two or combining them into one workout.

Cardio vs. Strength Training: My Personal Experience

Personally, I have found that incorporating both cardio and strength training into my workout routine has been the most effective for losing weight and getting in shape. I enjoy running and cycling for my cardio workouts and weightlifting for my strength training. By combining the two, I have been able to improve my endurance, build muscle, and burn calories.

The Benefits of Cardio and Strength Training for Weight Loss

Cardio and strength training both have unique benefits when it comes to weight loss. Cardio is great for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health, while strength training is great for building muscle and boosting metabolism. By doing both types of exercises, you can get the best of both worlds and achieve your weight loss goals.

Benefits of Cardio for Weight Loss

Cardio is great for weight loss because it burns calories and increases your heart rate. This can help you create a calorie deficit and lose weight. Cardio also has other health benefits, such as reducing the risk of heart disease, diabetes, and high blood pressure.

Benefits of Strength Training for Weight Loss

Strength training is great for weight loss because it helps build muscle, which can boost your metabolism and burn more calories throughout the day. It also has other health benefits, such as improving bone health and reducing the risk of injury.

My Favorite Cardio and Strength Training Exercises

My favorite cardio exercises include running, cycling, and dancing. For strength training, I enjoy weightlifting, push-ups, and squats. By incorporating a variety of exercises into my workout routine, I stay motivated and challenged.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio and strength training to lose weight?

A: Aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week and two to three strength training sessions per week.

Q: Can I do cardio and strength training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both types of exercises on the same day. Just be sure to give your muscles time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Is it better to do cardio or strength training first?

A: It's up to personal preference, but some studies suggest that doing strength training before cardio can lead to greater fat loss.

Q: Do I need to lift heavy weights to see results?

A: No, you don't need to lift heavy weights to see results. Using lighter weights with more repetitions can still build muscle and improve your overall fitness.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio or Strength Training to Lose Weight

If you're looking to lose weight and get in shape, incorporating both cardio and strength training into your workout routine is the way to go. By doing both types of exercises, you can burn calories, build muscle, and improve your overall health. So, don't choose between cardio or strength training - do both!
