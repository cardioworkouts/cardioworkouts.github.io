Easy Workout Plan For Beginners At The Gym

Written by Frank Jun 25, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to the gym and feeling overwhelmed with where to start with your workout plan? Do you want to improve your health and fitness but don't know how to get started? If so, keep reading to learn about an easy workout plan for beginners at the gym.

Starting a new workout routine can be intimidating, especially if you have never stepped foot in a gym before. And with so many equipment and exercise options available, it can be difficult to know where to begin.

The target of this easy workout plan for beginners at the gym is to provide a simple and effective routine to get you started on your fitness journey. The plan consists of five exercises that target different muscle groups and can be completed in under an hour.

In summary, the easy workout plan for beginners at the gym includes five exercises: the squat, bench press, bent-over row, shoulder press, and deadlift. These exercises target different muscle groups and can be completed in under an hour. By following this plan consistently, you can improve your overall health and fitness level.

Exercise 1: Squat

One of the most effective exercises for building strength and muscle in your legs is the squat. To perform a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointed slightly outward. Hold a barbell on your shoulders and bend your knees, keeping your back straight, until your thighs are parallel with the ground. Then, push through your heels to return to a standing position.

When I first started doing squats, I struggled with keeping my balance and maintaining proper form. However, by starting with a lighter weight and gradually increasing it over time, I was able to improve my technique and build strength in my legs.

Exercise 2: Bench Press

The bench press is a classic exercise for building upper body strength, specifically targeting your chest, shoulders, and triceps. To perform a bench press, lie on a flat bench with your feet flat on the floor. Grab the bar with an overhand grip and lower it to your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body. Then, press the bar back up to the starting position.

When I first started doing bench press, I struggled with the weight and finding the proper hand placement on the bar. However, by starting with a lighter weight and working on my form, I was able to increase the weight and build strength in my chest and arms.

Exercise 3: Bent-Over Row

The bent-over row is an excellent exercise for building strength in your back muscles. To perform a bent-over row, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and hold a barbell with an overhand grip. Bend forward at the hips, keeping your back straight, until your torso is parallel with the floor. Then, pull the bar up to your chest, keeping your elbows close to your body, and lower it back down.

When I first started doing bent-over rows, I struggled with maintaining proper form and keeping my back straight. However, by starting with a lighter weight and gradually increasing it over time, I was able to improve my technique and build strength in my back muscles.

Exercise 4: Shoulder Press

The shoulder press is an effective exercise for building strength in your shoulders and upper back. To perform a shoulder press, sit on a bench with a barbell in front of you. Grab the bar with an overhand grip and lift it to shoulder height. Then, press the bar overhead until your arms are fully extended, and lower it back down to your shoulders.

When I first started doing shoulder presses, I struggled with the weight and maintaining proper form. However, by starting with a lighter weight and focusing on my technique, I was able to build strength in my shoulders and improve my overall form.

Exercise 5: Deadlift

The deadlift is a compound exercise that targets multiple muscle groups, including your back, legs, and core. To perform a deadlift, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and a barbell on the ground in front of you. Bend down and grip the bar with an overhand grip, keeping your back straight. Then, lift the bar up to your waist, keeping your arms straight, and lower it back down to the ground.

When I first started doing deadlifts, I struggled with maintaining proper form and lifting the weight. However, by starting with a lighter weight and focusing on my technique, I was able to build strength in my back and legs and improve my overall form.

Question and Answer

Q: How many sets and reps should I do for each exercise?

A: Aim for 3 sets of 8-12 reps for each exercise.

Q: How often should I do this workout plan?

A: Aim to complete this workout plan 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between.

Q: Can I modify this workout plan to fit my fitness level?

A: Yes, you can modify the weight and reps for each exercise to fit your fitness level. Start with a lighter weight and gradually increase it over time as you build strength.

Q: Should I warm up before starting this workout plan?

A: Yes, it is important to warm up before starting any workout to prevent injury. Aim to spend 5-10 minutes warming up with light cardio or dynamic stretches.

Conclusion of Easy Workout Plan for Beginners at the Gym

Starting a new workout routine can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. By following this easy workout plan for beginners at the gym, you can start building strength and improving your overall health and fitness level. Remember to start with a lighter weight and focus on your technique, gradually increasing the weight over time as you build strength. And always warm up before starting your workout to prevent injury. Happy lifting!
