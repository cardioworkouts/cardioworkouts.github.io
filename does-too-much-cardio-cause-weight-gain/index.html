Does Too Much Cardio Cause Weight Gain

Written by Robby Jul 06, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who spends hours on the treadmill, trying to burn off those extra calories? Have you been working out consistently and still seeing no results? If yes, then you might have come across the question “does too much cardio cause weight gain?”

This question is one of the most commonly asked questions in the fitness industry. People who want to lose weight often resort to cardio exercises, but sometimes, they end up gaining weight instead of losing it. This can be frustrating and demotivating for those who have been working hard to achieve their fitness goals.

The simple answer to the question “does too much cardio cause weight gain?” is no. Cardio exercises are meant to help you lose weight and improve your cardiovascular health. However, doing too much of it can have the opposite effect.

In this article, we will discuss the relationship between cardio exercises and weight gain. We will explore the reasons why some people gain weight despite doing cardio exercises and how to prevent it.

My Personal Experience

I used to be someone who believed that cardio was the only way to lose weight. I would spend hours on the treadmill, running at a steady pace, trying to burn off the extra calories. However, after a few weeks of doing this, I noticed that I was not losing any weight. In fact, I had gained a few pounds.

It was then that I realized that too much cardio can cause weight gain. I started doing some research and found out that doing too much cardio can lead to muscle loss, which can slow down your metabolism and make it harder to lose weight.

The Relationship Between Cardio and Weight Gain

Cardio exercises are meant to help you burn calories and lose weight. However, doing too much cardio can cause your body to go into a catabolic state, which can lead to muscle loss. When you lose muscle, your metabolism slows down, making it harder for you to burn calories and lose weight.

Another reason why some people gain weight despite doing cardio exercises is that they tend to overeat after their workouts. They feel like they have burned a lot of calories and can afford to indulge in unhealthy foods. This can lead to weight gain instead of weight loss.

How Much Cardio Should You Do?

The amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness goals and your body type. If you want to lose weight, you should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week. This can be spread out over five days, with each session lasting for 30 minutes.

However, if you are someone who is looking to build muscle, you should limit your cardio to 20-30 minutes per session, 2-3 times a week. This will help you maintain your muscle mass and prevent muscle loss.

How to Prevent Weight Gain from Cardio

The best way to prevent weight gain from cardio exercises is to combine them with strength training. Strength training helps you build muscle, which can speed up your metabolism and help you burn more calories.

Another way to prevent weight gain from cardio is to make sure that you are not overeating after your workouts. Stick to a healthy and balanced diet and avoid indulging in unhealthy foods.

Question and Answer

Q: Can too much cardio cause weight gain?

A: Yes, too much cardio can cause weight gain if it leads to muscle loss, which can slow down your metabolism.

Q: How much cardio should I do to lose weight?

A: If you want to lose weight, you should aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week.

Q: How can I prevent weight gain from cardio?

A: You can prevent weight gain from cardio by combining it with strength training and avoiding overeating after your workouts.

Q: Can cardio exercises help me build muscle?

A: Cardio exercises can help you build endurance and improve your cardiovascular health, but they are not the most effective way to build muscle. Strength training is the best way to build muscle.

Conclusion

In conclusion, doing too much cardio can lead to weight gain if it leads to muscle loss. It is important to find a balance between cardio exercises and strength training to achieve your fitness goals. Make sure that you are not overeating after your workouts and stick to a healthy and balanced diet to prevent weight gain.
