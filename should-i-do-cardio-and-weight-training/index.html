Should I Do Cardio And Weight Training

Written by Aprilia Sep 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you wondering whether you should do cardio and weight training? Are you confused because you've heard different opinions about which one is better? Don't worry, you're not alone. Many people are unsure whether they should focus on cardio or weight training or do both. In this article, we'll answer your questions and help you decide what's best for you.

If you're reading this article, you probably have some pain points related to cardio and weight training. Maybe you're not sure which one is more effective for weight loss or muscle gain. Or maybe you're afraid that doing cardio will make you lose muscle, while weight training will make you bulky. These are common concerns that we'll address in this article.

The short answer is that you should do both cardio and weight training. Both types of exercise have their benefits and can help you achieve your fitness goals. Cardio will improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and help you lose weight. Weight training will build muscle, increase strength, and boost your metabolism. By combining the two, you'll get the best of both worlds.

In summary, you should do both cardio and weight training to achieve your fitness goals. Cardio will help you lose weight and improve your cardiovascular health, while weight training will build muscle and increase strength. By combining the two, you'll get the best results.

Cardio and Weight Training: How to Get Started

When it comes to cardio and weight training, it's important to find a balance that works for you. You don't have to do both types of exercise every day, but you should aim to do each one at least twice a week. Here's an example of a weekly workout schedule that incorporates both cardio and weight training:

Monday: Cardio (30 minutes on the treadmill or elliptical)
Tuesday: Weight Training (upper body)
Wednesday: Rest
Thursday: Cardio (30 minutes on the bike or rowing machine)
Friday: Weight Training (lower body)
Saturday: Rest
Sunday: Cardio (30 minutes on the stair climber or treadmill)

Of course, you can adjust this schedule to fit your preferences and fitness level. If you're new to exercise, start with shorter workouts and gradually increase the duration and intensity. If you have any health concerns, consult with a doctor or fitness professional before starting a new exercise program.

The Benefits of Cardio and Weight Training

Cardio and weight training have numerous benefits, both physical and mental. Here are some of the top benefits of each type of exercise:

Cardio

- Improves cardiovascular health
- Burns calories and helps with weight loss
- Boosts mood and reduces stress
- Increases endurance and stamina
- Lowers the risk of chronic diseases like diabetes, heart disease, and cancer

Weight Training

- Builds muscle and increases strength
- Boosts metabolism and helps with weight loss
- Improves bone density and reduces the risk of osteoporosis
- Increases energy and reduces fatigue
- Improves posture and reduces the risk of back pain

Question and Answer

Q: Will cardio make me lose muscle?
A: No, cardio will not make you lose muscle as long as you're eating enough protein and doing weight training to maintain your muscle mass.

Q: Will weight training make me bulky?
A: No, weight training will not make you bulky unless you're specifically trying to build muscle mass. Most people will see a toned and defined physique with weight training.

Q: How much cardio should I do?
A: Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week.

Q: How often should I do weight training?
A: Aim for at least 2-3 weight training sessions per week, with a focus on all major muscle groups.

Conclusion of Should I Do Cardio and Weight Training

In conclusion, cardio and weight training are both important for achieving your fitness goals. By combining the two, you'll get the best results and improve your overall health and wellbeing. Remember to find a balance that works for you and start with a manageable exercise routine that you can stick to. With time and consistency, you'll see the benefits of cardio and weight training in your body and mind.
