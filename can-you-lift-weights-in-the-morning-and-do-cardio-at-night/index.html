Can You Lift Weights In The Morning And Do Cardio At Night

Written by Oliver Oct 16, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym but struggles with finding the right time to fit in both weight lifting and cardio? You may have heard people say that it's best to do weight lifting in the morning and cardio at night, or vice versa. But is it really possible to do both? Let's find out!

The Pain Points

Many people struggle with finding the right time to fit in both weight lifting and cardio. Some may not have enough time to do both during the same workout session or may feel too fatigued to do both in one go. Others may have heard conflicting advice about when to do each type of exercise, leaving them unsure of what to do.

The Answer

The short answer is yes, you can lift weights in the morning and do cardio at night, or vice versa. The important thing is to find a routine that works for you and your schedule. Some people find that splitting their workouts into two sessions helps them to have more energy and focus during each workout. However, it's important to keep in mind that everyone's body is different and what works for one person may not work for another.

Main Points on Can You Lift Weights in the Morning and Do Cardio at Night

1. It's possible to do both weight lifting and cardio in the same day, either in the same session or split into two separate sessions.

2. It's important to listen to your body and find a routine that works for you and your schedule.

3. Everyone's body is different, so what works for one person may not work for another.

Personal Experience - Splitting Workouts

Personally, I find that splitting my workouts into two sessions - weight lifting in the morning and cardio at night - works best for me. I have more energy and focus during each workout, and it allows me to fit in both types of exercise without feeling too fatigued. However, I know some people who prefer to do both in the same session and feel great doing so. It's all about finding what works best for you.

Personal Experience - Same Session

On the other hand, my friend prefers to do both weight lifting and cardio in the same session. She finds that it saves her time and allows her to get both types of exercise done in one go. She also feels energized and focused throughout the workout. Again, it's all about finding what works best for your body and your schedule.

More Detail on Can You Lift Weights in the Morning and Do Cardio at Night

If you're still unsure about whether you can lift weights in the morning and do cardio at night, it's important to keep in mind some key factors. First, make sure you're getting enough rest and recovery time between workouts. This will help prevent injury and allow your body to repair and grow stronger.

Second, make sure you're fueling your body with the right nutrients before and after your workouts. This will help give you the energy you need to power through your workouts and aid in recovery.

Third, listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed. If you feel too fatigued doing both in the same session, try splitting them up. If you feel great doing both in the same session, stick with it!

How to Lift Weights in the Morning and Do Cardio at Night

If you're ready to try splitting your workouts into two sessions, here are some tips:

1. Plan your workouts ahead of time to make sure you're getting enough of both weight lifting and cardio each week.

2. Make sure you're getting enough rest and recovery time between sessions.

3. Fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after each session.

4. Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed.

Personal Experience - Finding Balance

For me, finding the right balance between weight lifting and cardio has been a journey. I used to focus solely on weight lifting and neglect cardio altogether. However, I soon realized the importance of incorporating cardio into my routine for overall health and fitness. Now, I try to fit in both weight lifting and cardio each week, whether it's in the same session or split into two sessions.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight lifting?

A: It's generally recommended to do weight lifting before cardio, as weight lifting requires more energy and focus. However, ultimately it's up to personal preference and what works best for your body.

Q: How much cardio should I do each week?

A: The American Heart Association recommends at least 150 minutes of moderate aerobic exercise or 75 minutes of vigorous aerobic exercise per week. This can be split into multiple sessions throughout the week.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight lifting on the same day?

A: Yes, it's possible to do both cardio and weight lifting on the same day. However, make sure to listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed.

Q: Should I do cardio every day?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio every day. It's important to give your body rest and recovery time between workouts. Aim for at least 2-3 days of cardio per week, with rest days in between.

Conclusion of Can You Lift Weights in the Morning and Do Cardio at Night

Ultimately, whether you choose to lift weights in the morning and do cardio at night or vice versa, it's important to find a routine that works for you and your schedule. Listen to your body, fuel it with the right nutrients, and adjust your routine as needed. With the right approach, you can successfully incorporate both weight lifting and cardio into your fitness routine.
