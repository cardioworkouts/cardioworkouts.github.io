How Many Calories Are Burned With 20 Minutes Of Cardio

Written by Robby Sep 02, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to burn some calories but don't have a lot of time to spare? Cardio might be the answer for you! With just 20 minutes of cardio, you can burn a significant amount of calories. In this article, we'll explore how many calories are burned with 20 minutes of cardio and some tips to maximize your calorie burn.

The Pain Points of How Many Calories Are Burned with 20 Minutes of Cardio

Many people struggle with finding time to work out, but they still want to see results. With only 20 minutes of cardio, it's understandable to wonder if it's enough time to burn a significant amount of calories. It's also easy to get discouraged when the number on the scale doesn't seem to budge, even with regular exercise. However, understanding how many calories are burned with 20 minutes of cardio can help you track your progress and stay motivated.

How Many Calories Are Burned with 20 Minutes of Cardio?

The number of calories burned with 20 minutes of cardio varies depending on several factors, such as your weight, age, and the type of exercise you're doing. On average, a person can burn between 100-300 calories with 20 minutes of cardio.

If you weigh less, you'll burn fewer calories than someone who weighs more, and if you're doing high-intensity exercises like running or jumping jacks, you'll burn more calories than if you're doing low-intensity exercises like walking or cycling.

Maximizing Your Calorie Burn with 20 Minutes of Cardio

There are several ways to maximize your calorie burn during 20 minutes of cardio. One of the most effective ways is to do high-intensity interval training (HIIT) workouts. HIIT workouts involve short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by brief periods of rest. This type of workout has been shown to burn more calories in less time than steady-state cardio.

Another way to increase your calorie burn is to add resistance training to your cardio workout. Resistance training helps build muscle, which burns more calories than fat, even at rest. By combining cardio and resistance training, you can burn more calories in less time.

The Science Behind Calorie Burn with 20 Minutes of Cardio

When you exercise, your body burns calories to fuel the activity. Cardio exercises like running, cycling, and jumping jacks increase your heart rate and breathing rate, which boosts your metabolism and calorie burn. The more intense the exercise, the more calories you'll burn.

Your body also continues to burn calories after you finish exercising. This is known as the "afterburn effect" or excess post-exercise oxygen consumption (EPOC). HIIT workouts and resistance training have been shown to increase the afterburn effect, which means you'll continue to burn calories even after your workout is over.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your 20-Minute Cardio Workout

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your 20-minute cardio workout:

  • Choose high-intensity exercises like running, jumping jacks, or burpees
  • Try HIIT workouts to maximize your calorie burn
  • Add resistance training to your cardio workout to build muscle
  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water before, during, and after your workout
  • Warm up before your workout to prevent injury
  • Cool down after your workout to gradually lower your heart rate

Question and Answer

Q: Can I burn more calories with longer cardio sessions?

A: Yes, typically, the longer you exercise, the more calories you'll burn. However, the intensity of the exercise also plays a role in calorie burn.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after resistance training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to build muscle, it's best to do resistance training first, as it requires more energy and focus. If your goal is to burn fat and improve cardiovascular health, it's best to do cardio first, as it will warm up your muscles and prepare your body for resistance training.

Q: How often should I do cardio to see results?

A: It's recommended to do cardio at least three times a week for 20-30 minutes per session to see results. However, the frequency and duration of your cardio workouts depend on your fitness goals and physical abilities.

Q: Can I lose weight with 20 minutes of cardio every day?

A: Yes, you can lose weight with 20 minutes of cardio every day, as long as you're in a calorie deficit. A calorie deficit means you're burning more calories than you're consuming. To lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit of 500-1000 calories per day through a combination of diet and exercise.

Conclusion of How Many Calories Are Burned with 20 Minutes of Cardio

With just 20 minutes of cardio, you can burn a significant amount of calories and improve your cardiovascular health. By understanding how many calories are burned with 20 minutes of cardio and incorporating tips to maximize your calorie burn, you can achieve your fitness goals in less time. Remember to choose high-intensity exercises, add resistance training to your workout, and stay hydrated to get the most out of your 20-minute cardio sessions.
