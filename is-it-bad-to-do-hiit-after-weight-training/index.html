Is It Bad To Do Hiit After Weight Training

Written by Daniel Aug 30, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who enjoys both weight training and HIIT workouts? Have you ever wondered if it is a good idea to combine both of these types of workouts? Many people ask the question, "Is it bad to do HIIT after weight training?" In this post, we will explore the answer to that question and provide you with some valuable information about how to approach combining these workouts.

Combining weight training and HIIT workouts can be challenging, and it's essential to understand the possible pain points that can arise when doing both types of workouts in one day. These pain points can include fatigue, soreness, and the potential for injury.

The answer to whether it is bad to do HIIT after weight training is not a straightforward one. The answer depends on your goals, fitness level, and overall health. In general, if you are a beginner or someone who is just starting a workout routine, it may not be the best idea to do both workouts in the same day.

In summary, combining weight training and HIIT workouts is possible, but it's essential to approach it with caution. It's also essential to consider your fitness level and health before attempting both workouts in one day.

My Personal Experience with HIIT after Weight Training

As someone who enjoys both weight training and HIIT workouts, I have experimented with combining both types of workouts. In my experience, it's best to do HIIT after weight training. Doing HIIT after weight training allows me to get my heart rate up and burn more calories while also building muscle.

The Benefits of Doing HIIT after Weight Training

There are several benefits to doing HIIT after weight training. First, it allows you to burn more calories without sacrificing muscle mass. Second, it can help improve cardiovascular health and endurance. Third, it can help you break through a plateau in your workout routine.

How to Approach Doing HIIT after Weight Training

If you decide to combine weight training and HIIT workouts, it's essential to approach it with caution. Here are some tips to help you approach doing HIIT after weight training:

  • Start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts.
  • Make sure to warm up before each workout.
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workouts.
  • Listen to your body and take rest days when needed.
  • Consult with a personal trainer or medical professional before starting any new workout routine.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it safe to do HIIT after weight training?

A: It depends on your fitness level and health. It's best to approach it with caution and start slowly.

Q: Can doing HIIT after weight training help me lose weight?

A: Yes, doing HIIT after weight training can help you burn more calories and lose weight.

Q: How often should I do HIIT after weight training?

A: It's best to start with once or twice a week and gradually increase the frequency as you build up your fitness level.

Q: Can I do HIIT before weight training?

A: It's generally not recommended to do HIIT before weight training as it can lead to fatigue and decreased performance during weight training.

Conclusion of Is it Bad to Do HIIT after Weight Training

Combining weight training and HIIT workouts can provide numerous benefits, but it's crucial to approach it with caution. Always listen to your body and consult with a personal trainer or medical professional before starting any new workout routine. Remember to start slowly, stay hydrated, and take rest days when needed. With the right approach, you can safely and effectively combine weight training and HIIT workouts to achieve your fitness goals.
