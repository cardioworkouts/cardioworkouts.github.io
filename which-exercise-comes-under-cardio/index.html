Which Exercises Come Under Cardio A Beginner s Guide

Written by Frank Aug 07, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to get fit and healthy, but you're not sure which exercises come under cardio? Look no further! In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about cardio exercises and how they can benefit you.

Many people struggle with finding the right type of exercise to help them achieve their fitness goals. Some may think that lifting weights or doing yoga is the best way to get in shape, but cardio is an essential component to any exercise routine. Cardio exercises are specifically designed to increase your heart rate and improve your overall cardiovascular health.

So, which exercises come under cardio? The short answer is any exercise that gets your heart rate up and keeps it elevated for an extended period. Examples of cardio exercises include running, cycling, swimming, jumping rope, and dancing. These activities can be done indoors or outdoors, and they can be tailored to your fitness level.

In summary, cardio exercises are an excellent way to improve your overall health and fitness. By incorporating these exercises into your routine, you can increase your endurance, strengthen your heart and lungs, and burn calories. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced athlete, there's a cardio exercise out there for you. So, get moving and start reaping the benefits of cardio today!

Running

Running is one of the most popular cardio exercises out there, and for a good reason. It's an excellent way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. When I first started running, I struggled to run for more than a few minutes without getting winded. However, over time, I built up my endurance and was able to run for longer periods.

If you're new to running, start by jogging for short intervals, and gradually increase your time and distance. Remember to wear proper running shoes and stretch before and after your run to prevent injury. Running is an excellent cardio exercise that can be done anywhere, and it's a great way to get outside and enjoy the fresh air.

Cycling

Cycling is another popular cardio exercise that's low impact and easy on your joints. When I first started cycling, I was surprised at how quickly my heart rate increased, even though I was going at a leisurely pace. Cycling is an excellent way to get your heart rate up and burn calories while enjoying the great outdoors.

Whether you're cycling on a stationary bike or a road bike, make sure to wear proper gear and adjust your bike to fit your body correctly. Start by cycling for short intervals and gradually increase your time and distance. Cycling is a fun and rewarding cardio exercise that can be done alone or with friends.

Swimming

Swimming is a fantastic cardio exercise that's easy on your joints and perfect for those who have injuries or chronic pain. When I first started swimming, I was surprised at how challenging it was to swim for an extended period. However, over time, I built up my endurance and was able to swim longer distances.

If you're new to swimming, start by swimming laps for short intervals, and gradually increase your time and distance. Remember to wear proper swim gear and stretch before and after your swim to prevent injury. Swimming is an excellent cardio exercise that can help improve your overall fitness and health.

Jumping Rope

Jumping rope is a simple yet effective cardio exercise that can be done anywhere. When I first started jumping rope, I was surprised at how quickly my heart rate increased. Jumping rope is an excellent way to get your heart rate up and burn calories while having fun.

If you're new to jumping rope, start by jumping for short intervals and gradually increase your time and speed. Remember to wear proper footwear and stretch before and after your jump rope session to prevent injury. Jumping rope is a fun and challenging cardio exercise that can help improve your overall fitness and endurance.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio exercises?

A: It's recommended that you do cardio exercises for at least 30 minutes a day, five days a week. However, if you're new to exercising, start by doing cardio exercises for a shorter period and gradually increase your time and intensity.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio exercises?

A: The time it takes to see results from cardio exercises varies from person to person. However, if you're consistent with your cardio exercises and maintain a healthy diet, you should start to see results within a few weeks.

Q: Can I do cardio exercises if I have joint pain or injuries?

A: Yes, there are many low-impact cardio exercises that are perfect for those with joint pain or injuries, such as cycling or swimming. Always consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine.

Q: Can I do cardio exercises at home without any equipment?

A: Yes, there are many cardio exercises that can be done at home without any equipment, such as jumping jacks, high knees, or burpees. You can also go for a run or walk outside or use household items such as stairs for a cardio workout.

Conclusion of which exercise comes under cardio

Cardio exercises are an essential component to any exercise routine. They can help improve your overall fitness and health, increase your endurance, strengthen your heart and lungs, and burn calories. Whether you prefer running, cycling, swimming, or jumping rope, there's a cardio exercise out there for you. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase your time and intensity to prevent injury. So, get moving and start reaping the benefits of cardio today!
