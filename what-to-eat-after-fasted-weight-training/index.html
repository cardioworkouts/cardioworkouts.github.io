What To Eat After Fasted Weight Training

Written by Oliver Sep 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Do you ever feel tired or drained after a workout? You're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right post-workout meal, especially after fasted weight training. But fear not, we've got you covered. In this article, we'll discuss what to eat after fasted weight training to help you recover and feel energized.

After fasted weight training, your body needs fuel to repair and rebuild muscles. It's essential to eat the right nutrients to help your body recover and prevent muscle soreness. But with so many options out there, it can be overwhelming to know which foods to choose.

The target of what to eat after fasted weight training is to replenish glycogen stores and provide your body with protein to repair muscle tissue. Ideally, you should consume a meal within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout. This meal should include a combination of carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats to aid in recovery.

To summarize, the right post-workout meal after fasted weight training should include carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats. The carbohydrates will replenish glycogen stores, while protein helps repair muscle tissue, and healthy fats provide energy and aid in nutrient absorption.

My Personal Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I've experimented with various post-workout meals. After fasted weight training, I found that a protein smoothie with fruits and nuts works best for me. It's easy to make, and I can consume it on the go.

I blend one scoop of protein powder, frozen berries, a banana, and a tablespoon of almond butter with almond milk. This smoothie provides me with the right combination of nutrients, and I feel energized after drinking it.

What Foods to Eat After Fasted Weight Training?

Now that we know what to look for in a post-workout meal let's dive into specific foods to eat after fasted weight training.

Carbohydrates: Sweet potatoes, brown rice, quinoa, fruits, and vegetables.

Protein: Chicken, eggs, Greek yogurt, tofu, and protein powder.

Healthy fats: Avocado, nuts, seeds, and olive oil.

Why Carbohydrates?

Carbohydrates are essential after fasted weight training because they replenish glycogen stores. Glycogen is a form of glucose stored in muscles and used as energy during exercise. When you exercise, glycogen stores become depleted, and it's crucial to replenish them to avoid fatigue and muscle soreness.

Why Protein?

Protein is critical after fasted weight training because it helps repair muscle tissue. When you exercise, micro-tears occur in muscle fibers, and protein helps repair and rebuild these fibers. This process is essential for muscle growth and recovery.

My Favorite Post-Workout Meal

As I mentioned earlier, my favorite post-workout meal after fasted weight training is a protein smoothie with fruits and nuts. But if you're looking for more meal options, here are a few of my go-to's:

Grilled chicken breast with sweet potato and broccoli.

Scrambled eggs with avocado toast and a side of fruit.

Greek yogurt with granola, berries, and a drizzle of honey.

Question and Answer

Q: How much protein should I consume after fasted weight training?

A: It's recommended to consume at least 20 grams of protein after fasted weight training.

Q: Can I eat carbs after fasted weight training if my goal is to lose weight?

A: Yes, you can still eat carbs after fasted weight training if your goal is to lose weight. Carbohydrates are essential for recovery and replenishing glycogen stores, which will help you perform better during your next workout.

Q: Can I drink a protein shake instead of eating a meal after fasted weight training?

A: Yes, you can drink a protein shake instead of eating a meal after fasted weight training. Just make sure it includes carbohydrates and healthy fats as well.

Q: How long should I wait to eat after fasted weight training?

A: Ideally, you should consume a meal within 30 minutes to an hour after fasted weight training.

Conclusion of What to Eat After Fasted Weight Training

Knowing what to eat after fasted weight training can be overwhelming, but it doesn't have to be. By including carbohydrates, protein, and healthy fats in your post-workout meal, you'll be on your way to a speedy recovery and feeling energized for your next workout. Remember to listen to your body and experiment with different foods to find what works best for you.
