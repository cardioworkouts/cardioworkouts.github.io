What Should I Do First Abs Or Cardio

Written by Daniel Jun 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you someone who wants to start exercising but is unsure whether to do abs or cardio first? If yes, then this blog post is for you. In this post, we will discuss the benefits of doing abs or cardio first, and help you decide which one is right for you.

Pain Points of What Should I Do First Abs or Cardio

Starting an exercise routine can be overwhelming, especially if you are not sure where to start. Many people struggle with deciding whether to do abs or cardio first. Doing the wrong type of exercise first can cause injury or not provide the desired results. This decision can be confusing for beginners and even experienced exercisers.

Answer to What Should I Do First Abs or Cardio

The answer to this question depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build endurance, improve heart health, and lose weight, then cardio should be your priority. If you want to build core strength, improve posture, and get a toned midsection, then doing abs first is the way to go.

Summary of What Should I Do First Abs or Cardio

In summary, the decision of whether to do abs or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight and improve heart health, then cardio should be your priority. If you want to build core strength and get a toned midsection, then doing abs first is the way to go.

Benefits of Doing Cardio First

Personally, I prefer doing cardio first because it warms up my body and gets my heart pumping. Doing cardio first also helps burn more calories, which is great for weight loss. When you do cardio, your body burns calories from fat and carbohydrates, which can help you lose weight faster. Another benefit of doing cardio first is that it improves heart health and endurance. By improving your cardiovascular health, you can reduce the risk of heart disease, high blood pressure, and stroke.

Benefits of Doing Abs First

On the other hand, doing abs first can help you build core strength and improve posture. When you do abs exercises, you are engaging your core muscles, which can help you develop a strong midsection. A strong core can also help you improve your posture, which can reduce the risk of back pain and other injuries. Doing abs first can also help you get a toned midsection, which is great for those who want to show off their abs.

How to Do Cardio First?

If you decide to do cardio first, it is important to start with a warm-up. A warm-up can help prevent injury and prepare your body for exercise. You can start with a light jog or walk for five to ten minutes. Once you have warmed up, you can move on to your cardio workout. You can do a variety of cardio exercises, such as running, cycling, swimming, or dancing. Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, five times a week.

How to Do Abs First?

If you decide to do abs first, it is important to start with a warm-up as well. You can start with light jog or walk for five to ten minutes. Once you have warmed up, you can move on to your abs workout. You can do a variety of abs exercises, such as crunches, plank, leg raises, or bicycle crunches. Aim for at least 15 minutes of abs workout per day, three times a week.

Additional Benefits of Doing Both

Doing both cardio and abs can provide additional benefits. Doing cardio can help you burn calories and improve heart health, while doing abs can help you build core strength and improve posture. By doing both, you can achieve a well-rounded workout routine that can help you achieve your fitness goals faster.

Conclusion of What Should I Do First Abs or Cardio

Ultimately, the decision of whether to do abs or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight and improve heart health, then cardio should be your priority. If you want to build core strength and get a toned midsection, then doing abs first is the way to go. However, doing both can provide additional benefits and help you achieve a well-rounded workout routine. Remember to start with a warm-up before starting any exercise and always listen to your body.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and abs on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and abs on the same day. It is important to start with a warm-up and then move on to your cardio or abs workout. You can also alternate between cardio and abs exercises to prevent muscle fatigue.

Q: How long should I do cardio and abs on the same day?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals. If you are a beginner, aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio and 15 minutes of abs exercises per day. If you are an experienced exerciser, you can increase the duration and intensity of your workout.

Q: Which cardio exercises are best for weight loss?

A: The best cardio exercises for weight loss are those that get your heart rate up and burn more calories. Running, cycling, swimming, and dancing are all great cardio exercises for weight loss.

Q: How often should I do cardio and abs exercises?

A: Aim for at least 30 minutes of cardio per day, five times a week, and 15 minutes of abs exercises per day, three times a week. You can also alternate between cardio and abs exercises to prevent muscle fatigue.
