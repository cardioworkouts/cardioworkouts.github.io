How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training

Written by Christine Jul 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who is looking to lose weight or build muscle? Do you often wonder how long you should do cardio after weight training? You're not alone. This is a common question among gym-goers and fitness enthusiasts. It's important to know the answer because doing cardio for too long or too little can have negative effects on your overall fitness goals.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance between weight training and cardio. They often wonder whether cardio should be done before or after weight training, how long to do cardio after weight training, and how much cardio to do. These are all valid concerns, and we'll be addressing them in this article.

The answer to how long you should do cardio after weight training varies depending on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle, then you should limit your cardio sessions to 20-30 minutes. However, if you're looking to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, then you can do cardio for up to an hour.

In summary, the ideal duration of cardio after weight training is 20-30 minutes for muscle building and up to an hour for weight loss and cardiovascular health. It's important to note that everyone's fitness goals are different, so it's best to consult a fitness expert before making any changes to your routine.

My Personal Experience with How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training

As someone who has been weight training for a while, I struggled with finding the right balance between cardio and weight training. Initially, I used to do an hour of cardio after every weight training session, but I noticed that I wasn't seeing the results I wanted. I decided to consult a fitness expert, and they advised me to limit my cardio sessions to 20-30 minutes if I wanted to build muscle. I followed their advice, and I noticed a significant improvement in my muscle growth.

How Much Cardio Should You Do After Weight Training?

The amount of cardio you should do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle, then you should limit your cardio sessions to 20-30 minutes. However, if you're looking to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health, then you can do cardio for up to an hour. It's best to consult a fitness expert to determine the right amount of cardio for your fitness goals.

Cardio Before or After Weight Training?

Cardio can be done before or after weight training, depending on your fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle, then it's best to do weight training first and cardio second. This is because weight training requires more energy and strength, and doing cardio first can tire you out, making it difficult to lift heavy weights. However, if you're looking to lose weight, then it's best to do cardio first and weight training second. This is because doing cardio first can help you burn more calories during your weight training session.

How to Make the Most Out of Your Cardio After Weight Training?

If you want to make the most out of your cardio after weight training, then it's essential to choose the right type of cardio. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is a great option because it helps you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health in a shorter amount of time. Additionally, it's important to vary your cardio routine to prevent boredom and to challenge your body.

Conclusion of How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Training

The ideal duration of cardio after weight training is 20-30 minutes for muscle building and up to an hour for weight loss and cardiovascular health. It's important to choose the right type of cardio and to consult a fitness expert to determine the right amount of cardio for your fitness goals. Remember that everyone's fitness goals are different, so it's best to find what works for you and stick to it.

Question and Answer

Q: Can doing too much cardio after weight training have negative effects?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio after weight training can lead to muscle loss and fatigue.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day. Just make sure to prioritize weight training if your goal is muscle building.

Q: Should I do cardio every day?

A: It's not necessary to do cardio every day. It's best to vary your routine and give your body time to rest and recover.

Q: What is the best time to do cardio after weight training?

A: The best time to do cardio after weight training is within 30 minutes to an hour after your weight training session.
