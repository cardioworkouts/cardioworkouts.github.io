Is Hiit Cardio Better Than Running

Written by Aprilia Jul 27, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you tired of running for hours on end without seeing any results? Are you looking for a more effective workout that will help you burn fat and build muscle? Look no further than HIIT cardio.

For many people, running is the go-to form of cardio. However, it can be time-consuming, monotonous, and can lead to injuries such as shin splints or knee pain.

HIIT (High-Intensity Interval Training) cardio, on the other hand, involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. It has been shown to burn more calories in less time than steady-state cardio, making it a popular choice for those looking to improve their fitness levels.

In this article, we will explore the benefits of HIIT cardio and compare it to running to determine which is the better option for you.

What is HIIT Cardio?

HIIT cardio involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. The high-intensity intervals are typically 20-90 seconds long, and the rest periods are usually 10-30 seconds long. This type of training can be done with a variety of exercises, including running, cycling, and bodyweight exercises.

HIIT cardio is designed to elevate your heart rate quickly, which causes your body to burn more calories than it would during steady-state cardio. This type of training is also known to boost your metabolism, making it easier to burn fat even when you’re not exercising.

Benefits of HIIT Cardio

There are many benefits to HIIT cardio, including:

  • Burns more calories than steady-state cardio
  • Boosts your metabolism
  • Improves your cardiovascular fitness
  • Can be done anywhere with little to no equipment
  • Helps you maintain muscle mass while losing fat

Overall, HIIT cardio is a great option for those looking to improve their fitness levels and burn fat quickly.

HIIT Cardio vs. Running

While both HIIT cardio and running can be effective forms of cardio, there are a few key differences between the two.

Firstly, HIIT cardio typically burns more calories than running. This is because HIIT cardio elevates your heart rate quickly, causing your body to burn more calories in a shorter amount of time.

Secondly, HIIT cardio is more time-efficient than running. You can complete a full HIIT workout in 20-30 minutes, whereas running for the same amount of time may not burn as many calories.

Finally, HIIT cardio is more versatile than running. You can do HIIT workouts with a variety of exercises, including bodyweight exercises, cycling, and even swimming. Running, on the other hand, can be monotonous and can lead to injuries such as shin splints or knee pain.

My Experience with HIIT Cardio

Personally, I have found that HIIT cardio is a much more effective form of cardio than running. I used to spend hours running on a treadmill without seeing any results, but as soon as I switched to HIIT, I started seeing changes in my body almost immediately.

I love the variety that HIIT offers, and I can complete a full workout in just 30 minutes. It’s also great for those days when I don’t have access to a gym, as I can do bodyweight exercises in my living room.

How to Get Started with HIIT Cardio

If you’re interested in trying HIIT cardio, there are a few things you should keep in mind:

  • Start slowly: HIIT cardio is intense, so it’s important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity over time.
  • Choose exercises that you enjoy: HIIT can be done with a variety of exercises, so choose ones that you enjoy to make it more fun.
  • Rest properly: It’s important to rest properly between intervals to allow your body to recover.
  • Stay hydrated: HIIT cardio can be intense, so it’s important to stay hydrated throughout your workout.

FAQs

Q: Is HIIT cardio better than running for weight loss?

A: Yes, HIIT cardio is generally considered more effective for weight loss than running.

Q: How many times per week should I do HIIT cardio?

A: It’s recommended to do HIIT cardio 2-3 times per week.

Q: How long should a HIIT workout be?

A: HIIT workouts can range from 10-30 minutes.

Q: Can HIIT cardio be done at home?

A: Yes, HIIT cardio can be done anywhere with little to no equipment.

Conclusion of Is HIIT Cardio Better Than Running

While both HIIT cardio and running can be effective forms of cardio, HIIT cardio is generally considered more effective for weight loss and is more versatile than running. If you’re looking to improve your fitness levels and burn fat quickly, HIIT cardio is definitely worth a try.
