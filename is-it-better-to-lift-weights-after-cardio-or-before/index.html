Is It Better To Lift Weights After Cardio Or Before

Written by Aprilia Sep 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you confused about when to lift weights and do cardio? Are you unsure whether it's better to lift weights after cardio or before? You're not alone. It's a common dilemma for people who want to maximize their workouts. In this post, we'll explore the pros and cons of both approaches and help you determine which one is right for you.

The goal of any workout is to get the most out of your time and effort. You want to burn calories, build muscle, and improve your overall fitness. But when it comes to lifting weights and doing cardio, there are different opinions on the best order to do them. Some people swear by lifting weights before cardio, while others prefer to do it the other way around.

So, is it better to lift weights after cardio or before? The answer is...it depends. It depends on your fitness goals, your personal preferences, and your schedule.

In general, if your goal is to build muscle, it's better to lift weights before doing cardio. This is because weightlifting requires a lot of energy and focus. If you're already tired from cardio, you may not be able to perform your weightlifting exercises with proper form and intensity. Additionally, lifting weights first will help you warm up your muscles and prepare them for the cardio session.

On the other hand, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness, it's better to do cardio before lifting weights. Cardio will get your heart rate up and help you burn calories, which will give you more energy for your weightlifting session. Additionally, doing cardio first can help you avoid injury by warming up your muscles and joints.

My Personal Experience

Personally, I prefer to do weightlifting before cardio. I find that I have more energy and focus when I start with weightlifting. Doing cardio first tends to tire me out and make me less motivated to lift weights.

Pros and Cons of Lifting Weights After Cardio or Before

There are pros and cons to both approaches. Here are some of the main ones:

Pros of lifting weights before cardio:

  • More energy and focus for weightlifting
  • Can help warm up muscles for cardio
  • May be better for building muscle

Cons of lifting weights before cardio:

  • May not have as much energy for cardio
  • May not burn as many calories during cardio
  • May be more prone to injury during cardio

Pros of doing cardio before lifting weights:

  • More energy and stamina for cardio
  • May burn more calories during cardio
  • Can help warm up muscles and prevent injury

Cons of doing cardio before lifting weights:

  • May not have as much energy or focus for weightlifting
  • May not be as effective for building muscle
  • May tire you out for weightlifting

Factors to Consider

When deciding whether to lift weights after cardio or before, consider the following factors:

  • Your fitness goals
  • Your personal preferences
  • Your schedule
  • Your current fitness level
  • Whether you're more focused on building muscle or improving your cardiovascular fitness

Tips for Combining Cardio and Weightlifting

If you want to combine cardio and weightlifting in the same workout, here are some tips to help you get the most out of your session:

  • Start with a warm-up to get your muscles ready for exercise
  • Do weightlifting exercises that target multiple muscle groups
  • Alternate between weightlifting and cardio intervals
  • End with a cool-down and stretching session

My Personal Experience

I like to alternate between weightlifting and cardio intervals when I'm short on time. This helps me get a full-body workout and burn calories while still building muscle. I also make sure to do a proper warm-up and cool-down to prevent injury.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can. Just make sure to warm up properly and alternate between weightlifting and cardio intervals.

Q: How long should I wait between cardio and weightlifting?

A: It's best to wait at least 30 minutes between cardio and weightlifting to give your muscles time to recover.

Q: Can I do weightlifting and cardio on different days?

A: Yes, you can. Just make sure to schedule your workouts in a way that allows for proper rest and recovery.

Q: Is it better to do cardio and weightlifting on the same day or on different days?

A: It depends on your schedule and fitness goals. If you're short on time, doing both on the same day can be effective. If you have more time and want to focus on one area at a time, splitting them up can be a good idea.

Conclusion

In the end, the decision of whether to lift weights after cardio or before depends on your personal preferences and fitness goals. If you're looking to build muscle, lifting weights before cardio may be the way to go. If you're looking to improve your cardiovascular fitness, doing cardio before weightlifting may be more effective. Whichever approach you choose, make sure to warm up properly, schedule your workouts wisely, and listen to your body to avoid injury.
