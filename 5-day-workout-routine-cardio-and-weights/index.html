5 Day Workout Routine Cardio And Weights A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Eveline Nov 03, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for a way to take your fitness routine to the next level? Incorporating both cardio and weight training into your workout regimen can lead to significant improvements in strength, endurance, and overall health. In this guide, we will explore a 5 day workout routine that includes both cardio and weights.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance between cardio and weight training. Some focus solely on cardio in an attempt to lose weight, while others prioritize weight lifting to build muscle. However, a well-rounded fitness routine should include both types of exercise. Cardio workouts help to improve cardiovascular health, burn calories, and increase endurance, while weight training builds strength and muscle mass.

What is the Target of 5 Day Workout Routine Cardio and Weights?

The target of a 5 day workout routine that includes both cardio and weights is to provide a comprehensive approach to fitness. The routine is designed to challenge your body in various ways while allowing for adequate rest and recovery time. By alternating between cardio and weight training, you can ensure that you are targeting different muscle groups and giving your body the variety it needs to avoid hitting a plateau.

Here is an example of a 5 day workout routine that includes both cardio and weights:

Day 1: Upper Body and Cardio

Warm-up: 10 minutes on the rowing machine

  • Bench press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Seated row (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Shoulder press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Bicep curls (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Tricep dips (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Cardio: 20 minutes on the elliptical

For the upper body exercises, choose a weight that challenges you but allows you to complete all reps with proper form. Rest for 60 seconds between sets.

Day 2: Lower Body and Cardio

Warm-up: 10 minutes on the stationary bike

  • Squats (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Deadlifts (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Lunges (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Calf raises (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Leg press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Cardio: 20 minutes on the stair climber

For the lower body exercises, choose a weight that challenges you but allows you to complete all reps with proper form. Rest for 60 seconds between sets.

Day 3: Rest Day

Rest and recover. You can also use this day for stretching or yoga.

Day 4: Full Body and Cardio

Warm-up: 10 minutes on the rowing machine

  • Deadlifts (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Bench press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Squats (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Shoulder press (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Bicep curls (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Tricep dips (3 sets of 10 reps)
  • Cardio: 20 minutes on the elliptical

For the full body exercises, choose a weight that challenges you but allows you to complete all reps with proper form. Rest for 60 seconds between sets.

Day 5: HIIT and Abs

Warm-up: 5 minutes of jump rope

  • High-intensity interval training (HIIT): 20 seconds of all-out effort followed by 10 seconds of rest for 10 rounds
  • Plank (3 sets of 30 seconds)
  • Crunches (3 sets of 20 reps)
  • Russian twists (3 sets of 20 reps)
  • Leg raises (3 sets of 10 reps)

For the HIIT workout, choose exercises that you enjoy and that challenge you. You can use equipment like a stationary bike or treadmill, or opt for bodyweight exercises like burpees or jumping jacks.

Benefits of 5 Day Workout Routine Cardio and Weights

By following a 5 day workout routine that incorporates both cardio and weights, you can reap numerous benefits. Some of the benefits include:

  • Increased muscle strength and mass
  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Better endurance and stamina
  • Increased calorie burn
  • Reduced risk of chronic diseases like heart disease and diabetes

Personal Experience with 5 Day Workout Routine Cardio and Weights

As a fitness enthusiast, I have tried many different workout routines over the years. However, I have found that a 5 day workout routine that includes both cardio and weights is the most effective for me. Not only does it challenge me physically, but it also keeps me from getting bored with my workouts. I have noticed significant improvements in my strength, endurance, and overall health since incorporating this routine into my fitness regimen.

Tips for Success with 5 Day Workout Routine Cardio and Weights

Here are some tips to help you get the most out of your 5 day workout routine that includes both cardio and weights:

  • Stay hydrated by drinking plenty of water throughout the day
  • Eat a balanced diet that includes plenty of protein, complex carbs, and healthy fats to fuel your workouts
  • Get enough sleep to allow for proper recovery and repair of your muscles
  • Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed to avoid injury or burnout

Question and Answer

Q: Can I switch up the exercises in my 5 day workout routine?

A: Yes! It's important to vary your exercises to prevent boredom and ensure that you are targeting all of your muscle groups. Just make sure to still include both cardio and weight training.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Rest for 60 seconds between sets to allow your muscles to recover and prepare for the next set. However, if you feel like you need more or less rest, listen to your body and adjust accordingly.

Q: Do I need to do the exercises in a specific order?

A: No, you can switch up the order of the exercises to keep things interesting. Just make sure to include all of the exercises listed.

Q: Can I do this routine at home?

A: Absolutely! Many of the exercises can be done with just a set of dumbbells or bodyweight. However, if you prefer to use gym equipment, you can modify the routine accordingly.

Conclusion of 5 Day Workout Routine Cardio and Weights

Incorporating both cardio and weight training into your workout routine can lead to significant improvements in strength, endurance, and overall health. By following a 5 day workout routine that includes both types of exercise, you can challenge your body in various ways while allowing for adequate rest and recovery time. Remember to stay hydrated, eat a balanced diet, and listen to your body to get the most out of your workouts.
