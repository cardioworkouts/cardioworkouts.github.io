Can I Do Hiit And Weight Training On The Same Day

Written by Luffy Sep 23, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you someone who enjoys both HIIT and weight training but is unsure if it's safe or effective to do both on the same day? If so, you're not alone. Many people wonder if it's possible to combine these two forms of exercise without compromising their results or risking injury.

Combining HIIT and weight training is a popular choice for people who want to improve their overall fitness level. HIIT helps burn calories quickly, while weight training builds muscle and strengthens bones. However, doing both forms of exercise on the same day can be challenging, especially if you're new to working out or have limited time.

The short answer to whether you can do HIIT and weight training on the same day is yes. However, it's essential to understand the best practices for combining these two types of exercise to avoid injury and achieve your fitness goals.

My Personal Experience

As someone who's been doing both HIIT and weight training for years, I can attest that it's possible to combine them in a single workout session. I typically do a 30-minute HIIT workout followed by 45 minutes of weight training four times a week. However, I had to gradually build up my endurance and strength to reach this level.

The Benefits of Combining HIIT and Weight Training

There are several benefits to combining HIIT and weight training, including:

  • Burns calories quickly
  • Builds muscle and strength
  • Boosts metabolism
  • Improves cardiovascular health
  • Increases endurance

How to Combine HIIT and Weight Training

Here are some tips to help you combine HIIT and weight training effectively:

  • Start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts
  • Don't do HIIT and weight training on consecutive days to allow your muscles to rest and recover
  • Warm-up before and stretch after your workout to prevent injury
  • Choose exercises that work different muscle groups to avoid overworking specific areas
  • Listen to your body and adjust your workout intensity as needed

Example HIIT and Weight Training Workout

Here's an example of how you can combine HIIT and weight training in a single workout session:

  • Warm-up for 5-10 minutes by doing some light cardio or stretching
  • Do a 10-minute HIIT workout (e.g., jumping jacks, burpees, mountain climbers, high knees)
  • Rest for 2-3 minutes
  • Do 3 sets of 10 reps of weight training exercises (e.g., squats, lunges, bench press, bicep curls, tricep dips)
  • Rest for 30 seconds between sets
  • Repeat steps 2-5 for a total of 45-60 minutes
  • Cool down for 5-10 minutes by doing some light stretching

Question and Answer

Q: Is it safe to do HIIT and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, it's safe to do HIIT and weight training on the same day as long as you follow proper form and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts.

Q: How often should I do HIIT and weight training in a week?

A: It's recommended to do HIIT and weight training 2-3 times a week with at least 24 hours of rest between sessions.

Q: Can I do HIIT and weight training in the same workout session?

A: Yes, you can do HIIT and weight training in the same workout session. However, it's recommended to do HIIT before weight training to avoid fatigue and injury.

Q: What are some good exercises for combining HIIT and weight training?

A: Some good exercises for combining HIIT and weight training include squats, lunges, bench press, bicep curls, tricep dips, jumping jacks, burpees, mountain climbers, and high knees.

Conclusion

Combining HIIT and weight training can be an effective way to improve your overall fitness level. However, it's essential to follow proper form and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts to avoid injury and achieve your fitness goals. With the right approach, you can safely and effectively do HIIT and weight training on the same day to get the most out of your workouts.
