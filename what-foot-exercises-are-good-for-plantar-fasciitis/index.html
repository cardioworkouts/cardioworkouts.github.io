Effective Foot Exercises For Plantar Fasciitis Relief

Written by Aprilia Sep 02, 2023 Â· 2 min read

Plantar fasciitis is a common condition that causes pain and discomfort in the heel and foot. If you're experiencing this condition, you know how debilitating it can be. Fortunately, there are several exercises that can help alleviate the pain and discomfort associated with plantar fasciitis.

Plantar fasciitis can be caused by a variety of factors, including overuse, improper footwear, and tight calf muscles. Common symptoms include pain in the heel or arch of the foot, stiffness, and tenderness. If left untreated, the condition can worsen over time, making it difficult to walk or stand for long periods of time.

What Foot Exercises are Good for Plantar Fasciitis?

Stretching exercises are particularly effective for relieving the pain and discomfort associated with plantar fasciitis. These exercises help to loosen tight calf muscles and strengthen the plantar fascia, which is the connective tissue that runs along the bottom of the foot.

One effective exercise is the calf stretch. To perform this exercise, stand facing a wall with your hands on the wall at shoulder height. Place one foot behind the other and press the heel of the back foot into the ground. Hold the stretch for 30 seconds and then switch feet.

Another effective exercise is the toe stretch. Sit in a chair with your feet flat on the ground. Lift your toes off the ground, keeping your heel firmly planted. Hold the stretch for 10 seconds and then release. Repeat the exercise 10 times.
