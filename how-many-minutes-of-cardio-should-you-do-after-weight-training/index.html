How Many Minutes Of Cardio Should You Do After Weight Training

Written by Robby Sep 16, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who hits the gym regularly? Do you often find yourself wondering how much cardio you should do after weight training? If yes, then this post is for you. Many gym-goers struggle with figuring out the perfect balance between weight training and cardio. While weight training is essential for building muscle, cardio is necessary for maintaining overall health and burning calories. But, how much cardio is too much or too little?

Figuring out the right amount of cardio after weight training can be a pain point for many gym enthusiasts. It is crucial to understand your body's needs and tailor your workout accordingly. In this post, we will discuss how many minutes of cardio should you do after weight training and related keywords.

The answer to how many minutes of cardio should you do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to build muscle mass, it is recommended to keep your cardio sessions short and sweet. A 10-15 minute cardio session after weight training is sufficient to get your heart rate up and burn a few extra calories. However, if your goal is to lose weight, you might want to increase your cardio sessions' duration to 30-45 minutes.

In summary, the amount of cardio you should do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. For muscle gain, stick to a short 10-15 minute cardio session, and for weight loss, increase it to 30-45 minutes.

My Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I have experimented with different cardio durations after weight training. Initially, I used to do 30-45 minutes of cardio after weight training, thinking it would help me burn more calories. However, I noticed that it took a toll on my energy levels, and my strength training suffered. After some research, I learned that a shorter cardio session after weight training is more effective for muscle gain. I now stick to a 10-15 minute cardio session and feel more energized during my strength training sessions.

Benefits of Short Cardio Sessions

Short cardio sessions after weight training have several benefits. Firstly, it helps maintain a balance between weight training and cardio, allowing you to focus on building muscle and burning calories simultaneously. Secondly, it improves cardiovascular health, reduces the risk of heart diseases, and boosts endurance. Lastly, a short cardio session after weight training helps increase metabolism and burn calories even after your workout.

Tips to Optimize Cardio

Now that you know how many minutes of cardio you should do after weight training, here are some tips to optimize your cardio session:

- Pick a cardio exercise you enjoy doing

- Start slow and increase intensity gradually

- Incorporate high-intensity interval training (HIIT) for maximum calorie burn

- Stay hydrated before, during, and after your workout

- Fuel your body with a protein-rich snack post-workout

HIIT vs. Steady-State Cardio

When it comes to cardio after weight training, there are two types of cardio exercises- HIIT and steady-state cardio. HIIT involves high-intensity exercises for short intervals, followed by periods of rest. On the other hand, steady-state cardio involves maintaining a steady pace throughout the workout. While both types of cardio are effective, HIIT is more efficient in burning calories and reducing fat. However, it is essential to consult a trainer before incorporating HIIT into your workout routine.

FAQs

Q. How long should I wait to do cardio after weight training?

A. It is recommended to wait at least 30 minutes after weight training to do cardio.

Q. Is it okay to do cardio before weight training?

A. Yes, it is okay to do cardio before weight training. However, it is recommended to keep the cardio session short to avoid fatigue during strength training.

Q. How many days a week should I do cardio?

A. It is recommended to do cardio at least 3-4 days a week for overall health and fitness.

Q. Should I do cardio on rest days?

A. It is okay to do light cardio on rest days. However, it is recommended to give your body enough rest to recover from the previous workouts.

Conclusion

In conclusion, how many minutes of cardio should you do after weight training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to build muscle, stick to a short and sweet cardio session, and for weight loss, increase it to 30-45 minutes. It is essential to listen to your body and tailor the workout accordingly. Remember to stay hydrated, fuel your body with the right nutrients, and consult a trainer before making any significant changes to your workout routine.
