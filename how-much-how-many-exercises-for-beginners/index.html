How Much How Many Exercises For Beginners A Complete Guide

Written by Frank Aug 03, 2023 Â· 4 min read

As a beginner, starting a fitness routine can be overwhelming. One common question that beginners ask is, "How much how many exercises should I do?" This guide will help you answer that question and provide you with tips to get started on your fitness journey.

Pain Points

As a beginner, it can be difficult to know where to start with exercise. You may be unsure of how much exercise you should be doing, how many exercises you should be doing, and how often you should be exercising. Additionally, you may be concerned about injury or not knowing how to perform exercises correctly.

Answering the Target

The amount of exercise a beginner should do can vary depending on their goals and fitness level. However, a general guideline is to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week. This can be broken down into 30 minutes of exercise, five days a week. As for the number of exercises, it's recommended to start with a full-body workout that includes 8-10 exercises, performed for 10-15 reps each.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, as a beginner, it's important to focus on getting at least 150 minutes of exercise per week, which can be broken down into 30 minutes of exercise, five days a week. Starting with a full-body workout that includes 8-10 exercises, performed for 10-15 reps each, is recommended. It's also important to take precautions to prevent injury and learn proper form for each exercise.

How Much How Many Exercises for Beginners: Explained

When I first started my fitness journey, I had no idea how much exercise I should be doing or how many exercises I should be performing. I was overwhelmed and didn't know where to start. However, I found that starting with a full-body workout that included a variety of exercises helped me feel more confident and comfortable in the gym.

It's important to note that the amount of exercise you should be doing may vary depending on your goals and fitness level. Starting with 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week and 8-10 exercises in a full-body workout is a good starting point for most beginners. However, it's important to listen to your body and adjust as needed.

Preventing Injury

One concern that many beginners have is the risk of injury. To prevent injury, it's important to start with low-impact exercises and gradually increase intensity. Additionally, learning proper form for each exercise is crucial to prevent injury and maximize the effectiveness of the exercise.

Importance of Rest Days

Rest days are just as important as exercise days. It's recommended to have at least one rest day per week to allow your body to recover and prevent injury. On rest days, you can still engage in light activity such as stretching, yoga, or a leisurely walk.

How to Make Exercise a Habit

One of the biggest challenges for beginners is making exercise a habit. To make exercise a habit, it's important to find an activity that you enjoy and make it a part of your daily routine. Setting realistic goals and tracking progress can also help keep you motivated and accountable.

Tips for Staying Motivated

Staying motivated can be difficult, especially when you don't see results right away. To stay motivated, try finding a workout buddy or joining a fitness class. Rewarding yourself for reaching milestones can also help keep you motivated and on track.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I exercise as a beginner?

A: It's recommended to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity exercise per week, broken down into 30 minutes of exercise, five days a week.

Q: How many exercises should I perform in a full-body workout?

A: It's recommended to perform 8-10 exercises in a full-body workout.

Q: How can I prevent injury as a beginner?

A: Starting with low-impact exercises, gradually increasing intensity, and learning proper form for each exercise can help prevent injury.

Q: How can I make exercise a habit?

A: Finding an activity you enjoy, setting realistic goals, tracking progress, and making exercise a part of your daily routine can help make exercise a habit.

Conclusion of How Much How Many Exercises for Beginners

Starting a fitness routine as a beginner can be challenging, but by following the guidelines of at least 150 minutes of exercise per week and starting with a full-body workout that includes 8-10 exercises, performed for 10-15 reps each, you can set yourself up for success. Remember to listen to your body, prevent injury, and make exercise a habit by finding an activity you enjoy and making it part of your daily routine.
