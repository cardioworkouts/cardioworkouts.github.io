Does Too Much Cardio Make You Gain Weight The Truth Revealed

Written by Luffy Jul 15, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who spends hours doing cardio exercises in the hopes of losing weight, but instead, you seem to be gaining weight? You're not alone. This is a common frustration for many people who are trying to lose weight. In this article, we will discuss the truth about whether too much cardio can actually make you gain weight.

When it comes to losing weight, many people believe that cardio is the most effective method. However, doing too much cardio can actually be counterproductive. Instead of burning fat, your body can start to burn muscle, which can lead to weight gain. Additionally, too much cardio can increase the production of cortisol, a stress hormone that can cause your body to store fat.

So, does too much cardio make you gain weight? The answer is yes, but only if you're not doing it correctly. To avoid gaining weight from cardio, it's important to strike a balance between cardio and strength training. Strength training helps to build muscle, which can increase your metabolism and promote fat loss. Additionally, you should pay attention to your diet, as consuming too many calories can also lead to weight gain.

To summarize, here are the main points to keep in mind:

  • Too much cardio can cause your body to burn muscle instead of fat
  • Too much cardio can increase the production of cortisol, a stress hormone that can cause your body to store fat
  • Balance cardio with strength training and pay attention to your diet to avoid gaining weight

My Personal Experience with Cardio and Weight Gain

As someone who used to do hours of cardio every day, I can attest to the fact that too much cardio can indeed lead to weight gain. Despite my efforts to lose weight through cardio, I found that I was actually gaining weight and losing muscle. It wasn't until I incorporated strength training into my routine that I started to see real results. Not only did I start to lose fat, but I also gained muscle and felt stronger overall.

The Science Behind Cardio and Weight Gain

When you do cardio, your body burns calories for energy. However, if you do too much cardio, your body can start to burn muscle for energy instead of fat. This is because your body needs glucose to fuel your workouts, and if you don't have enough glucose in your bloodstream, your body will break down muscle tissue to get it. Additionally, too much cardio can increase the production of cortisol, which can cause your body to store fat.

How Much Cardio Should You Do?

The amount of cardio you should do depends on your goals and fitness level. However, as a general rule, you should aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week. This can include activities such as brisk walking, cycling, or dancing. Additionally, you should incorporate strength training into your routine to build muscle and boost your metabolism.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Strength Training

If you're new to strength training, it can be intimidating to incorporate it into your routine. Here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Start with bodyweight exercises such as push-ups, squats, and lunges
  • Use free weights or weight machines to gradually increase the resistance
  • Focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once
  • Take rest days to allow your muscles time to recover and grow

FAQs About Cardio and Weight Gain

Q: Can cardio alone help me lose weight?

A: While cardio can help you burn calories and lose weight, it's important to incorporate strength training into your routine to build muscle and boost your metabolism.

Q: How much cardio should I do per week?

A: The amount of cardio you should do depends on your goals and fitness level. However, as a general rule, aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week.

Q: Can too much cardio make me gain weight?

A: Yes, too much cardio can cause your body to burn muscle instead of fat and increase the production of cortisol, a stress hormone that can cause your body to store fat.

Q: How can I avoid gaining weight from cardio?

A: To avoid gaining weight from cardio, balance it with strength training and pay attention to your diet to avoid consuming too many calories.

Conclusion

In conclusion, doing too much cardio can indeed lead to weight gain if you're not doing it correctly. To avoid this, it's important to balance cardio with strength training and pay attention to your diet. By incorporating these tips into your routine, you can achieve your weight loss goals while still maintaining a healthy, strong body.
