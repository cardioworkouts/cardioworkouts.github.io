New Beginner Gym Workout A Complete Guide

Written by Aprilia Jul 06, 2023 · 5 min read

Starting a new exercise routine can be daunting, especially if you’re new to the gym. With so many machines and equipment, it’s easy to feel overwhelmed and unsure where to start. In this guide, we’ll walk you through a new beginner gym workout routine that’s perfect for anyone just starting out.

The pain points around starting a new gym routine are real. It’s easy to feel self-conscious, unsure of proper form and technique, and intimidated by other gym-goers who seem to know what they’re doing. But it’s important to remember that everyone has to start somewhere, and with a little guidance and patience, you can achieve your fitness goals.

The target of this new beginner gym workout is to help you build a solid foundation of strength and endurance. We’ll focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once, helping you gain functional strength that will translate to everyday activities. We’ll also include cardio to help improve your cardiovascular health and burn calories.

In summary, this guide will provide a complete new beginner gym workout routine that focuses on compound exercises and cardio to help you build strength, endurance, and improve your overall fitness level.

Upper Body Workout

When I first started going to the gym, I was intimidated by the weight room and unsure where to start. But after a few sessions with a personal trainer, I learned the importance of compound exercises and how they can help build functional strength.

For this upper body workout, we’ll focus on three compound exercises: the bench press, the lat pulldown, and the shoulder press. These exercises work multiple muscle groups at once, helping you build overall upper body strength.

The bench press targets your chest, shoulders, and triceps, while the lat pulldown targets your back and biceps. The shoulder press targets your shoulders, triceps, and upper back. We’ll perform each exercise for three sets of 10-12 reps.

Lower Body Workout

Building strong legs and glutes is essential for overall strength and balance. For this lower body workout, we’ll focus on three compound exercises: the squat, the deadlift, and the lunge.

When I first started doing squats, I struggled with proper form and experienced some knee pain. But with the help of a personal trainer, I learned the importance of proper form and technique, and how it can help prevent injury and maximize results.

The squat targets your quads, hamstrings, and glutes, while the deadlift targets your hamstrings and glutes. The lunge targets your quads, hamstrings, and glutes. We’ll perform each exercise for three sets of 10-12 reps.

Cardio

Cardiovascular exercise is important for overall health and fitness. For this new beginner gym workout, we’ll incorporate 20-30 minutes of cardio after each strength training session.

Whether you choose to use the treadmill, elliptical, or stationary bike, it’s important to find an activity that you enjoy and will stick with. Consistency is key when it comes to achieving your fitness goals.

Nutrition

Eating a balanced diet is essential for fueling your workouts and aiding in recovery. Focus on consuming lean protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats. Aim to eat a variety of fruits and vegetables to ensure you’re getting a wide range of nutrients.

It’s also important to stay hydrated throughout the day, especially when exercising. Aim to drink at least 8-10 glasses of water per day.

Final Thoughts

Starting a new beginner gym workout routine can be intimidating, but with the right guidance and patience, you can achieve your fitness goals. Remember to focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups, incorporate cardio for overall health, and fuel your body with a balanced diet. And most importantly, don’t be afraid to ask for help or guidance from a personal trainer or gym staff.

Question and Answer

Q: How many times a week should I do this new beginner gym workout?

A: Aim to do this workout routine 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between each session.

Q: Can I modify this workout if I have any injuries or limitations?

A: Yes, it’s important to listen to your body and make modifications as needed. Consult with a personal trainer or physical therapist if you have any concerns.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: Aim to rest for 1-2 minutes between sets to allow your muscles time to recover.

Q: Can I do cardio on the same day as strength training?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on the same day as strength training. Just be sure to give your muscles time to recover and replenish with proper nutrition and hydration.

Conclusion

This new beginner gym workout routine is perfect for anyone just starting out at the gym. By focusing on compound exercises, incorporating cardio, and fueling your body with a balanced diet, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being. Remember to listen to your body and make modifications as needed, and don’t be afraid to ask for help or guidance from a personal trainer or gym staff.
