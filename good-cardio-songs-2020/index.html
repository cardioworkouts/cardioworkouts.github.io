Good Cardio Songs 2020 The Ultimate Playlist To Get Your Heart Pumping

Written by Bardi Oct 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of slogging through your workouts? Do you find yourself losing motivation halfway through your cardio routine? The solution might be as simple as switching up your playlist. Good cardio songs 2020 are the perfect way to inject some energy into your workouts and help you power through to the end.

Music is a powerful motivator when it comes to exercise. It can distract you from fatigue, boost your mood, and even increase your endurance. However, not all songs are created equal when it comes to getting your heart rate up. That's why we've put together the ultimate playlist of good cardio songs 2020, designed to keep you moving and grooving.

So, what makes a song a good cardio song? First and foremost, it needs to have a fast tempo. Generally, songs with a tempo of 120-140 beats per minute (BPM) are ideal for cardio workouts. These songs match the natural pace of your body while exercising, making it easier to keep up the momentum.

The Top Good Cardio Songs 2020

One of the best things about good cardio songs 2020 is that there's something for everyone. Whether you're into pop, hip-hop, or rock, there's a song out there that will get your heart pumping. Here are some of our top picks:

1. "Blinding Lights" by The Weeknd

This infectious pop hit has a BPM of 171, making it perfect for high-intensity cardio workouts. The driving beat and catchy chorus will keep you motivated throughout your routine.

2. "Dynamite" by BTS

This K-pop sensation is a fan favorite for a reason. With a BPM of 114, it's perfect for lower-intensity cardio workouts like jogging or cycling. Plus, the upbeat melody and positive lyrics are sure to put a smile on your face.

3. "Don't Start Now" by Dua Lipa

This disco-inspired hit has a BPM of 123, making it perfect for dance workouts or high-intensity cardio intervals. The infectious chorus and funky bassline will keep you moving throughout your routine.

4. "Lose You To Love Me" by Selena Gomez

This emotional ballad might not seem like an obvious choice for a cardio workout, but with a BPM of 108, it's perfect for lower-intensity routines like yoga or stretching. The soaring vocals and poignant lyrics will help you connect with your body and stay focused.

Why Good Cardio Songs 2020 Are Important

Good cardio songs 2020 are more than just a fun distraction during your workout. They can actually improve your performance and help you achieve your fitness goals. Studies have shown that listening to music while exercising can:

- Increase endurance

- Reduce perceived exertion (i.e. make your workout feel easier)

- Improve mood and motivation

- Increase overall enjoyment of the workout

When you're enjoying your workout and feeling motivated, you're more likely to stick with it long-term. That's why good cardio songs 2020 are such an important tool for anyone looking to improve their fitness routine.

How to Build the Perfect Good Cardio Songs 2020 Playlist

Building the perfect playlist is easier than you might think. Here are some tips to get you started:

- Choose songs with a BPM of 120-140 for high-intensity cardio workouts, and 100-120 for lower-intensity workouts.

- Look for songs with a driving beat and catchy chorus to keep you motivated.

- Mix up genres and artists to keep things interesting.

- Consider adding some slower, more emotional songs for cooldowns or stretching.

How to Use Good Cardio Songs 2020 During Your Workout

Once you've built your playlist, it's time to put it to use. Here are some tips for incorporating good cardio songs 2020 into your workout:

- Start with a warm-up song to get you in the mood and ease into your routine.

- Use upbeat songs for high-intensity intervals, and slower songs for recovery periods.

- Save your favorite song for the end of your workout as a reward for a job well done.

- Don't be afraid to dance or sing along! The more fun you have, the more motivated you'll be.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I listen to any type of music during my cardio workout?

A: While you can technically listen to any type of music during your workout, good cardio songs 2020 are specifically chosen to match the natural pace of your body while exercising. This can help improve your performance and make your workout feel easier.

Q: How many songs should I include in my cardio playlist?

A: This depends on the length of your workout and your personal preferences. However, a good rule of thumb is to aim for at least 30 minutes of music to match the average length of a cardio workout.

Q: Do I need to use headphones while listening to my cardio playlist?

A: While headphones can help you stay focused and block out distractions, they aren't necessary. If you prefer to listen to your music without headphones, just be mindful of those around you and keep the volume at a reasonable level.

Q: Can I use the same playlist for every cardio workout?

A: While you certainly can use the same playlist every time, mixing things up can help keep your workouts interesting and prevent boredom. Consider switching up your playlist every few weeks to keep things fresh.

Conclusion of Good Cardio Songs 2020

Good cardio songs 2020 are a powerful tool for anyone looking to improve their fitness routine. By choosing songs with a fast tempo and catchy chorus, you can improve your performance, reduce perceived exertion, and stay motivated throughout your workout. So, put together the ultimate playlist, lace up your sneakers, and let the music carry you through to the end.
