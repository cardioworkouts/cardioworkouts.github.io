How To Mix Cardio Into Weight Training

Written by Daniel Oct 08, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of doing endless hours of cardio on the treadmill? Do you want to maximize your time at the gym by incorporating both weight training and cardio into your workout routine? If so, you're in the right place! In this tutorial, we'll show you how to mix cardio into weight training for a more effective and efficient workout.

When it comes to working out, many people struggle with finding the right balance between weight training and cardio. Some people focus solely on weight training, while others spend hours on the treadmill without touching a weight. However, it's important to incorporate both types of exercise into your routine in order to see the best results.

The key to mixing cardio into weight training is to find a balance between the two. You don't want to do too much cardio and neglect your weight training, but you also don't want to do too much weight training and neglect your cardio. The ideal balance will vary depending on your individual goals and fitness level.

To mix cardio into your weight training, you can try a variety of different methods. One popular method is to do a circuit-style workout, where you alternate between weight training exercises and cardio exercises. For example, you could do a set of squats followed by a minute of jumping jacks, then a set of lunges followed by a minute of jump rope, and so on. This will allow you to get the benefits of both weight training and cardio in one workout.

Personal Experience with Mixing Cardio into Weight Training

Personally, I found that mixing cardio into my weight training has been a game-changer for my fitness routine. I used to dread doing cardio and would often skip it altogether. However, by incorporating it into my weight training, I've been able to see better results in terms of both strength and endurance. Plus, it keeps my workouts interesting and challenging.

Tips for Mixing Cardio into Weight Training

If you're new to mixing cardio into your weight training, here are some tips to help you get started:

1. Start Slow

Don't try to do too much too soon. Start with just a few minutes of cardio in between your weight training exercises and gradually increase the amount over time.

2. Mix Up Your Cardio

Don't just stick to one type of cardio. Mix it up with jumping jacks, jump rope, running, biking, or any other type of cardio exercise you enjoy.

3. Don't Neglect Your Weights

Make sure you're still incorporating weight training into your routine. Don't let the cardio take over.

4. Listen to Your Body

Pay attention to how your body feels. If you're feeling fatigued or sore, take a break and rest. Don't push yourself too hard.

FAQs

Q: How much cardio should I do with my weight training?

A: The amount of cardio you should do will depend on your individual goals and fitness level. A good starting point is to aim for 20-30 minutes of cardio per workout.

Q: What are some good cardio exercises to mix with weight training?

A: Some good cardio exercises to mix with weight training include jumping jacks, jump rope, running, biking, rowing, and stair climbing.

Q: Can I do cardio on the same day as weight training?

A: Yes, you can do cardio on the same day as weight training. Just make sure to give yourself enough time to rest between the two types of exercise.

Q: Can I mix cardio into all of my weight training exercises?

A: It's possible to mix cardio into most weight training exercises, but it may not be necessary or beneficial for all of them. Use your best judgment and listen to your body.

Conclusion of How to Mix Cardio into Weight Training

By mixing cardio into your weight training, you can maximize your time at the gym and see better results in terms of both strength and endurance. Just remember to start slow, mix up your cardio, don't neglect your weights, and listen to your body. With these tips in mind, you'll be able to create a balanced and effective workout routine that incorporates both weight training and cardio.
