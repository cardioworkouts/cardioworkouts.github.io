Should You Lift Weights Or Do Cardio First To Lose Weight

Written by Bardi Nov 01, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you trying to lose weight but unsure whether to start with lifting weights or doing cardio? It can be a confusing decision as both exercises have their benefits towards weight loss. In this article, we will explore the answer to the question, "should you lift weights or do cardio first to lose weight?"

Pain Points Related to Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First to Lose Weight

One of the biggest pain points related to deciding between lifting weights and doing cardio for weight loss is the lack of clarity. There seems to be conflicting advice out there, and it can be hard to know what to do. Additionally, some people may not enjoy one type of exercise and find it challenging to stick to a routine that includes it.

Answer: Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First to Lose Weight?

The answer to the question depends on your fitness goals. If your primary goal is to lose weight, then doing cardio first may be more beneficial. Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, or swimming are great for burning calories and losing weight. However, if you want to build muscle and tone your body, lifting weights first may be more effective.

Summary of Main Points

To summarize, the answer to whether you should lift weights or do cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, cardio may be more effective. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle, lifting weights may be the way to go.

Starting with Cardio First

Personally, I prefer starting with cardio first when trying to lose weight. I find that it helps me get my heart rate up and burn more calories. Additionally, it helps me get into the right mindset for the rest of my workout. I usually do around 20-30 minutes of cardio before moving on to lifting weights.

Starting with Weightlifting First

However, some people prefer to start with weightlifting first. This is because lifting weights can help build muscle, which in turn can help burn more calories throughout the day. Additionally, weightlifting can help improve overall strength and endurance, making cardio exercises easier in the long run.

Why Weightlifting is Beneficial for Weight Loss

Weightlifting is beneficial for weight loss because it helps build muscle. Muscle requires more energy to maintain than fat, which means that the more muscle you have, the more calories you will burn throughout the day. Additionally, weightlifting can help improve your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories even when you are not exercising.

How to Incorporate Both into Your Workout

If you want to incorporate both weightlifting and cardio into your workout, there are several ways to do so. You can alternate between the two, doing cardio one day and weightlifting the next. Alternatively, you can do both in the same workout, starting with either cardio or weightlifting and then switching to the other.

Personal Experience: What Works for Me

Personally, I prefer to alternate between cardio and weightlifting days. This helps me stay motivated and not get bored with my workouts. On cardio days, I usually do around 30 minutes of running or cycling. On weightlifting days, I focus on different muscle groups and try to challenge myself with heavier weights or more reps.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Is it better to lift weights or do cardio for weight loss?
A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, cardio may be more effective. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle, lifting weights may be the way to go.

Q: How often should I do cardio or weightlifting?
A: It is recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio per week, along with two or more days of weightlifting.

Q: Can I do both cardio and weightlifting in the same workout?
A: Yes, you can. However, it is important to start with either cardio or weightlifting and then switch to the other, as doing both at the same time can lead to overtraining and injury.

Q: Do I need to lift heavy weights to see results?
A: No, you do not. Lifting lighter weights with more reps can also be effective for building muscle and losing weight.

Conclusion of Should You Lift Weights or Do Cardio First to Lose Weight

Deciding whether to lift weights or do cardio first to lose weight depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, cardio may be more effective, while lifting weights can help build muscle and tone your body. The best approach is to find a routine that works for you and stick to it consistently.
