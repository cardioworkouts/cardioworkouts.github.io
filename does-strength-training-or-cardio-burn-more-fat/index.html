Does Strength Training Or Cardio Burn More Fat

Written by Petter Aug 13, 2023 · 4 min read

Looking to lose weight and get fit? One of the most commonly asked questions is whether strength training or cardio burns more fat. It's a pain point for many people who are trying to lose weight, but don't know which type of exercise to focus on.

The answer to this question isn't as simple as you might think. Both strength training and cardio can help you burn fat, but they do it in different ways.

Strength training is a type of exercise that involves using weights or resistance to build muscle. Cardio, on the other hand, is any type of exercise that gets your heart rate up, such as running, cycling, or swimming.

So, which one burns more fat? The truth is, both types of exercise can help you lose weight and burn fat, but they do it in different ways.

My Personal Experience with Strength Training and Cardio

Personally, I have found that a combination of strength training and cardio has been the most effective way for me to lose weight and burn fat. When I first started my fitness journey, I focused primarily on cardio. I would spend hours on the treadmill, but I didn't see the results I was hoping for.

It wasn't until I started incorporating strength training into my routine that I began to see real changes in my body. I started lifting weights and doing bodyweight exercises, and I noticed that my body was becoming more toned and defined. Plus, I was burning more calories even when I wasn't working out.

The Benefits of Strength Training for Fat Loss

Strength training is incredibly effective for burning fat because it helps you build muscle. The more muscle you have, the more calories your body burns at rest. This means that even when you're not working out, your body is still burning calories.

Additionally, strength training can help you burn more calories during your workouts. When you lift weights or do bodyweight exercises, your body is working harder to move the weight, which means you burn more calories than you would during a cardio workout.

The Benefits of Cardio for Fat Loss

While strength training is great for building muscle and burning calories, cardio is still an important part of any weight loss program. Cardio helps you burn calories during your workout, and it can also help you improve your cardiovascular health.

Cardio exercises like running, cycling, and swimming are great for getting your heart rate up and improving your endurance. Plus, they're a great way to burn off stress and boost your mood.

How to Incorporate Strength Training and Cardio into Your Workout Routine

If you're looking to lose weight and burn fat, it's important to incorporate both strength training and cardio into your workout routine. Here are some tips for getting started:

  • Start with two to three days of strength training per week.
  • Incorporate bodyweight exercises like push-ups, squats, and lunges.
  • Gradually increase the weight you're lifting over time.
  • On the other days, do some form of cardio exercise.
  • Try running, cycling, swimming, or taking a dance class.
  • Gradually increase the intensity of your cardio workouts over time.

What's the Verdict?

So, does strength training or cardio burn more fat? The answer is both. Both types of exercise can help you lose weight and burn fat, but they do it in different ways. Strength training helps you build muscle, which boosts your metabolism and helps you burn more calories at rest. Cardio helps you burn calories during your workout and improve your cardiovascular health.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should I do cardio each day to burn fat?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals, but most experts recommend at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per day.

Q: Can I do strength training and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes! In fact, doing both types of exercise on the same day can be very effective for weight loss and fat burning.

Q: Should I focus on one type of exercise more than the other?

A: It's important to incorporate both types of exercise into your routine for the best results. However, if you're short on time, prioritize strength training over cardio.

Q: How quickly will I see results?

A: It depends on your fitness level and diet, but most people start to see results within a few weeks of starting a new exercise program.

Conclusion of Does Strength Training or Cardio Burn More Fat

So, there you have it. Both strength training and cardio can help you burn fat and lose weight, but they do it in different ways. The key is to incorporate both types of exercise into your workout routine and gradually increase the intensity over time. With a little patience and dedication, you can achieve your weight loss goals and feel great about your body.
