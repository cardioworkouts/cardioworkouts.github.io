Does It Matter What Time Of Day You Do Cardio

Written by William Jun 29, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you one of those people who has a hard time waking up early in the morning to exercise? Or do you prefer to hit the gym after work? Regardless of your preference, you might be wondering, does it matter what time of day you do cardio? The truth is, there are pros and cons to working out at different times of the day. In this article, we’ll explore the benefits and drawbacks of morning, afternoon, and evening cardio workouts and help you decide what works best for you.

For many people, finding the time to exercise is already a challenge. However, if you're trying to maximize your workout and achieve specific goals, the timing of your workout can be crucial. If you're an early bird, waking up before the sun rises to get your cardio in might be ideal for you. On the other hand, if you're a night owl, working out in the evening might be more your style. Some people even prefer to squeeze in a quick workout during their lunch break. Whatever your preference, it's important to understand how the timing of your workout can affect your performance.

The short answer is that it depends on your goals. If you're looking to lose weight, research suggests that morning workouts might be more effective. This is because working out on an empty stomach can help you burn fat more efficiently. Additionally, morning workouts can help boost your metabolism, which means you'll continue to burn calories throughout the day. On the other hand, if you're looking to build muscle, working out in the afternoon or evening might be better. This is because your body temperature is higher, which can help you lift heavier weights and increase your endurance.

In summary, there are pros and cons to working out at different times of the day. Morning workouts might be better for weight loss, while afternoon or evening workouts might be better for building muscle. Ultimately, the best time of day to work out is the time that works best for you and your schedule.

The Benefits of Morning Cardio Workouts

As someone who has struggled with keeping a consistent workout routine, I've found that morning workouts are the best for me. I'm not a morning person by nature, but I've found that getting up early to exercise helps me start my day on the right foot. Additionally, morning workouts help me feel more energized and focused throughout the day. I've also noticed that I'm more likely to stick to my workout routine when I exercise in the morning, as I'm less likely to get distracted by other things that come up during the day.

Research also suggests that morning cardio workouts might be more effective for weight loss. When you work out on an empty stomach, your body is forced to burn fat for fuel, which can help you shed those extra pounds. Additionally, morning workouts can help boost your metabolism, which means you'll continue to burn calories throughout the day.

The Benefits of Afternoon or Evening Cardio Workouts

While morning workouts might be better for weight loss, afternoon or evening workouts might be better for building muscle. This is because your body temperature is higher later in the day, which means you'll be able to lift heavier weights and increase your endurance. Additionally, working out in the afternoon or evening can be a great way to relieve stress after a long day at work. Many people find that exercise helps them unwind and clear their mind, which can help them sleep better at night.

Factors to Consider When Choosing the Best Time of Day to Work Out

When choosing the best time of day to work out, there are several factors to consider:

  • Your schedule: The best time of day to work out is the time that works best for you and your schedule. If you're a morning person, try to schedule your workout for the morning. If you're a night owl, try to schedule your workout for the evening.
  • Your goals: If you're looking to lose weight, morning workouts might be more effective. If you're looking to build muscle, afternoon or evening workouts might be better.
  • Your energy levels: Some people feel more energized in the morning, while others feel more energized in the afternoon or evening. Choose a time of day when you feel the most energized and focused.

Tips for Making the Most of Your Cardio Workout

No matter what time of day you choose to work out, there are several things you can do to make the most of your cardio workout:

  • Stay hydrated: Drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout to stay hydrated.
  • Eat a balanced diet: Fuel your body with healthy foods to give yourself the energy you need to power through your workout.
  • Warm up and cool down: Always warm up before your workout and cool down afterward to prevent injury.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to work out on an empty stomach?

A: Working out on an empty stomach can help you burn fat more efficiently, but it's important to fuel your body with healthy foods before and after your workout.

Q: How long should I wait to eat after a workout?

A: It's important to refuel your body with healthy foods within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout to help your muscles recover and rebuild.

Q: Can I work out every day?

A: It's important to give your body time to rest and recover. Aim to work out 3-4 times a week and give yourself at least one rest day in between workouts.

Q: Can I work out at night?

A: Yes, working out at night can be a great way to relieve stress and clear your mind before bed. Just make sure to give yourself enough time to wind down afterward so you can get a good night's sleep.

Conclusion of Does It Matter What Time of Day You Do Cardio

In conclusion, the best time of day to work out is the time that works best for you and your schedule. Morning workouts might be better for weight loss, while afternoon or evening workouts might be better for building muscle. Ultimately, the most important thing is to stay consistent and make exercise a regular part of your routine.
