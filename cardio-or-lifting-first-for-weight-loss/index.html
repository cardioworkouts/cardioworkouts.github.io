Cardio Or Lifting First For Weight Loss Which Is Better

Written by Christine Jul 14, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight and wondering which type of exercise is best for you - cardio or lifting? The truth is, both types of exercise can be effective for weight loss, but it depends on your goals and preferences. In this article, we'll explore the benefits of cardio and lifting for weight loss and help you make an informed decision on which one to prioritize.

Cardio First for Weight Loss: Target and Benefits

Cardiovascular exercise, or cardio for short, involves activities that increase your heart rate and breathing, such as running, cycling, or swimming. Cardio is often recommended for weight loss because it burns more calories in a shorter amount of time than lifting. For instance, a 30-minute run can burn up to 300 calories, while a 30-minute weight lifting session burns around 130-220 calories. Additionally, cardio can improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and boost your mood.

Personally, I prefer doing cardio first thing in the morning before breakfast. It helps me kickstart my metabolism and feel energized for the day ahead. I usually go for a jog around my neighborhood for 30 minutes, and it's a great way to clear my mind and get some fresh air.

Lifting First for Weight Loss: Target and Benefits

Weight lifting, or resistance training, involves using weights, machines, or your body weight to build muscle and strength. While lifting may not burn as many calories as cardio during the actual workout, it has numerous benefits for weight loss. For one, building muscle can increase your metabolism, which means you'll burn more calories at rest. Additionally, lifting can help you tone your body and prevent loss of muscle mass, which can occur during weight loss. Finally, lifting can improve your bone density and reduce your risk of osteoporosis.

Personally, I like to lift weights in the evening after work. It's a great way to unwind and relieve stress. I usually focus on compound exercises, such as squats, deadlifts, and bench presses, as they work multiple muscle groups at once and burn more calories overall.

Cardio or Lifting First: Which is Better?

So, should you do cardio or lifting first for weight loss? The answer is - it depends on your goals and preferences. If your main goal is to burn calories and improve your cardiovascular health, then doing cardio first may be the way to go. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle, increase your metabolism, and tone your body, then lifting first may be more effective. The best approach is to combine both types of exercise into your routine and find a balance that works for you.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Lifting for Weight Loss

If you want to maximize your weight loss results, here are some tips for combining cardio and lifting:

  • Do cardio and lifting on separate days or at different times of the day to avoid fatigue and injury
  • Incorporate high-intensity interval training (HIIT) into your cardio routine to burn more calories in less time
  • Focus on compound exercises during lifting, as they work multiple muscle groups and burn more calories overall
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts to avoid plateauing
  • Eat a balanced diet with enough protein to support muscle growth and repair

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio and lifting for weight loss?

A: It depends on your fitness level and schedule, but a good rule of thumb is to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate cardio or 75 minutes of vigorous cardio per week, and 2-3 full-body lifting sessions per week.

Q: Can I lose weight by just doing cardio or lifting?

A: Yes, you can lose weight by just doing cardio or lifting, but combining both types of exercise will yield better results in terms of weight loss, muscle tone, and overall health.

Q: Is it safe to do cardio and lifting if I have a medical condition?

A: It depends on the condition and severity. It's best to consult with your doctor or a certified trainer before starting a new exercise program, especially if you have a medical condition or are taking medication.

Q: Should I do cardio or lifting first if I'm short on time?

A: If you're short on time, prioritize the type of exercise that aligns with your goals. For instance, if you want to burn calories quickly, do cardio first. If you want to build strength and muscle, do lifting first.

Conclusion of Cardio or Lifting First for Weight Loss

In conclusion, both cardio and lifting can be effective for weight loss, but it depends on your goals and preferences. Cardio is great for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health, while lifting is great for building muscle, increasing metabolism, and toning your body. The best approach is to combine both types of exercise into your routine and find a balance that works for you. Remember to listen to your body, stay hydrated, and fuel yourself with a balanced diet.
