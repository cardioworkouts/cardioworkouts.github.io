Do Bodybuilders Do Cardio Before Or After Weights

Written by Eveline Aug 11, 2023 · 5 min read

Bodybuilding is a popular sport that requires a lot of dedication, hard work, and discipline. Many people who are trying to build muscle and strength wonder if they should do cardio before or after their weightlifting sessions. This question has been the subject of much debate among fitness enthusiasts, and there is no one-size-fits-all answer. In this blog post, we will explore whether bodybuilders should do cardio before or after weights.

Many bodybuilders struggle with balancing their cardio and weightlifting workouts. Some people believe that doing cardio before weights can help them warm up and increase their heart rate, while others think that doing cardio after weights is better because it can help them burn more calories and fat. The truth is that there is no right or wrong answer, as it depends on your individual goals and preferences.

Cardio and weightlifting are both important for building muscle and burning fat. Cardiovascular exercise can improve your heart health, increase your endurance, and help you burn more calories. Weightlifting, on the other hand, can help you build muscle, increase your strength, and boost your metabolism. Therefore, it is important to find a balance between the two, depending on your goals.

In general, it is recommended to do cardio after weights, especially if your primary goal is to build muscle and strength. This is because doing cardio before weights can deplete your energy and make it harder to lift heavy weights. However, if your main goal is to lose weight and burn fat, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and fat, as your body will be in a fat-burning state.

To summarize, the best time to do cardio depends on your individual goals and preferences. If you are trying to build muscle and strength, it is recommended to do cardio after weights. However, if weight loss is your primary goal, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and fat.

Personal Experience: Doing Cardio Before Weights

As a bodybuilder who is primarily focused on weight loss, I like to do cardio before weights. I find that doing cardio first thing in the morning helps me wake up and get energized for my workout. Plus, it helps me burn more calories and fat, which is important for achieving my weight loss goals. However, I make sure to warm up properly before lifting weights to avoid injury and ensure that I am able to lift heavy.

Personal Experience: Doing Cardio After Weights

On the other hand, some bodybuilders prefer to do cardio after weights. They believe that this helps them burn more calories and fat, as their body is already in a fat-burning state after lifting weights. Additionally, doing cardio after weights can be a good way to cool down and stretch out your muscles after a tough workout.

The Importance of Balancing Cardio and Weightlifting

Regardless of whether you do cardio before or after weights, it is important to find a balance between the two. You don't want to overdo it with cardio and neglect weightlifting, or vice versa. Ideally, you should aim to do both types of exercise each week, depending on your goals and fitness level.

Tips for Doing Cardio Before or After Weights

If you decide to do cardio before weights, make sure to warm up properly and avoid doing too much cardio, as this can deplete your energy and make it harder to lift weights. If you choose to do cardio after weights, make sure to stretch properly and avoid doing too much cardio, as this can lead to overtraining and burnout.

Personal Experience: Finding a Balance

For me, finding a balance between cardio and weightlifting has been key to achieving my fitness goals. I like to do cardio before weights a few times a week, but I also make sure to lift heavy and challenge myself with new exercises. Additionally, I try to mix things up by doing different types of cardio, such as running, biking, or swimming.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weights?

A: It depends on your individual goals and preferences. If you are trying to build muscle and strength, it is recommended to do cardio after weights. However, if weight loss is your primary goal, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and fat.

Q: How much cardio should I do before or after weights?

A: It is recommended to do 20-30 minutes of cardio before or after weights, depending on your fitness level and goals.

Q: Will doing cardio before weights make me too tired to lift heavy?

A: It is possible that doing too much cardio before weights can deplete your energy and make it harder to lift heavy. However, if you warm up properly and avoid doing too much cardio, you should be able to lift heavy without feeling too tired.

Q: Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day. However, it is important to find a balance between the two and avoid overtraining, as this can lead to burnout and injury.

Conclusion of Do Bodybuilders Do Cardio Before or After Weights

In conclusion, the answer to whether bodybuilders should do cardio before or after weights depends on their individual goals and preferences. If you are trying to build muscle and strength, it is recommended to do cardio after weights. However, if weight loss is your primary goal, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and fat. Regardless of when you do cardio, it is important to find a balance between cardio and weightlifting, and avoid overtraining or neglecting one type of exercise.
