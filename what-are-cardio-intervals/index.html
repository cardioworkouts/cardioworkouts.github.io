What Are Cardio Intervals A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Nov 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to improve your cardiovascular fitness but find traditional cardio exercises boring or too difficult? Do you want to burn more calories in less time during your workouts? If so, then cardio intervals might be the solution you're looking for.

Cardio intervals are a type of exercise that involves alternating between periods of high-intensity exercise and periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of workout is designed to increase your heart rate and improve your cardiovascular fitness, while also burning more calories than traditional steady-state cardio.

So, what exactly are cardio intervals? In short, they are a form of high-intensity interval training (HIIT) that can be done with a variety of exercises, such as running, cycling, jumping jacks, or burpees. The idea is to push yourself as hard as you can during the high-intensity intervals, followed by a period of rest or low-intensity exercise to allow your body to recover before the next high-intensity interval.

In this article, we'll dive deeper into what are cardio intervals and how you can incorporate them into your fitness routine to improve your cardiovascular fitness and burn more calories in less time.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Intervals

As a fitness enthusiast, I've tried a variety of cardio exercises over the years, from steady-state running to high-intensity spin classes. However, it wasn't until I discovered cardio intervals that I truly saw a difference in my cardiovascular fitness and overall health.

At first, I was intimidated by the idea of pushing myself to my limits during the high-intensity intervals. But with time and practice, I found that I was able to increase my endurance and push myself further than I ever thought possible.

Now, cardio intervals are a regular part of my fitness routine, and I'm constantly challenging myself to push harder and improve my fitness levels.

Benefits of Cardio Intervals

There are many benefits to incorporating cardio intervals into your fitness routine. Here are just a few:

1. Improved Cardiovascular Fitness: Cardio intervals are designed to increase your heart rate and improve your cardiovascular fitness, which can lead to a variety of health benefits, such as improved endurance, lower blood pressure, and reduced risk of heart disease.

2. Increased Caloric Burn: Because cardio intervals involve pushing yourself to your limits, they can help you burn more calories in less time than traditional steady-state cardio exercises.

3. Time-Saving: Cardio intervals are a time-efficient way to improve your fitness levels, as they can be done in as little as 20-30 minutes.

4. Versatile: Cardio intervals can be done with a variety of exercises, so you can mix up your workouts and prevent boredom.

How to Incorporate Cardio Intervals into Your Fitness Routine

If you're interested in trying cardio intervals for yourself, here are some tips to get you started:

1. Choose Your Exercise: Decide on an exercise that you enjoy and that you can do with good form. This could be running, cycling, jumping jacks, or burpees, among others.

2. Warm Up: Before starting your cardio intervals, make sure to warm up your muscles with some light cardio and dynamic stretching.

3. Set Your Intervals: Decide on the length of your high-intensity intervals and your rest periods. A common ratio is 30 seconds of high-intensity exercise followed by 30 seconds of rest, but you can adjust this to your fitness level and goals.

4. Start Slow: Don't push yourself too hard too soon. Start with shorter intervals and gradually build up your endurance over time.

FAQs About Cardio Intervals

Q: How often should I do cardio intervals?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals, but generally 2-3 times per week is a good starting point.

Q: Can cardio intervals be done at home?

A: Yes! Cardio intervals can be done with bodyweight exercises, such as jumping jacks, burpees, or mountain climbers, which can all be done at home with little to no equipment.

Q: Are cardio intervals suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, but it's important to start slowly and gradually build up your endurance over time. Always listen to your body and rest when needed.

Q: Can cardio intervals help with weight loss?

A: Yes! Because cardio intervals can help you burn more calories in less time than traditional steady-state cardio, they can be an effective way to support weight loss goals, along with a healthy diet.

Conclusion of What Are Cardio Intervals

Cardio intervals are a form of high-intensity interval training that can help improve your cardiovascular fitness, increase your caloric burn, and save you time in your workouts. By incorporating cardio intervals into your fitness routine, you can challenge yourself to push harder and see improvements in your overall health and fitness levels.
