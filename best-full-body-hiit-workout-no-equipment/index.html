The Best Full Body Hiit Workout Without Equipment A Step By Step Guide

Written by William Nov 23, 2023 · 6 min read

Are you tired of going to the gym or investing in expensive equipment to get a full body workout? Do you want to burn fat while building muscle in the comfort of your own home? If you answered yes to these questions, then a full body HIIT workout without equipment might be the perfect solution for you.

HIIT stands for High Intensity Interval Training, which involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest. This type of workout is known for burning fat and building muscle quickly, making it a popular choice for those who want to see results fast.

In this article, we will explore the best full body HIIT workout without equipment, including the benefits of this type of workout, the exercises you should include, and tips for getting the most out of your workout.

The Target of the Best Full Body HIIT Workout Without Equipment

Before we dive into the specifics of the workout, it's important to understand the target of a full body HIIT workout without equipment. This type of workout is designed to burn fat and build muscle in all areas of your body, including your arms, legs, abs, and back. By incorporating a variety of exercises that target different muscle groups, you can get a full body workout without the need for any equipment.

Personally, I have found that incorporating a full body HIIT workout into my fitness routine has helped me to burn fat and build muscle quickly. Not only do I feel more energized and confident, but I have also noticed a significant improvement in my overall strength and endurance.

The Benefits of a Full Body HIIT Workout Without Equipment

There are many benefits to incorporating a full body HIIT workout without equipment into your fitness routine. First and foremost, this type of workout is incredibly effective for burning fat and building muscle. By pushing your body to its limits with short bursts of intense exercise, you can increase your metabolism and continue to burn calories long after your workout is over.

In addition to its fat-burning and muscle-building benefits, a full body HIIT workout without equipment is also incredibly convenient. You don't need any special equipment or a gym membership to get started, which means you can do this workout anywhere, anytime. Whether you're at home, in a park, or on vacation, you can get a full body workout without any equipment.

Finally, a full body HIIT workout without equipment is also incredibly efficient. Because you are incorporating high-intensity exercises into your routine, you can get a full body workout in a short amount of time. This means you can get in a great workout even if you only have a few minutes to spare.

The Exercises You Should Include in a Full Body HIIT Workout Without Equipment

Now that you understand the benefits of a full body HIIT workout without equipment, let's take a look at the exercises you should include in your routine. These exercises are designed to target all areas of your body and can be modified to suit your fitness level.

1. Burpees

Burpees are a great full body exercise that can be modified to suit your fitness level. To perform a burpee, start in a standing position, then squat down and place your hands on the ground. Jump your feet back into a plank position, then jump your feet back up to your hands and stand up. Repeat for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

2. Jumping Jacks

Jumping jacks are a classic exercise that can help get your heart rate up and work your legs, arms, and core. To perform a jumping jack, stand with your feet together and your arms at your sides. Jump your feet out to the sides while raising your arms overhead, then jump your feet back together and lower your arms. Repeat for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

3. Mountain Climbers

Mountain climbers are a challenging exercise that work your core, arms, and legs. To perform a mountain climber, start in a plank position, then bring one knee up towards your chest. Quickly switch legs, bringing the other knee up towards your chest. Continue alternating legs for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

4. Squat Jumps

Squat jumps are a great way to work your legs and glutes while getting your heart rate up. To perform a squat jump, start in a squat position, then jump up explosively while extending your arms overhead. Land softly in a squat position and repeat for 30 seconds, then rest for 10 seconds.

Tips for Getting the Most Out of Your Full Body HIIT Workout Without Equipment

Now that you have an idea of the exercises you should include in your full body HIIT workout without equipment, here are some tips for getting the most out of your workout:

1. Warm up before you start. A quick warm up can help prevent injury and prepare your body for the workout ahead.

2. Start slow and work your way up. If you are new to HIIT workouts, don't push yourself too hard too fast. Start with shorter intervals and gradually increase the intensity as you get stronger.

3. Focus on form. It's important to perform each exercise with proper form to prevent injury and get the most out of your workout.

4. Stay hydrated. Drinking water before, during, and after your workout can help keep you energized and prevent dehydration.

Question and Answer

Q: Do I need any equipment for a full body HIIT workout?

A: No, you don't need any equipment for a full body HIIT workout. All of the exercises can be done using your own bodyweight.

Q: How long should a full body HIIT workout be?

A: A full body HIIT workout can be as short as 10 minutes or as long as 30 minutes. The key is to incorporate high-intensity exercises that work your entire body.

Q: How often should I do a full body HIIT workout?

A: You can do a full body HIIT workout 2-3 times per week, depending on your fitness level and schedule. It's important to give your body time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q: Can I modify the exercises to suit my fitness level?

A: Yes, all of the exercises can be modified to suit your fitness level. For example, you can do modified push-ups instead of regular push-ups, or step back into a plank position instead of jumping back.

Conclusion of The Best Full Body HIIT Workout Without Equipment

A full body HIIT workout without equipment is a great way to burn fat, build muscle, and improve your overall fitness. By incorporating high-intensity exercises that target all areas of your body, you can get a full body workout without the need for any equipment. Remember to warm up before you start, focus on form, and stay hydrated throughout your workout. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy the many benefits of a full body HIIT workout without equipment.
