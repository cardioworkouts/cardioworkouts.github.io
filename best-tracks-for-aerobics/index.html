The Best Tracks For Aerobics Get Your Heart Pumping

Written by Luffy Oct 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of doing aerobics to the same old songs? Do you find yourself losing motivation halfway through your workout? Look no further, because we have compiled a list of the best tracks for aerobics that will keep you energized and moving.

Aerobics is a great way to get your heart pumping and burn calories, but finding the right music can be a challenge. You want something that is fast-paced, upbeat, and motivational. The wrong music can make your workout feel like a chore, rather than something enjoyable and energizing.

So, what are the best tracks for aerobics? We have scoured the internet and asked fitness experts to find the top songs that will keep you moving and motivated throughout your workout.

Here are some of the best tracks for aerobics and related keywords:

1. "Can't Stop the Feeling" by Justin Timberlake

When it comes to upbeat and catchy tunes, Justin Timberlake never disappoints. "Can't Stop the Feeling" is a great choice for aerobics because of its high energy and infectious beat. This song is sure to make you want to get up and dance, making it the perfect choice for your next aerobics session.

2. "Uptown Funk" by Mark Ronson ft. Bruno Mars

If you're looking for a song that will get you pumped up and ready to move, "Uptown Funk" is a great choice. With its funky beat and catchy chorus, this song is sure to keep you energized throughout your entire workout.

3. "Eye of the Tiger" by Survivor

This classic song is a staple in the world of fitness and for good reason. The driving beat and motivational lyrics make it the perfect choice for any workout, including aerobics. You'll feel like a champion as you power through your workout to this iconic tune.

4. "I Gotta Feeling" by The Black Eyed Peas

Another high-energy tune that is sure to get your heart pumping is "I Gotta Feeling" by The Black Eyed Peas. The catchy chorus and upbeat tempo make it the perfect choice for any aerobics routine.

5. "Shape of You" by Ed Sheeran

Ed Sheeran's "Shape of You" is a great choice for those who want a more laid-back and relaxed aerobics routine. The smooth beat and catchy melody make it the perfect choice for low-impact workouts or cool-down sessions.

How to Create Your Own Playlist

Creating your own playlist is a great way to keep your workouts fresh and exciting. Here are some tips for creating the ultimate aerobics playlist:

1. Start with a warm-up song that has a slower tempo to ease you into your workout.

2. Choose songs with a fast tempo and upbeat rhythm to keep you energized and motivated.

3. Mix it up with different genres and artists to keep your playlist interesting.

4. End your playlist with a cool-down song that has a slower tempo to help you relax and wind down.

FAQs

Q: How many songs should be in an aerobics playlist?

A: It really depends on the length of your workout, but a good rule of thumb is to have at least 10-12 songs to keep things interesting.

Q: Can I use any type of music for aerobics?

A: While you can technically use any type of music for aerobics, it's important to choose songs with a fast tempo and upbeat rhythm to keep you motivated and energized.

Q: What are some other good songs for aerobics?

A: Some other great songs for aerobics include "Jump" by Van Halen, "Dancing Queen" by ABBA, and "Walking on Sunshine" by Katrina and the Waves.

Q: Is it okay to repeat songs in an aerobics playlist?

A: While it's okay to repeat songs in an aerobics playlist, it's important to mix it up to keep things interesting. Try to have a variety of different songs and genres in your playlist.

Conclusion

The best tracks for aerobics are those that are fast-paced, upbeat, and motivational. With these songs in your playlist, you're sure to stay energized and motivated throughout your entire workout. So put on your dancing shoes and get ready to sweat!
