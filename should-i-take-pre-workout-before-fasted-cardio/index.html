Should I Take Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Written by Oliver Aug 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those who prefers to do fasted cardio first thing in the morning? Fasted cardio is a great way to burn fat, but have you ever wondered if taking pre-workout before fasted cardio is a good idea? In this post, we will discuss the benefits and drawbacks of taking pre-workout before fasted cardio to help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points of Taking Pre-Workout Before Fasted Cardio

When it comes to taking pre-workout before fasted cardio, there are a few pain points to consider. Firstly, pre-workout supplements are designed to give you an energy boost, which can be counterintuitive if you're doing fasted cardio to burn fat. Secondly, pre-workout supplements can cause stomach discomfort, especially if you haven't eaten anything before your workout. Lastly, taking pre-workout before fasted cardio can mess with your natural energy levels and sleep patterns, making it difficult to fall asleep at night.

Answering the Target Question

The answer to the question of whether you should take pre-workout before fasted cardio depends on your fitness goals and personal preference. If your goal is to burn fat, doing fasted cardio without any supplements can be an effective way to achieve it. However, if you're looking for an energy boost to power through your workout, taking pre-workout before fasted cardio can help you achieve that. It's important to note that everyone's body is different, and what works for one person may not work for another.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, taking pre-workout before fasted cardio is a personal choice that depends on your fitness goals and preferences. While pre-workout supplements can give you an energy boost, they can also cause stomach discomfort and affect your sleep patterns. If you decide to take pre-workout before fasted cardio, it's important to choose a supplement that's specifically designed for fasted cardio and to listen to your body's cues.

The Pros of Taking Pre-Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Personally, I've found that taking pre-workout before fasted cardio has its benefits. It helps me power through my workout and gives me the energy boost I need to get started. It also helps me stay focused and alert during my workout, which is essential when doing fasted cardio. However, I always make sure to choose a pre-workout supplement that's designed for fasted cardio and to take it in moderation.

The Cons of Taking Pre-Workout Before Fasted Cardio

On the other hand, taking pre-workout before fasted cardio can have some drawbacks. For instance, some pre-workout supplements can cause stomach discomfort, especially if you haven't eaten anything before your workout. Additionally, pre-workout supplements can affect your sleep patterns, making it difficult to fall asleep at night.

Should You Take Pre-Workout Before Fasted Cardio?

Ultimately, the decision of whether to take pre-workout before fasted cardio is up to you. However, there are a few things to keep in mind before making your decision. Firstly, make sure to choose a pre-workout supplement that's specifically designed for fasted cardio. Secondly, listen to your body's cues and take the supplement in moderation. Lastly, if you experience any negative side effects, stop taking the supplement immediately and consult with your doctor.

How to Choose the Right Pre-Workout Supplement for Fasted Cardio

When choosing a pre-workout supplement for fasted cardio, look for ingredients that provide an energy boost without causing jitters or stomach discomfort. Some good ingredients to look for include caffeine, beta-alanine, and creatine. Additionally, choose a supplement that's low in calories and sugar, as these can counteract the benefits of fasted cardio.

My Personal Experience

As someone who regularly does fasted cardio, I've found that taking pre-workout supplements before my workout can be helpful. However, I always make sure to choose a supplement that's designed for fasted cardio and to take it in moderation. I've also noticed that when I take pre-workout before fasted cardio, I have more energy and focus during my workout, which helps me achieve better results.

Question and Answer

Q: How long before fasted cardio should I take pre-workout?

A: It's recommended to take pre-workout 30-45 minutes before your workout to allow it to fully kick in.

Q: Can taking pre-workout before fasted cardio cause muscle loss?

A: No, taking pre-workout before fasted cardio will not cause muscle loss. However, it's important to maintain a balanced diet and engage in strength training to preserve muscle mass.

Q: Can I take pre-workout on an empty stomach?

A: Yes, you can take pre-workout on an empty stomach. However, some pre-workout supplements can cause stomach discomfort, so it's important to choose a supplement that's specifically designed for fasted cardio and to take it in moderation.

Q: Is it safe to take pre-workout every day?

A: It's not recommended to take pre-workout every day, as it can lead to dependence and negative side effects. Instead, use pre-workout supplements as needed and take breaks in between.

Conclusion of Should I Take Pre Workout Before Fasted Cardio

Choosing whether to take pre-workout before fasted cardio is a personal decision that depends on your fitness goals and preferences. While pre-workout supplements can give you an energy boost, they can also cause stomach discomfort and affect your sleep patterns. If you decide to take pre-workout before fasted cardio, make sure to choose a supplement that's specifically designed for it and to take it in moderation. Always listen to your body's cues and consult with your doctor if you experience any negative side effects.
