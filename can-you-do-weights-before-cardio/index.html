Can You Do Weights Before Cardio Here s What You Need To Know

Written by Aprilia Jun 18, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you someone who's trying to figure out the best order to do your workouts? Maybe you're wondering if you can do weights before cardio or if it's better to do cardio first? Whatever the case may be, it's important to understand the benefits and drawbacks of each approach.

When it comes to can you do weights before cardio, there is no one-size-fits-all answer. It really depends on your fitness goals and what you hope to achieve with your workouts.

That being said, if your primary goal is to build muscle and strength, it's generally recommended that you do your weightlifting routine before cardio. This is because weightlifting requires a lot of energy and focus, and doing cardio beforehand can leave you feeling fatigued and unable to perform at your highest level.

On the other hand, if your primary goal is to improve your cardiovascular fitness and burn fat, you may want to consider doing cardio before lifting weights. This can help increase your heart rate and get your body warmed up for the more intense weightlifting exercises that follow.

My Personal Experience with Weights Before Cardio

As someone who's been weightlifting for several years, I've found that doing weights before cardio works best for me. I like to give my all to my weightlifting routine and feel like I'm getting the most out of my workout.

However, I've also tried doing cardio before weights on occasion, and I've found that it can be a good way to get my body warmed up and ready to go. It all depends on what I'm hoping to achieve with my workout that day.

The Benefits of Doing Weights Before Cardio

There are several benefits to doing weights before cardio:

  • It can help you build muscle and strength more effectively
  • You'll have more energy and focus for your weightlifting routine
  • You'll be less likely to get injured during weightlifting exercises

The Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weights

On the other hand, there are also some benefits to doing cardio before weights:

  • You'll be more warmed up and ready to go for your weightlifting routine
  • You'll burn more calories during your cardio workout
  • You'll improve your cardiovascular fitness more effectively

Tips for Doing Weights Before Cardio

If you decide to do weights before cardio, here are some tips to help you get the most out of your workout:

  • Make sure you're properly warmed up before starting your weightlifting routine
  • Focus on compound exercises that work multiple muscle groups at once
  • Take breaks between sets to catch your breath and recover
  • Stay hydrated and fuel your body with the right nutrients before and after your workout

How to Do Cardio Before Weights

If you decide to do cardio before weights, here are some tips to help you get the most out of your workout:

  • Start with a low-intensity cardio exercise to get your heart rate up
  • Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio workout
  • Focus on high-intensity interval training (HIIT) to maximize your calorie burn
  • Take breaks between sets of weightlifting exercises to catch your breath and recover

Conclusion of Can You Do Weights Before Cardio

Ultimately, whether you do weights before cardio or vice versa will depend on your fitness goals and personal preferences. Both approaches have their own benefits, so it's important to experiment and find what works best for you. Just remember to stay safe, stay hydrated, and listen to your body!

Question and Answer

Q: Is it bad to do cardio after weights?

A: Not necessarily. It really depends on your goals and how you feel after weightlifting. Some people find that doing cardio after weights helps them recover and feel better, while others prefer to do cardio before weights.

Q: How long should I wait between weights and cardio?

A: It's generally recommended to wait at least 30 minutes between weightlifting and cardio to allow your body to recover and recharge.

Q: Can doing weights before cardio help me lose weight?

A: Yes, doing weights before cardio can help you lose weight by building muscle and increasing your metabolism. Just make sure you're also eating a healthy diet and getting enough rest.

Q: Should I do weights and cardio on the same day?

A: It's up to you, but doing both weights and cardio on the same day can be an effective way to get a full-body workout and maximize your results.
