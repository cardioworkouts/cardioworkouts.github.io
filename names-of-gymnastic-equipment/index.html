Names Of Gymnastic Equipment A Beginner s Guide

Written by Petter Jul 21, 2023 · 5 min read

Gymnastics is an exciting sport that requires a lot of dedication and practice. One of the most important aspects of gymnastics is understanding the different types of equipment that are used in the sport. If you're a beginner, you might find the names of gymnastic equipment confusing. In this article, we'll break down the different types of equipment and their names to help you get started.

The Pain Points of Gymnastic Equipment Names

One of the biggest pain points when it comes to names of gymnastic equipment is simply understanding what each piece of equipment is and what it's used for. For beginners, the names of the equipment can be overwhelming, and it can be difficult to know where to start. Additionally, there are often multiple names for the same piece of equipment, which can further add to the confusion.

Understanding Names of Gymnastic Equipment

Let's start by breaking down some of the most common types of equipment used in gymnastics:

  • Vault: A padded piece of equipment used for running and jumping over.
  • Balance Beam: A narrow beam set at a raised height that gymnasts perform acrobatic moves on.
  • Uneven Bars: Two horizontal bars of different heights that gymnasts perform swings, releases, and transitions on.
  • Floor Exercise: A padded mat where gymnasts perform tumbling passes and dance routines.
  • Pommel Horse: A cylindrical piece of equipment where gymnasts perform routines consisting of circular movements, swings, and handstands.

These are just a few of the most common pieces of equipment used in gymnastics. There are many other types of equipment that are used for specific events or training purposes.

Vault

The vault is one of the most iconic pieces of equipment in gymnastics. As a beginner, running and jumping over a padded vault may seem like a daunting task. When I first started, I was terrified of the vault, but with practice and guidance, I was able to master it. It's important to remember that everyone starts somewhere and that with practice, you can improve.

One of the keys to mastering the vault is understanding the approach. You want to build up enough speed to carry you over the vault but not so much that you lose control. You'll also want to focus on proper technique, such as keeping your arms straight and your body tight in the air.

Balance Beam

The balance beam is another piece of equipment that can be intimidating for beginners. When I first started, I was scared to even step onto the beam. However, with practice and guidance, I was able to overcome my fear and learn to perform acrobatic moves on it.

One of the keys to mastering the balance beam is staying focused and confident. It's important to take your time and not rush through your routine. You'll also want to focus on proper technique, such as keeping your body tight and your eyes focused on the end of the beam.

Uneven Bars

The uneven bars are a challenging piece of equipment that require a lot of upper body strength and coordination. When I first started, I struggled with even getting up onto the bars. However, with practice and guidance, I was able to learn how to perform swings, releases, and transitions.

One of the keys to mastering the uneven bars is building up your upper body strength. You'll want to focus on exercises that target your arms, shoulders, and core. You'll also want to focus on proper technique, such as keeping your body tight and your legs together during your routine.

Pommel Horse

The pommel horse is a difficult piece of equipment that requires a lot of balance and coordination. When I first started, I found it challenging to even stay on the horse. However, with practice and guidance, I was able to learn how to perform circular movements, swings, and handstands.

One of the keys to mastering the pommel horse is building up your core strength. You'll want to focus on exercises that target your abs and lower back. You'll also want to focus on proper technique, such as keeping your body tight and your legs together during your routine.

Frequently Asked Questions

What is the difference between the uneven bars and the parallel bars?

The uneven bars are two horizontal bars of different heights that gymnasts perform swings, releases, and transitions on. The parallel bars, on the other hand, are two horizontal bars of equal height that are used for performing swinging and balancing skills.

What is the difference between the balance beam and the floor exercise?

The balance beam is a narrow beam set at a raised height that gymnasts perform acrobatic moves on. The floor exercise, on the other hand, is a padded mat where gymnasts perform tumbling passes and dance routines.

What is the purpose of the vault?

The purpose of the vault is to test a gymnast's ability to run, jump, and perform acrobatic moves over a padded piece of equipment.

What is the purpose of the pommel horse?

The purpose of the pommel horse is to test a gymnast's ability to perform circular movements, swings, and handstands on a cylindrical piece of equipment.

Conclusion of Names of Gymnastic Equipment

Understanding the different types of equipment used in gymnastics is an important part of the sport. While it can be overwhelming at first, with practice and guidance, you can learn to master each piece of equipment. Whether you're a beginner or an experienced gymnast, it's important to focus on proper technique and safety at all times.
