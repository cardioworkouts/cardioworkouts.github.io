Is It Good To Do Cardio After Strength Training

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you someone who enjoys hitting the gym and working out? Do you have a fitness routine that you follow religiously? If yes, then you may have heard of the debate on whether it is good to do cardio after strength training. Some people swear by it, while others believe it is not beneficial. In this article, we will explore the topic in detail and help you make an informed decision.

Have you ever felt confused about whether you should do cardio after strength training? Are you unsure about what is good for your body and what is not? If yes, then you are not alone. Many people face this dilemma and struggle to find a definitive answer. It can be frustrating to put in so much effort and not know if you are doing the right thing.

The short answer is that it depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, then doing cardio after strength training can be beneficial. However, if you are trying to build muscle, then it may not be the best option. Let us delve deeper into the topic and understand why.

In conclusion, doing cardio after strength training can be good or bad depending on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, then it can be beneficial. However, if you are trying to build muscle, then it may not be the best option. Ultimately, it is up to you to decide what is best for your body and your fitness goals.

Cardio After Strength Training For Weight Loss

Personally, I have found doing cardio after strength training to be helpful in my weight loss journey. When I started my fitness routine, I was primarily focused on building muscle. However, as I progressed, I realized that my body was not responding the way I wanted it to. I was gaining muscle, but I was not losing the excess fat around my midsection.

That is when I decided to incorporate cardio into my routine. I started doing 30 minutes of cardio on the treadmill after every strength training session. Within a few weeks, I noticed a significant difference in my body. My midsection was getting slimmer, and my overall weight was decreasing.

From my personal experience, I can say that doing cardio after strength training can be beneficial for weight loss. When you do strength training, you burn calories, but you primarily focus on building muscle. Cardio, on the other hand, is excellent for burning fat. Doing cardio after strength training can help you get the best of both worlds.

Cardio After Strength Training For Muscle Building

While doing cardio after strength training can be beneficial for weight loss, it may not be the best option for building muscle. When you do strength training, you are breaking down your muscle fibers. To build muscle, you need to give your body time to recover and repair those fibers. Doing cardio immediately after strength training can interfere with that process and hinder muscle growth.

Additionally, doing cardio can fatigue your body and make it difficult to lift weights effectively. When you lift weights, you need to have the energy and strength to perform each exercise correctly. If you are tired from doing cardio, you may not be able to lift weights effectively, which can hamper your muscle-building progress.

Alternatives To Doing Cardio After Strength Training

If you are looking to build muscle, there are alternatives to doing cardio after strength training. One option is to do cardio on your rest days. When you take a break from strength training, you can use that time to do cardio and burn fat. Another option is to do cardio before strength training. Doing cardio before strength training can help you warm up your body and get your heart rate up, preparing you for an intense workout.

How To Incorporate Cardio Into Your Fitness Routine

If you are looking to incorporate cardio into your fitness routine, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure you choose a form of cardio that you enjoy. If you hate running on the treadmill, then find another form of cardio that you enjoy, such as cycling or swimming. Second, start slow and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your cardio sessions. Lastly, listen to your body and adjust your routine accordingly. If you feel tired or fatigued, take a break and rest.

Conclusion Of Is It Good To Do Cardio After Strength Training

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio after strength training depends on your fitness goals. If you are looking to lose weight, then it can be beneficial. However, if you are trying to build muscle, then it may not be the best option. There are alternatives to doing cardio after strength training, such as doing cardio on your rest days or doing cardio before strength training. Whatever you choose, make sure you listen to your body and do what is best for you.

Question and Answer

Q1. Does doing cardio after strength training burn fat?

A1. Yes, doing cardio after strength training can help you burn fat.

Q2. Can doing cardio after strength training hinder muscle growth?

A2. Yes, doing cardio after strength training can interfere with muscle growth.

Q3. What are the alternatives to doing cardio after strength training?

A3. Alternatives to doing cardio after strength training include doing cardio on your rest days or doing cardio before strength training.

Q4. Is it necessary to do cardio after strength training?

A4. No, it is not necessary to do cardio after strength training. It depends on your fitness goals.
