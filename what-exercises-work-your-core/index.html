What Exercises Work Your Core A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Robby Jul 09, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of doing endless crunches and still not seeing results in your core? You're not alone. Many people struggle to find the right exercises to work their core effectively. In this comprehensive guide, we'll cover everything you need to know about what exercises work your core.

Having a strong core is essential for good posture, balance, and overall health. But finding the right exercises to target your core can be challenging, especially if you're not sure where to start.

In general, exercises that involve stabilizing your midsection or moving your spine are great for working your core. This includes exercises like planks, side planks, Russian twists, and bird dogs. But there are many other exercises that can help you strengthen your core, and we'll cover them all in this guide.

To summarize, there are many exercises that can effectively work your core, including planks, side planks, Russian twists, and bird dogs. But there are also many other exercises you can add to your routine to target your core in different ways.

Planks

When it comes to what exercises work your core, planks are one of the most effective. Planks work your entire core, including your abs, back, and glutes. To do a plank, start in a push-up position with your arms straight and your hands shoulder-width apart. Lower your forearms to the ground and hold your body in a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold for as long as you can.

Personally, planks are one of my go-to exercises for working my core. They're simple, effective, and can be done anywhere with no equipment.

Side Planks

Side planks are another great exercise for working your core. They target your obliques, which are the muscles on the sides of your abs. To do a side plank, start by lying on your side with your legs straight. Prop yourself up on your forearm, keeping your elbow directly beneath your shoulder. Lift your hips off the ground and hold for as long as you can.

Side planks are a great way to target your obliques, which can be difficult to work with traditional ab exercises.

Russian Twists

Russian twists are a great exercise for targeting your entire core, including your obliques. To do a Russian twist, sit on the ground with your knees bent and your feet flat on the floor. Lean back slightly and lift your feet off the ground, balancing on your tailbone. Hold a weight or a medicine ball with both hands and twist your torso to the left, then to the right.

Russian twists are a great exercise for targeting your obliques and improving your rotational strength.

Bird Dogs

Bird dogs are a great exercise for improving your core stability and balance. To do a bird dog, start on your hands and knees with your wrists directly beneath your shoulders and your knees directly beneath your hips. Reach your right arm forward and your left leg back, keeping both straight. Hold for a few seconds, then switch sides.

Bird dogs are a great exercise for improving your core stability and balance, which can help prevent injuries and improve your overall fitness.

Conclusion of What Exercises Work Your Core

There are many exercises that can effectively work your core, including planks, side planks, Russian twists, and bird dogs. But there are also many other exercises you can add to your routine to target your core in different ways. Incorporating a variety of exercises into your routine is key to strengthening your core and seeing results.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do core exercises every day?

A: Yes, you can do core exercises every day. However, it's important to give your muscles time to rest and recover between workouts. Aim to do core exercises 2-3 times per week, with at least one rest day in between.

Q: Do I need equipment to work my core?

A: No, you don't need equipment to work your core. Many effective core exercises, like planks and bird dogs, can be done with no equipment at all. However, adding weights or resistance bands to your routine can help you challenge your muscles in new ways.

Q: Are crunches effective for working my core?

A: Crunches can be effective for working your abs, but they only target a small portion of your core. To effectively work your entire core, including your back and glutes, it's important to incorporate a variety of exercises into your routine.

Q: How long should I hold a plank?

A: Aim to hold a plank for at least 30 seconds, and work your way up to a minute or longer. Remember to keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels, and engage your abs and glutes to maintain proper form.
