Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

Written by Bardi Nov 06, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those people who have been spending hours on cardio machines but still find it difficult to lose weight? Are you looking for a more efficient way to burn calories and shed those extra pounds? Well, you might have heard that strength training is the way to go. But does strength training really burn more calories than cardio? Let's find out.

Pain Points Related to Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

Many people struggle to lose weight despite their best efforts. They spend hours on the treadmill or stationary bike, only to see minimal results. This can be frustrating and demotivating. Some people might even give up on their weight loss goals because they feel like they are not making any progress.

Answer to Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

The short answer is yes, strength training does burn more calories than cardio. While cardio is great for improving cardiovascular health and burning calories during the exercise session, strength training has a greater impact on your metabolism. When you lift weights, your body continues to burn calories even after you finish your workout. This is because strength training increases your muscle mass, which in turn increases your basal metabolic rate (BMR). Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest to keep your vital organs functioning. The more muscle you have, the higher your BMR will be, and the more calories you will burn throughout the day.

Summary of Main Points of Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

Strength training burns more calories than cardio because it increases your muscle mass, which in turn increases your basal metabolic rate. Your BMR is the number of calories your body burns at rest to keep your vital organs functioning. The more muscle you have, the higher your BMR will be, and the more calories you will burn throughout the day.

Personal Experience with Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

As a personal trainer, I have seen many clients who have struggled to lose weight despite doing hours of cardio every week. Once they started incorporating strength training into their routine, they started seeing better results. One of my clients, Sarah, started lifting weights three times a week and saw a significant improvement in her body composition. She lost body fat and gained lean muscle mass, which boosted her metabolism and helped her burn more calories throughout the day.

The Science Behind Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

Strength training creates micro-tears in your muscle fibers, which your body then repairs during the rest period. This repair process requires energy, which means you burn calories even when you're not working out. Additionally, strength training increases your muscle mass, which is metabolically active tissue. This means that it requires more energy to maintain than fat tissue, which boosts your metabolism and helps you burn more calories throughout the day.

The Benefits of Strength Training

Strength training has many benefits beyond just burning calories. It can help improve your posture, increase your bone density, reduce your risk of injury, and enhance your overall quality of life. Additionally, strength training can help you build confidence and improve your mental health.

How to Incorporate Strength Training into Your Routine

If you're new to strength training, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity. You can start with bodyweight exercises like squats, lunges, and push-ups, and then add weights as you get stronger. It's also important to focus on proper form and technique to avoid injury. Consider working with a personal trainer or fitness professional to create a safe and effective strength training program.

Personal Experience with Strength Training

Personally, I have been strength training for several years and have seen significant improvements in my body composition, strength, and overall health. I used to focus solely on cardio, but once I started lifting weights, I saw better results in terms of muscle definition and fat loss. I also feel stronger and more confident in everyday life.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Can I still do cardio if I am strength training?

A: Absolutely! Cardio is still important for cardiovascular health and calorie burning during the exercise session. Just make sure you don't overdo it and prioritize strength training in your routine.

Q: How often should I strength train?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and schedule. Ideally, you should aim to strength train at least two to three times a week, with at least one day of rest in between sessions.

Q: Will I bulk up if I strength train?

A: It's unlikely that you will bulk up unless you are specifically training for hypertrophy (muscle growth) and consuming a surplus of calories. Most people will see increases in muscle tone and definition without getting bulky.

Q: Can I strength train at home without equipment?

A: Yes! Bodyweight exercises like squats, lunges, push-ups, and planks can be done at home without any equipment. You can also use household items like water bottles or cans as makeshift weights.

Conclusion of Does Strength Training Burn More Calories Than Cardio

While cardio is great for burning calories during the exercise session, strength training has a greater impact on your metabolism and can help you burn more calories throughout the day. Incorporating strength training into your routine can help you build muscle, boost your metabolism, and improve your overall health and fitness. So, if you're looking to lose weight and improve your body composition, consider adding strength training to your fitness routine.
