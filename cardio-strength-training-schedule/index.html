Cardio Strength Training Schedule A Beginner s Guide

Written by Daniel Oct 22, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you looking for a way to improve your overall fitness level and burn fat at the same time? Look no further than cardio strength training. This type of workout combines cardiovascular exercise with strength training to give you the best of both worlds. But how do you create a cardio strength training schedule that works for you? Read on to find out.

Many people struggle to find a workout routine that they can stick to long-term. They may feel overwhelmed by the number of options available or unsure of how to structure their workouts. Others may experience boredom or lack of motivation when doing the same type of exercise every day.

The goal of cardio strength training is to create a balanced workout that includes both cardiovascular exercise and strength training. By doing so, you can improve your endurance, build muscle, and burn fat all in one workout.

In this article, we will cover the basics of creating a cardio strength training schedule that works for you. We will discuss the benefits of cardio strength training, how to create a schedule that fits your needs, and some tips for staying motivated and achieving your fitness goals.

What is cardio strength training?

Cardio strength training is a type of workout that combines cardiovascular exercise with strength training. This type of workout can be done in a variety of ways, including circuit training, HIIT (high-intensity interval training), and plyometrics.

The goal of cardio strength training is to improve your overall fitness level by challenging your cardiovascular system and building muscle at the same time. By doing so, you can improve your endurance, burn fat, and build lean muscle mass.

Personally, I started with cardio strength training a few months ago. At first, I was intimidated by the idea of doing both cardio and strength training in one workout. But after a few sessions, I quickly realized the benefits of this type of workout. Not only was I able to burn more calories in a shorter amount of time, but I also felt stronger and more energized throughout the day.

How to create a cardio strength training schedule?

When creating a cardio strength training schedule, it's important to consider your fitness level, goals, and schedule. Here are some tips to get you started:

1. Determine your fitness level

Before starting any new workout routine, it's important to assess your current fitness level. This will help you determine how much intensity and volume you can handle without risking injury or burnout. You can do this by taking a fitness test or consulting with a personal trainer.

2. Set realistic goals

Once you have determined your fitness level, you can set realistic goals for your cardio strength training routine. This could include improving your endurance, building muscle, or losing weight. Make sure your goals are specific, measurable, and achievable.

3. Choose your workout style

There are many different styles of cardio strength training, including circuit training, HIIT, and plyometrics. Choose a workout style that fits your fitness level and goals, and that you enjoy doing.

4. Schedule your workouts

When creating your cardio strength training schedule, make sure to schedule your workouts at a time that works for you. This could be in the morning before work, during your lunch break, or in the evening after work. Aim to do at least 3-4 workouts per week.

5. Mix it up

To avoid boredom and plateaus, it's important to mix up your cardio strength training routine. This could include changing up the exercises, increasing the intensity or volume, or trying a new workout style.

How to stay motivated?

Staying motivated is key to achieving your fitness goals. Here are some tips for staying motivated and sticking to your cardio strength training schedule:

1. Find a workout buddy

Working out with a friend or family member can help keep you accountable and motivated. You can also challenge each other and make the workout more fun.

2. Track your progress

Tracking your progress can help you stay motivated and see the results of your hard work. This could include taking progress photos, measuring your body fat percentage, or logging your workouts.

3. Reward yourself

Setting small goals and rewarding yourself when you achieve them can help keep you motivated. This could include treating yourself to a massage or a new workout outfit.

4. Remember your "why"

When you feel unmotivated, remind yourself why you started your cardio strength training routine in the first place. Whether it's to improve your health, build confidence, or feel stronger, keeping your "why" in mind can help you push through tough workouts.

Conclusion of cardio strength training schedule

A cardio strength training schedule is a great way to improve your overall fitness level, burn fat, and build lean muscle mass. By following the tips outlined in this article, you can create a cardio strength training routine that works for you and stay motivated to achieve your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio strength training?

A: Aim to do at least 3-4 cardio strength training workouts per week. This will give your body enough time to recover between workouts.

Q: How long should a cardio strength training workout be?

A: A cardio strength training workout can range from 30-60 minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals. Make sure to include a warm-up and cool-down period in your workout.

Q: What are the benefits of cardio strength training?

A: Cardio strength training can improve your cardiovascular health, build lean muscle mass, and burn fat all in one workout.

Q: Can I do cardio strength training if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, cardio strength training is suitable for beginners. Start with low-intensity exercises and gradually increase the intensity and volume over time.
