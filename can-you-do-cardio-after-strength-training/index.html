Can You Do Cardio After Strength Training

Written by Daniel Jun 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who loves to hit the gym and get a good workout in? If so, you may have wondered about the best way to structure your routine. One question that comes up frequently is whether you can do cardio after strength training. In this post, we'll explore the answer to that question and provide you with some helpful tips to optimize your workouts.

Many people wonder about the order in which they should do their exercises. Some believe that cardio should come before strength training, while others prefer to do it the other way around. There are different opinions on this matter, and it ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences.

The short answer to the question is yes, you can do cardio after strength training. In fact, it can be beneficial to do so. By doing strength training first, you'll be able to focus on building your muscles and improving your strength. Then, by doing cardio afterwards, you'll be able to burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health.

In summary, doing cardio after strength training can be an effective way to get the most out of your workout routine. It can help you burn more calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and build muscle strength.

Benefits of Doing Cardio After Strength Training

Many people believe that doing cardio before strength training is the best way to go. However, there are several benefits to doing cardio after strength training that you should consider.

First, doing cardio after strength training can help you burn more calories. When you do strength training first, you'll be able to build up your muscles and increase your metabolism. Then, when you do cardio afterwards, you'll be able to burn more calories because your body is already in a fat-burning state.

Second, doing cardio after strength training can improve your cardiovascular health. Cardiovascular exercise, such as running or cycling, can help improve your heart health and reduce your risk of heart disease. By doing cardio after strength training, you'll be able to get the benefits of both types of exercise.

My Personal Experience

As someone who enjoys going to the gym, I've tried different workout routines over the years. I used to do cardio before strength training, but I found that I wasn't able to lift as heavy as I wanted to. Then, I switched to doing strength training first and cardio afterwards, and I noticed a significant improvement in my strength and endurance.

I also found that doing cardio after strength training helped me burn more calories and improve my cardiovascular health. Overall, I prefer doing cardio after strength training because it allows me to focus on building my muscles first and then getting a good cardiovascular workout in.

Tips for Doing Cardio After Strength Training

If you're planning to do cardio after strength training, there are a few things you should keep in mind to ensure that you get the most out of your workout.

First, make sure to give yourself enough time to recover between your strength training and cardio sessions. Your muscles need time to recover after strength training, so aim to wait at least 20-30 minutes before starting your cardio workout.

Second, choose the right type of cardio exercise. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) can be a great way to get in a quick and effective cardio workout after strength training. Aim to do 20-30 minutes of HIIT to get your heart rate up and burn more calories.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after strength training?

A: It depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. However, doing strength training first and cardio afterwards can be an effective way to burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health.

Q: How long should I wait between strength training and cardio?

A: Aim to wait at least 20-30 minutes between your strength training and cardio sessions to allow your muscles to recover.

Q: What type of cardio exercise is best after strength training?

A: High-intensity interval training (HIIT) can be a great way to get in a quick and effective cardio workout after strength training.

Q: Can I do strength training and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do strength training and cardio on the same day. Just make sure to give yourself enough time to recover between your workouts and choose the right type of cardio exercise.

Conclusion of Can You Do Cardio After Strength Training

In conclusion, doing cardio after strength training can be an effective way to get the most out of your workout routine. It can help you burn more calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and build muscle strength. By following the tips we've shared in this post, you can optimize your workouts and reach your fitness goals.
