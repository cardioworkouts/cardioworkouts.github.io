Is It Better To Do High Intensity Cardio

Written by Oliver Jul 05, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours on the treadmill or stationary bike at the gym without seeing any significant results? Are you wondering if there is a more effective way to burn calories and improve your overall fitness? If so, you may be considering high intensity cardio as a potential solution.

Many people struggle to find the right type of exercise that will help them achieve their fitness goals. While traditional low intensity cardio has been a popular choice for decades, recent studies suggest that high intensity cardio may be a more effective way to burn calories and improve overall fitness. However, it's important to understand the potential risks and benefits of high intensity cardio before making a decision about whether it is right for you.

High intensity cardio involves short bursts of intense exercise followed by periods of rest or low intensity exercise. This type of workout is designed to increase your heart rate and oxygen consumption, which can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular fitness. Some popular forms of high intensity cardio include sprinting, interval training, and high intensity interval training (HIIT).

In summary, high intensity cardio can be an effective way to burn calories and improve overall fitness. However, it is important to approach this type of exercise with caution and to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new workout routine.

My Experience with High Intensity Cardio

As a fitness enthusiast, I have tried a wide range of exercise routines over the years, including traditional low intensity cardio and high intensity cardio. While I enjoy both forms of exercise, I have found that high intensity cardio is much more effective at helping me achieve my fitness goals.

When I first started doing high intensity cardio, I was intimidated by the intensity of the workout. However, I quickly learned to love the challenge and the rush of endorphins that came with each session. Not only did I notice improvements in my cardiovascular fitness, but I also saw a significant decrease in my body fat percentage and an increase in my overall strength.

The Benefits of High Intensity Cardio

There are many potential benefits to high intensity cardio, including:

Improved Cardiovascular Fitness: High intensity cardio can help improve your heart health by increasing your heart rate and oxygen consumption.

Burn More Calories: High intensity cardio can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time than traditional low intensity cardio.

Increased Metabolism: High intensity cardio can help increase your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories even when you're not working out.

Improved Body Composition: High intensity cardio can help decrease body fat percentage and increase muscle mass, leading to a more toned and fit appearance.

The Risks of High Intensity Cardio

While there are many potential benefits to high intensity cardio, it is important to approach this type of exercise with caution. Some potential risks of high intensity cardio include:

Injury: High intensity cardio can put a lot of stress on your joints and muscles, increasing the risk of injury if not done properly.

Overtraining: Because of the intense nature of high intensity cardio, it is important to give your body enough time to rest and recover between workouts to prevent overtraining.

How to Incorporate High Intensity Cardio into Your Routine

Before starting any new workout routine, it is important to consult with a healthcare professional to ensure that you are healthy enough to engage in high intensity exercise. Once you have received clearance from your doctor, you can begin incorporating high intensity cardio into your routine by:

Starting Slowly: If you are new to high intensity cardio, it is important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts over time.

Using Proper Form: To avoid injury, it is important to use proper form when performing high intensity cardio exercises.

Listening to Your Body: If you experience pain or discomfort during a high intensity cardio workout, it is important to stop and rest before continuing.

FAQs About High Intensity Cardio

Q: Is high intensity cardio better than low intensity cardio for weight loss?

A: While both types of cardio can be effective for weight loss, high intensity cardio can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time than low intensity cardio.

Q: How often should I do high intensity cardio?

A: It is recommended that you engage in high intensity cardio workouts 2-3 times per week, with at least one day of rest in between each workout.

Q: Can high intensity cardio help me build muscle?

A: While high intensity cardio is not typically used for muscle building, it can help increase muscle tone and improve overall body composition.

Q: Is high intensity cardio safe for everyone?

A: High intensity cardio can be safe for most people, but it is important to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new workout routine.

Conclusion of Is It Better to Do High Intensity Cardio

In conclusion, high intensity cardio can be a highly effective way to burn calories, improve cardiovascular fitness, and achieve your fitness goals. However, it is important to approach this type of exercise with caution and to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new workout routine. With proper form and a gradual increase in intensity, high intensity cardio can help you achieve the results you desire.
