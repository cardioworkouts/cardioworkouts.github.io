Do I Have To Do Cardio After Weights

Written by Frank Nov 06, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you feel clueless about whether you should do cardio after weights? Are you unsure about the benefits of this practice? If you're someone who is trying to get fit or build muscle, you may have thought about incorporating cardio into your workout routine. However, the idea of doing cardio after lifting weights might seem daunting. If you're confused about whether you need to do cardio after weights, keep reading to find out.

If you're someone who is trying to lose weight or gain muscle, you may have heard that doing cardio after weights is essential. However, you might not be aware of the reasons behind this. It's a common misconception that cardio should only be done before weightlifting. But in reality, doing cardio after weights can be just as beneficial for your body.

The answer to the question, "Do I have to do cardio after weights?" is that it depends on your fitness goals. If you're aiming to lose weight, doing cardio after weights can help you burn more calories and fat. On the other hand, if you want to build muscle, doing cardio after weights can help you burn the excess fat and reveal the muscles you've built.

In conclusion, doing cardio after weights can be beneficial for both weight loss and muscle building goals. It can help you burn more calories and fat, reveal your muscles, and increase your overall fitness level.

How Can Doing Cardio After Weights Benefit You?

As someone who has been working out for a while now, I can tell you that doing cardio after weights has several benefits. When I started incorporating cardio into my workout routine, I noticed a significant change in my body. My muscles became more defined, and I was able to burn more calories and fat. Here are some reasons why doing cardio after weights can benefit you:

1. Burns More Calories and Fat

Doing cardio after lifting weights can help you burn more calories and fat. When you lift weights, you use up the energy stored in your muscles. Doing cardio after weights can help you burn the excess energy stored in your body, which in turn helps you burn more calories and fat.

2. Reveals Your Muscles

If you're someone who is trying to build muscle, doing cardio after weights can help you reveal your muscles. When you lift weights, you build muscle mass. However, to reveal your muscles, you need to burn the excess fat stored in your body. Doing cardio after weights can help you burn this excess fat and reveal the muscles you've built.

3. Increases Your Fitness Level

Doing cardio after weights can help you increase your overall fitness level. When you do cardio, you increase your endurance and stamina. This, in turn, can help you lift heavier weights and perform more reps during your weightlifting sessions.

4. Improves Your Heart Health

Doing cardio after weights can also improve your heart health. Cardiovascular exercises can help you lower your blood pressure, reduce your risk of heart disease, and improve your overall cardiovascular health.

Question and Answer

Q. Is it better to do cardio before or after weights?

A. It depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle, it's better to do weights before cardio. However, if you want to lose weight, doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories and fat.

Q. How long should I do cardio after weights?

A. It's recommended to do cardio for at least 20-30 minutes after weights. However, the duration can vary depending on your fitness level and goals.

Q. What type of cardio should I do after weights?

A. The type of cardio you should do after weights depends on your fitness level and goals. Some good options include running, cycling, swimming, or using the elliptical machine.

Q. Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day. However, it's important to give your body enough time to rest and recover between workouts.

Conclusion of Do I Have to Do Cardio After Weights

Doing cardio after weights can be beneficial for both weight loss and muscle building goals. It can help you burn more calories and fat, reveal your muscles, increase your overall fitness level, and improve your heart health. However, it's important to remember that the type and duration of cardio you do after weights depend on your fitness level and goals. If you're unsure about how to incorporate cardio into your workout routine, consult a fitness expert.
