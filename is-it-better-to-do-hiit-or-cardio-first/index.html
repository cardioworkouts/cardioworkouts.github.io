Is It Better To Do Hiit Or Cardio First

Written by Bardi Sep 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you one of those people who's unsure whether you should do HIIT or cardio first? You're not alone. Many people are confused about the order of their workout regimen. In this article, we'll discuss everything you need to know about whether it's better to do HIIT or cardio first.

Pain Points of HIIT or Cardio First

One of the major pain points of HIIT or Cardio first is that people are confused about which one to start with. They don't know which one is more effective or which one will burn more calories. It's a common question among gym-goers and fitness enthusiasts.

Answer to HIIT or Cardio First Target

The answer to whether you should do HIIT or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to increase endurance, burn more calories, and improve cardiovascular health, then cardio should be done first. However, if your goal is to improve overall fitness, build strength and muscle, and burn fat, then HIIT should be done first.

It's important to note that both HIIT and cardio have their benefits, and the order in which you perform them depends on your fitness goals.

Summary of HIIT or Cardio First Main Points

In summary, the order in which you should perform HIIT or cardio depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve your cardiovascular health, burn more calories, and increase endurance, then cardio should be done first. However, if you want to improve overall fitness, build strength and muscle, and burn fat, then HIIT should be done first.

Personal Experience with HIIT or Cardio First

Personally, I like to start my workout with HIIT. I find that it gets my heart rate up and helps me to warm up my muscles before moving on to cardio. However, I always end my workout with a cardio session to cool down and stretch my muscles.

Benefits of Doing HIIT First

One of the benefits of doing HIIT first is that it helps you to burn more calories during your workout. HIIT is a more intense workout than cardio, and it can help you to increase your metabolism, which means you'll continue to burn calories even after your workout is over. Additionally, starting with HIIT can help you to build muscle and strength, which can assist in burning more fat.

Factors to Consider When Doing HIIT First

When doing HIIT first, it's important to consider your fitness level. HIIT is a more intense workout than cardio, and if you're not used to high-intensity workouts, you may find it challenging. It's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity over time. Also, make sure to incorporate rest days into your workout regimen to allow your body to recover.

Tips for Doing HIIT First

If you're going to start with HIIT first, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Make sure to warm up before starting your workout
  • Start slowly and gradually increase your intensity over time
  • Incorporate rest days into your workout regimen
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout
  • Be mindful of your form to avoid injury

Personal Experience with Cardio First

On the other hand, some people prefer to start with cardio before moving on to HIIT. This can be beneficial if your goal is to improve cardiovascular health and endurance. Personally, I find that starting with cardio helps me to warm up my muscles and get my heart rate up. It also helps me to feel more energized during my HIIT workout.

Benefits of Doing Cardio First

One of the benefits of doing cardio first is that it helps you to improve your endurance and cardiovascular health. Cardio is a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. Additionally, starting with cardio can help you to improve your mental focus and energy levels, which can make your HIIT workout more effective.

Factors to Consider When Doing Cardio First

When doing cardio first, it's important to consider your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle and strength, then starting with cardio may not be the best option. Additionally, it's important to incorporate rest days into your workout regimen and gradually increase your intensity over time.

Tips for Doing Cardio First

If you're going to start with cardio first, here are some tips to keep in mind:

  • Make sure to warm up before starting your workout
  • Gradually increase your intensity over time
  • Incorporate rest days into your workout regimen
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout
  • Be mindful of your form to avoid injury

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do both HIIT and cardio in one workout?

A: Yes, you can do both HIIT and cardio in one workout. However, it's important to consider your fitness goals and schedule your workout accordingly.

Q: How long should I do HIIT or cardio?

A: The length of your HIIT or cardio workout depends on your fitness level and goals. It's recommended to start with 20-30 minutes and gradually increase your duration over time.

Q: Which is better for weight loss, HIIT or cardio?

A: Both HIIT and cardio can be effective for weight loss. It's important to consider your fitness goals and schedule your workout accordingly.

Q: Can I do HIIT or cardio every day?

A: It's not recommended to do HIIT or cardio every day. It's important to incorporate rest days into your workout regimen to allow your body to recover.

Conclusion of HIIT or Cardio First

In conclusion, whether you should do HIIT or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to improve cardiovascular health, burn more calories, and increase endurance, then cardio should be done first. However, if you want to improve overall fitness, build strength and muscle, and burn fat, then HIIT should be done first. Regardless of which one you choose to do first, it's important to incorporate both HIIT and cardio into your workout regimen for maximum benefits.
