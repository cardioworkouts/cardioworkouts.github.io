Should You Mix Cardio With Strength Training

Written by William Nov 27, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you trying to figure out if you should mix cardio with strength training? Many people wonder if combining these two types of exercise is beneficial or if it's better to focus on one or the other.

The decision to mix cardio with strength training largely depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. However, before making a decision, it's important to understand what each type of exercise entails and the benefits they offer.

Cardio and strength training are both important components of a well-rounded fitness routine. Cardio exercises, such as running, swimming, and cycling, primarily work to improve cardiovascular health and endurance. Strength training, on the other hand, focuses on building muscle mass and improving strength.

Incorporating both cardio and strength training into your workouts can provide a wide range of benefits. Cardio exercises can help you burn calories and fat, while strength training helps build lean muscle mass which leads to a faster metabolism.

My Personal Experience

As a fitness enthusiast, I have tried different workout routines over the years. I used to focus solely on cardio exercises to maintain my weight, but I soon realized that incorporating strength training helped me achieve my fitness goals faster. I was able to build more muscle mass and improve my overall strength, which ultimately helped me burn more calories and maintain a healthy weight.

The Benefits of Mixing Cardio and Strength Training

Combining cardio and strength training can help you achieve your fitness goals in a more efficient way. Some of the benefits of mixing these two types of exercise include:

  • Improved cardiovascular health
  • Increased muscle mass and strength
  • Reduced risk of injury
  • Improved metabolism and calorie burning
  • Enhanced overall fitness and endurance

How to Mix Cardio and Strength Training

The key to effectively mixing cardio and strength training is to vary your routine and focus on intensity. You can alternate between cardio and strength training on different days, or you can incorporate both types of exercises into the same workout. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is another effective way to combine cardio and strength training.

How to Get Started

If you're new to mixing cardio and strength training, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity. Consult a fitness professional to create a workout plan that's tailored to your fitness level and goals.

FAQs About Mixing Cardio and Strength Training

Q: Is it better to do cardio or strength training first?

A: The order in which you do cardio and strength training largely depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, it's best to start with strength training. If you want to improve endurance, start with cardio.

Q: How often should I mix cardio and strength training?

A: It's recommended to mix cardio and strength training at least three times a week for optimal results.

Q: Can I mix cardio and strength training in the same workout?

A: Yes, you can incorporate both types of exercises into the same workout. However, it's important to vary your routine and focus on intensity to achieve maximum benefits.

Q: Is mixing cardio and strength training suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, beginners can mix cardio and strength training, but they should start slowly and gradually increase their intensity. It's also recommended to consult a fitness professional to create a workout plan that's tailored to their fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of Should You Mix Cardio with Strength Training

Mixing cardio and strength training can provide a wide range of benefits and help you achieve your fitness goals in a more efficient way. However, it's important to vary your routine, focus on intensity, and consult a fitness professional to create a workout plan that's tailored to your fitness level and goals.
