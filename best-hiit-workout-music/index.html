Best Hiit Workout Music Pump Up Your Workouts With The Right Tunes

Written by Bardi Oct 10, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you tired of feeling sluggish during your HIIT workouts? Are you struggling to stay motivated and push yourself to your limits? One simple solution may be to switch up your workout music. The right tunes can make all the difference when it comes to getting in the zone and maximizing your results.

When it comes to finding the best HIIT workout music, there are a few things to consider. First and foremost, you want songs that have a high tempo and a strong beat to help keep you energized and focused. You also want to choose music that you enjoy and that motivates you - whether that's uplifting pop hits, hard-hitting rap tracks, or anything in between.

In this article, we'll explore some of the best HIIT workout music out there, as well as tips for creating your own custom playlists that will take your workouts to the next level.

The Power of Music: My Personal Experience

As someone who has been doing HIIT workouts for years, I can attest to the power of a great workout playlist. When I have the right tunes blasting in my headphones, I feel like I can push myself harder and longer than ever before. The beat of the music helps me stay on pace and keeps me from getting distracted or bored.

Over the years, I've experimented with a variety of different genres and artists to find the best HIIT workout music for my personal taste. Some of my favorite artists to listen to during a high-intensity workout include Eminem, Skrillex, and Beyonce. I also love remixes of popular songs that have a faster tempo or a more intense beat.

Top Playlists for HIIT Workouts

If you're not sure where to start when it comes to building your own HIIT workout playlist, there are plenty of pre-made playlists out there that can give you some inspiration. Here are a few of our favorites:

1. "HIIT Workout Music" by Filtr

This playlist on Spotify features over 100 high-energy tracks from a variety of genres, including pop, hip-hop, and EDM. With artists like Dua Lipa, Kanye West, and The Weeknd, you're sure to find something that gets you pumped up and ready to crush your workout.

2. "HIIT Workout" by Apple Music

This playlist on Apple Music features a mix of upbeat pop hits and high-energy EDM tracks. With over 50 songs to choose from, you're sure to find something that keeps you motivated and moving.

3. "Beast Mode" by Spotify

This playlist on Spotify is specifically designed for high-intensity workouts, with tracks that have a BPM (beats per minute) of 130 or higher. With artists like Cardi B, Drake, and Lil Wayne, you'll have plenty of hard-hitting tracks to choose from.

Tips for Creating Your Own HIIT Workout Playlist

If you prefer to create your own custom playlist for your HIIT workouts, here are a few tips to keep in mind:

1. Choose songs with a high BPM.

As we mentioned earlier, songs with a high tempo and a strong beat are ideal for HIIT workouts. Look for songs with a BPM of 130 or higher to keep you energized and motivated throughout your workout.

2. Mix up the genres.

Don't be afraid to mix up different genres in your playlist to keep things interesting. You might include some hip-hop tracks, some EDM hits, and some pop songs, for example.

3. Include songs that motivate you.

Choose songs that you love and that motivate you to push yourself harder. Whether it's a favorite artist or a song with inspiring lyrics, including these types of songs in your playlist can help keep you focused and driven.

Conclusion of Best HIIT Workout Music

When it comes to getting the most out of your HIIT workouts, the right music can make all the difference. Whether you opt for pre-made playlists or create your own custom playlist, be sure to choose songs with a high BPM and a strong beat to help keep you energized and focused. With the right tunes blasting in your headphones, you'll be able to push yourself harder and achieve your fitness goals in no time.

Question and Answer Section:

Q: What are some benefits of listening to music during a HIIT workout?

A: Listening to music during a HIIT workout can help increase your motivation and focus, allowing you to push yourself harder and achieve better results. It can also help distract you from any discomfort or fatigue you might be feeling, allowing you to power through your workout more easily.

Q: How can I find the best HIIT workout music for my personal taste?

A: Experiment with different genres and artists to find what works best for you. Consider creating a playlist featuring your favorite songs and tracks with a high BPM to help keep you energized and motivated.

Q: Are there any downsides to listening to music during a HIIT workout?

A: One potential downside is that you may become too reliant on music to motivate you during your workouts. It's important to learn how to push yourself without the aid of music, as you may not always have access to it during your workouts.

Q: How can I create a playlist that's the right length for my HIIT workout?

A: Consider the length of your workout and choose songs accordingly. For example, if your HIIT workout is 30 minutes long, you might choose a playlist with 10-12 songs that are each around 2-3 minutes in length.
