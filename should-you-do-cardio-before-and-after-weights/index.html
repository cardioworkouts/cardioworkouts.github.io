Should You Do Cardio Before Or After Weights

Written by William Sep 23, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who is torn between doing cardio before or after weight lifting? Are you confused about which one to prioritize? If yes, then you've come to the right place. In this blog post, we will discuss whether you should do cardio before or after weights and help you make an informed decision.

Pain Points Related to Cardio Before or After Weights

One of the most common pain points related to cardio before or after weights is time management. With our busy lives, it can be challenging to decide which one to prioritize and for how long. Another pain point is figuring out which one is more effective in achieving your fitness goals.

Answering the Question of Cardio Before or After Weights

The answer to this question is that it depends on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to lose weight, then doing cardio before weights can help you burn more calories. However, if you are looking to build muscle and strength, then doing weights before cardio is recommended. This is because if you tire out your muscles with cardio before lifting weights, you might not be able to give your best effort during weight lifting.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, whether you should do cardio before or after weights depends on your fitness goals. If you want to lose weight, do cardio before weights. If you want to build muscle and strength, do weights before cardio.

Benefits of Doing Cardio Before Weights

Personally, I prefer doing cardio before weights as it helps me warm up my body and get my heart rate up before lifting weights. It also helps me burn more calories during my workout, which is essential in achieving my weight loss goals. Additionally, doing cardio before weights can help me maintain my endurance during my workout, allowing me to lift weights for longer durations.

Benefits of Doing Weights Before Cardio

On the other hand, doing weights before cardio can help improve your strength and muscle growth. By lifting weights first, you are allowing your muscles to work at their full potential, leading to better results. Additionally, lifting weights first can help you burn more calories post-workout as your muscles continue to work to repair themselves.

Tips for Combining Cardio and Weights

If you are someone who wants to combine cardio and weights, it's essential to plan your workouts accordingly. Here are a few tips to help you get started:

  • Start with a light warm-up to get your muscles ready for the workout.
  • Do weights first if your main goal is strength and muscle building.
  • Do cardio first if your main goal is weight loss.
  • Keep your workouts balanced and don't overdo it with either cardio or weights.
  • Stay hydrated throughout your workout, especially if you are doing cardio.

How Long Should You Do Cardio and Weights?

When it comes to the duration of your cardio and weight lifting, it depends on your fitness goals and availability. However, it's recommended to do at least 30 minutes of cardio and 30 minutes of weight lifting per workout session. If you are short on time, you can combine both and do a 30-minute circuit workout that includes both cardio and weights.

My Personal Experience

Personally, I like to mix things up and do both cardio and weights in the same workout session. However, I always make sure to prioritize my fitness goals and do what's best for my body. If my main goal is weight loss, I start with cardio and then move on to weights. On the other hand, if my goal is to build muscle and strength, I start with weights and then do cardio.

Question and Answer Section

Q. Can you do cardio and weights on the same day?

A. Yes, it's possible to do both cardio and weights on the same day. Just make sure to plan your workout accordingly and prioritize your fitness goals.

Q. Is it bad to do cardio after weights?

A. No, it's not bad to do cardio after weights. However, if your main goal is to build muscle and strength, it's recommended to do weights first.

Q. How long should you wait to do cardio after weights?

A. You don't need to wait to do cardio after weights. However, it's recommended to take a short break between the two to recover your breath and heart rate.

Q. Is it better to lift weights or do cardio first?

A. It depends on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to lose weight, do cardio first. If your goal is to build muscle and strength, do weights first.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before or After Weights

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after weights depends on your fitness goals. Both cardio and weights are essential in achieving your fitness goals, and it's essential to find a balance between the two. Remember to prioritize your fitness goals and listen to your body to determine the best workout routine for you.
