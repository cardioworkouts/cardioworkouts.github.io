What Counts As A High Intensity Workout A Beginner s Guide

Written by Luffy Jul 13, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to take your fitness routine to the next level? Maybe you've heard about high intensity workouts, but you're not quite sure what they entail. Don't worry, we've got you covered with this beginner's guide to what counts as a high intensity workout.

When it comes to fitness, everyone has different goals and limitations. Some people are looking to lose weight, while others want to build muscle. Regardless of your individual goals, a high intensity workout can be a great way to challenge yourself and improve your overall fitness level. However, it's important to understand what counts as a high intensity workout before you jump in.

A high intensity workout is any exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or active recovery. These workouts are designed to get your heart rate up and challenge your body in a short amount of time. Some examples of high intensity workouts include HIIT (high intensity interval training), circuit training, and Tabata training. These workouts can be done with bodyweight exercises, weights, or cardio equipment.

In summary, a high intensity workout is any exercise that involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest or active recovery. These workouts can be done with a variety of exercises and equipment, and are designed to challenge your body in a short amount of time.

A Personal Experience with HIIT

As someone who has tried a variety of workout routines over the years, I was skeptical about trying high intensity workouts. However, I decided to give it a try and was pleasantly surprised by the results. I started doing HIIT workouts at home with just bodyweight exercises, and quickly noticed an improvement in my overall fitness level. I felt stronger and more energized, and I also noticed that my endurance had improved. While the workouts were challenging, they were also incredibly rewarding.

If you're new to high intensity workouts, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity as your fitness level improves. You don't want to push yourself too hard and risk injury. It's also important to listen to your body and take rest days when needed.

The Benefits of High Intensity Workouts

There are many benefits to incorporating high intensity workouts into your fitness routine. For one, they can help you burn more calories in a shorter amount of time than traditional steady-state cardio. High intensity workouts also challenge your body in a way that can lead to increased muscle tone and strength. Additionally, these workouts can improve your cardiovascular health and endurance.

How to Incorporate High Intensity Workouts into Your Routine

If you're interested in incorporating high intensity workouts into your routine, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, it's important to choose workouts that are appropriate for your fitness level. There are many beginner-friendly HIIT workouts available online that can help you get started. It's also important to vary your workouts to avoid boredom and prevent injury. Finally, make sure to fuel your body properly before and after your workouts to ensure that you have enough energy to perform at your best.

The Importance of Recovery

While high intensity workouts can be incredibly effective, it's important to give your body time to recover. In fact, recovery is just as important as the workouts themselves. Make sure to take rest days, stretch regularly, and prioritize sleep to allow your body to recover and repair itself. This will help you avoid injury and ensure that you're able to perform at your best during your workouts.

My Favorite High Intensity Workouts

There are so many different high intensity workouts to choose from, but here are a few of my personal favorites:

1. HIIT Cardio

This workout involves short bursts of cardio exercises (such as jumping jacks, high knees, or mountain climbers) followed by periods of rest or active recovery. It's a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories.

2. Tabata Training

Tabata training involves doing 20 seconds of work followed by 10 seconds of rest for eight rounds. You can do this with any exercise, but some popular options include burpees, squats, and push-ups.

3. Circuit Training

Circuit training involves doing a series of exercises (usually 5-10) for a certain amount of time or reps, with little to no rest in between. This is a great way to challenge your body and work multiple muscle groups at once.

4. EMOM Workouts

EMOM stands for "every minute on the minute." These workouts involve doing a set number of reps of a certain exercise at the start of every minute, with the remaining time in the minute used for rest. This is a great way to challenge yourself and improve your endurance.

Question and Answer

Q: Are high intensity workouts safe for beginners?

A: High intensity workouts can be safe for beginners as long as they start slowly and gradually increase the intensity over time. It's also important to choose workouts that are appropriate for your fitness level and to listen to your body.

Q: How often should I do high intensity workouts?

A: It's generally recommended to do high intensity workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between. This will give your body time to recover and prevent injury.

Q: Do I need any special equipment to do high intensity workouts?

A: While some high intensity workouts may require equipment (such as weights or cardio machines), many can be done with just your bodyweight. There are also many beginner-friendly workouts available online that require little to no equipment.

Q: How long should a high intensity workout be?

A: High intensity workouts can be as short as 10-15 minutes or as long as an hour, depending on your fitness level and goals. It's important to choose workouts that are appropriate for your individual needs.

Conclusion of What Counts as a High Intensity Workout

If you're looking to challenge yourself and take your fitness routine to the next level, high intensity workouts may be just what you need. By incorporating short bursts of intense activity into your routine, you can improve your overall fitness level and achieve your goals. Just remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and give yourself time to recover between workouts.
