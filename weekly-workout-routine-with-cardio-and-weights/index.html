Weekly Workout Routine With Cardio And Weights A Beginner s Guide

Written by Robby Jul 12, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking to get fit and healthy but don't know where to start? Weekly workout routine with cardio and weights can be daunting for beginners, but it doesn't have to be. With the right plan and mindset, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy the journey along the way.

Many people struggle with finding the right balance between cardio and weights in their weekly workout routine. They may feel overwhelmed by the variety of exercises and equipment available at the gym or unsure of how to structure their workouts for optimal results. Additionally, they may feel discouraged by the physical and mental challenges of starting a new fitness routine.

The target of a weekly workout routine with cardio and weights is to improve your overall fitness level, including your strength, endurance, and cardiovascular health. By combining these two types of exercise, you can create a well-rounded program that targets different muscle groups and energy systems in your body.

In this article, we will summarize the main points of a weekly workout routine with cardio and weights, provide tips for getting started, and answer common questions about this type of fitness program.

What is Cardio and Why is it Important?

Cardio, short for cardiovascular exercise, is any type of physical activity that increases your heart rate and breathing rate. Examples include running, cycling, swimming, and dancing. The goal of cardio is to improve your endurance and stamina, which can help you perform daily activities with less fatigue and improve your overall health.

When you do cardio, your body burns calories and fat, which can help you lose weight or maintain a healthy weight. Additionally, cardio can improve your heart health by reducing your risk of heart disease, stroke, and other chronic conditions.

What are Weights and Why are They Important?

Weights, also known as strength training or resistance training, involve using weights or resistance bands to strengthen your muscles. Examples include lifting dumbbells, doing push-ups, or using a weight machine at the gym. The goal of weights is to improve your muscular strength and endurance, which can help you perform daily activities with less effort and reduce your risk of injury.

When you do weights, your body burns calories and builds muscle, which can help you lose weight or maintain a healthy weight. Additionally, weights can improve your bone density and joint health, reducing your risk of osteoporosis and arthritis.

How to Create a Weekly Workout Routine with Cardio and Weights

To create a weekly workout routine with cardio and weights, you should aim to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio and two to three strength training sessions per week. Here's an example of how you could structure your workouts:

  • Monday: 30 minutes of running or cycling + upper body weights
  • Wednesday: 30 minutes of swimming or dancing + lower body weights
  • Friday: 30 minutes of rowing or elliptical machine + full body weights

Make sure to warm up before each workout and cool down afterwards. You can also add in flexibility or balance exercises as needed. Remember to listen to your body and adjust the intensity or frequency of your workouts as needed.

Tips for Getting Started

If you're new to weekly workout routine with cardio and weights, here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Set realistic goals and track your progress
  • Find a workout buddy or join a fitness class for motivation
  • Invest in proper workout gear and shoes
  • Eat a balanced diet and stay hydrated
  • Get enough rest and recovery time

Common Questions About Weekly Workout Routine with Cardio and Weights

Q: Should I do cardio or weights first?

A: It depends on your goals and preferences. Some people prefer to do cardio first to warm up and burn calories, while others prefer to do weights first to build strength and endurance. As long as you do both types of exercise, the order doesn't matter.

Q: How often should I change my workout routine?

A: It's good to switch up your routine every 4-6 weeks to keep challenging your body and prevent boredom. You can change the types of cardio or weights you do, the number of sets and reps, or the intensity level.

Q: Can I do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do both types of exercise on the same day as long as you give your muscles enough time to recover between workouts. You may want to alternate cardio and weights or do them at different times of the day.

Q: How long should I rest between sets?

A: It depends on the intensity and type of exercise you're doing. As a general rule, you should rest 30-60 seconds between sets for strength training and 60-90 seconds between cardio intervals. However, you can adjust the rest time based on your fitness level and goals.

Conclusion of Weekly Workout Routine with Cardio and Weights

A weekly workout routine with cardio and weights can be a fun and effective way to improve your overall fitness and health. By following the tips and guidelines in this article, you can create a customized program that works for your goals and lifestyle. Remember to stay consistent, stay motivated, and enjoy the journey!
