How Many Calories Does A 20 Minute Hiit Workout Burn

Written by Bardi Nov 20, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a fast and effective way to burn calories and get in shape? High-Intensity Interval Training (HIIT) might just be the solution you need. But, how many calories does a 20 minute HIIT workout burn? Let's explore this topic below.

HIIT is a type of workout that involves short bursts of intense exercise alternated with periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. The goal is to increase your heart rate and keep it elevated for the duration of the workout. HIIT workouts are typically shorter than traditional cardio workouts, but they can be more effective at burning calories and improving cardiovascular health.

So, how many calories does a 20 minute HIIT workout burn? The answer depends on several factors, including your weight, fitness level, and the intensity of the workout. On average, a person can burn anywhere from 200-400 calories during a 20 minute HIIT workout.

In order to maximize your calorie burn during a HIIT workout, it's important to push yourself to your limits during the high-intensity intervals. You should also focus on using large muscle groups, such as your legs, to get the most out of each exercise. Additionally, adding resistance with weights or resistance bands can also increase the calorie burn.

Personal Experience:

As someone who regularly incorporates HIIT workouts into my fitness routine, I can attest to the calorie-burning benefits. I typically burn around 300 calories during a 20 minute HIIT workout, but this can vary depending on the specific exercises and intensity level. I find that incorporating both cardio and strength exercises into my HIIT workouts helps to keep my heart rate elevated and maximize my calorie burn.

Tips for Increasing Calorie Burn:

If you're looking to increase your calorie burn during a HIIT workout, there are a few tips you can try:

1. Increase the Intensity:

The higher the intensity of your workout, the more calories you will burn. Try to push yourself to your limits during the high-intensity intervals, and take shorter rest periods to keep your heart rate elevated.

2. Incorporate Weights:

Adding weights or resistance bands to your exercises can help to increase the calorie burn and build muscle at the same time.

Personal Experience:

When I first started doing HIIT workouts, I found it challenging to push myself to my limits during the high-intensity intervals. But, as I continued to do the workouts, I found that I was able to increase the intensity and burn more calories. Adding weights to my exercises also helped to increase the calorie burn and improve my overall fitness level.

FAQs:

Q: Can I do HIIT workouts every day?

A: It's not recommended to do HIIT workouts every day, as your body needs time to recover between workouts. Aim to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, and incorporate other forms of exercise, such as strength training and cardio, on the other days.

Q: How long should I do a HIIT workout for?

A: HIIT workouts can range from 10-30 minutes, depending on your fitness level and goals. Aim to do a 20 minute HIIT workout to maximize calorie burn and improve cardiovascular health.

Q: What are some good exercises to include in a HIIT workout?

A: Some good exercises to include in a HIIT workout include burpees, jumping jacks, mountain climbers, and squat jumps. Focus on using large muscle groups and incorporating both cardio and strength exercises.

Q: Can I do HIIT workouts if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, HIIT workouts can be modified for beginners by decreasing the intensity and duration of the high-intensity intervals. Always listen to your body and modify the exercises as needed.

Conclusion:

In conclusion, a 20 minute HIIT workout can burn anywhere from 200-400 calories, depending on your weight, fitness level, and intensity. By incorporating HIIT workouts into your fitness routine and pushing yourself to your limits during the high-intensity intervals, you can maximize your calorie burn and improve your overall health and fitness.
