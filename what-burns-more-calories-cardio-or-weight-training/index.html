What Burns More Calories Cardio Or Weight Training

Written by Daniel Jun 27, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you looking for an effective way to burn calories and achieve your fitness goals? One of the most common questions that people have is whether cardio or weight training is more effective for burning calories. In this post, we'll explore the benefits of both and help you decide which one is right for you.

Understanding the Pain Points

Many people struggle with weight loss and achieving their fitness goals. They may spend hours in the gym without seeing the results they want, or they may feel overwhelmed by the conflicting advice they receive about what type of exercise is best for burning calories. It's important to understand that everyone's body is different, and what works for one person may not work for another.

Answering the Question

When it comes to burning calories, cardio is generally more effective than weight training. Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming are excellent for increasing your heart rate and boosting your metabolism, which helps you burn more calories. However, weight training can also be effective for burning calories, especially if you incorporate high-intensity interval training (HIIT) into your routine.

The best approach is to combine both cardio and weight training for optimal results. This will help you build lean muscle mass, boost your metabolism, and burn more calories both during and after your workout.

Main Points Summarized

Cardio is generally more effective for burning calories than weight training, but both can be effective when combined. The key is to find a workout routine that works for you.

Personal Experience with Cardio

When I started my fitness journey, I focused primarily on cardio exercises such as running and cycling. I found that these exercises helped me burn a lot of calories and lose weight quickly. However, I also noticed that I was losing muscle mass and not seeing the toning and definition that I wanted.

After doing some research, I started incorporating weight training into my routine. I found that this helped me build lean muscle mass, which in turn boosted my metabolism and helped me burn more calories even when I wasn't working out. By combining cardio and weight training, I was able to achieve my fitness goals and maintain my weight over the long term.

Personal Experience with Weight Training

When I first started weight training, I was intimidated by the equipment and the thought of lifting heavy weights. However, I quickly found that weight training was an effective way to build muscle and burn calories. By focusing on compound exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench presses, I was able to work multiple muscle groups at once and achieve maximum results.

I also found that incorporating high-intensity interval training (HIIT) into my weight training routine helped me burn more calories and achieve my fitness goals more quickly. By alternating between periods of high-intensity exercise and rest, I was able to keep my heart rate elevated and maximize my calorie burn.

Going Deeper into Cardio vs. Weight Training

Cardio exercises such as running, cycling, and swimming are excellent for burning calories and improving cardiovascular health. These exercises increase your heart rate and respiration, which helps you burn calories and improve your endurance. They are also great for reducing stress and improving your mood.

Weight training, on the other hand, is excellent for building lean muscle mass and improving your physical strength. By lifting weights, you challenge your muscles and force them to adapt and grow. This helps you burn more calories both during and after your workout, as your body works to repair and rebuild the muscle tissue.

How to Incorporate Both into Your Routine

The best way to incorporate both cardio and weight training into your routine is to create a balanced workout program that includes both types of exercise. You should aim to do at least 30 minutes of cardio exercise per day, such as running or cycling, and incorporate weight training exercises such as squats, lunges, and push-ups into your routine 2-3 times per week.

You can also incorporate HIIT into your cardio and weight training routines to maximize your calorie burn and achieve your fitness goals more quickly.

Personal Experience with Cardio and Weight Training Combined

By combining cardio and weight training into my routine, I was able to achieve my fitness goals and maintain my weight over the long term. I found that doing 30 minutes of cardio exercise per day, such as running or cycling, and incorporating weight training exercises into my routine 2-3 times per week helped me build lean muscle mass, boost my metabolism, and burn more calories both during and after my workout.

Question and Answer

Q1: Is cardio or weight training better for burning fat?

A1: Cardio is generally more effective for burning fat, but weight training can also be effective when combined with cardio.

Q2: How many calories can I burn with cardio?

A2: The number of calories you can burn with cardio depends on the type of exercise you do, your intensity level, and your body weight. On average, you can expect to burn between 300-600 calories per hour with cardio.

Q3: How many calories can I burn with weight training?

A3: The number of calories you can burn with weight training depends on the type of exercise you do, your intensity level, and your body weight. On average, you can expect to burn between 100-200 calories per hour with weight training.

Q4: How often should I do cardio and weight training?

A4: You should aim to do at least 30 minutes of cardio exercise per day, such as running or cycling, and incorporate weight training exercises into your routine 2-3 times per week.

Conclusion

Cardio and weight training are both effective for burning calories and achieving your fitness goals. The best approach is to combine both types of exercise into a balanced workout program that works for you. By doing so, you can build lean muscle mass, boost your metabolism, and achieve optimal results over the long term.
