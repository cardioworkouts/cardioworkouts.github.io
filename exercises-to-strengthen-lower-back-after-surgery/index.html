Exercises To Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Frank Sep 26, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Lower back surgery can be a life-altering experience, but the recovery process can be even more challenging. One of the most crucial aspects of post-surgery recovery is exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery. However, this can be painful and intimidating, especially for those who are not familiar with exercises. In this guide, we will discuss the target of these exercises and provide a step-by-step guide on how to perform them effectively.

After lower back surgery, the muscles in the lower back often become weak and stiff, leading to discomfort and pain. Exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery can help reduce pain, improve flexibility, and increase the overall strength of the muscles in the lower back. These exercises can also help prevent future injuries and improve posture.

The Target of Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery

The primary target of exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery is to improve the stability, strength, and flexibility of the muscles in the lower back. These exercises focus on the core muscles, including the lower back, abdominals, and glutes. They also help to improve posture, reduce pain, and prevent future injuries.

It's important to consult with a physical therapist or healthcare provider before starting any exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery. They can help create a personalized plan based on the specific surgery and recovery process.

Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery: Personal Experience

After my lower back surgery, I was intimidated by the idea of exercising. However, my physical therapist provided me with a set of exercises that were tailored to my needs and abilities. One of the most effective exercises was the pelvic tilt. To perform this exercise, I lay on my back with my knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Then, I tightened my abdominals and glutes, and lifted my hips off the floor. I held this position for a few seconds before lowering my hips back down. This exercise helped improve the strength and flexibility of my lower back muscles, and I noticed a significant reduction in pain.

Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery: Step-by-Step Guide

Here are some effective exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery:

Bridges

Lay on your back with knees bent and feet flat on the floor. Tighten your abdominals and glutes and lift your hips off the floor. Hold this position for a few seconds before lowering your hips back down. Repeat 10-15 times.

Planks

Start in a push-up position with your forearms on the ground. Tighten your abdominals and glutes and hold this position for 30-60 seconds. Repeat 3-5 times.

Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery: Personal Experience

Another exercise that helped me was the cat-cow stretch. To perform this exercise, I got on my hands and knees with my hands directly under my shoulders and my knees directly under my hips. Then, I rounded my back and tuck my chin to my chest, and then arch my back and look up to the ceiling. This exercise helped release tension in my lower back and improve flexibility.

Question and Answer

Q: Can exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery be painful?

A: Yes, these exercises may be uncomfortable or painful at first, but they should not cause sharp or intense pain. If you experience severe pain, stop the exercise and consult your healthcare provider.

Q: How often should I perform exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery?

A: It's important to follow the personalized plan created by your physical therapist or healthcare provider. In general, it's recommended to perform these exercises 2-3 times per week.

Q: How long does it take to see results from exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery?

A: It varies from person to person, but you may start to notice improvements in strength and flexibility within a few weeks of starting these exercises.

Q: Can I perform these exercises at home, or do I need to go to a gym?

A: These exercises can be performed at home with little to no equipment. However, it's important to consult with a physical therapist or healthcare provider to ensure proper form and technique.

Conclusion of Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back After Surgery

Exercises to strengthen lower back after surgery are crucial for a successful recovery process. These exercises can help reduce pain, improve flexibility, and increase the overall strength of the muscles in the lower back. It's important to consult with a physical therapist or healthcare provider before starting any exercises and follow a personalized plan based on your specific surgery and recovery process.
