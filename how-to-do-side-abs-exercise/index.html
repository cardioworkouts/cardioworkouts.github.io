How To Do Side Abs Exercise A Step By Step Guide

Written by Oliver Jul 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Have you been struggling to tone your obliques and get that coveted V-shaped torso? Side abs exercises are a great way to target those muscles and achieve a more defined waistline. In this tutorial, we will guide you through the process of how to do side abs exercise effectively.

Many people find it challenging to work on their side abs, and it can be frustrating when you don't see results. Incorrect form and not targeting the right muscles can lead to injuries and a lack of progress. But don't worry, with the right guidance, you can achieve your fitness goals.

What is Side Abs Exercise?

Side abs exercises, also known as oblique exercises, target the muscles on the sides of your abdomen. These muscles play a crucial role in stabilizing your core and improving your posture. They can also help you achieve a more toned and defined waistline.

One of the most effective ways to target your side abs is through exercises that involve twisting movements. These exercises engage your oblique muscles and help strengthen them.

My Personal Experience with Side Abs Exercises

As someone who has struggled with love handles, I know how frustrating it can be to work on your side abs. But with consistent effort and the right exercises, I was able to see results. One of my favorite exercises is the Russian twist, which involves twisting your torso while holding a weight or medicine ball.

Another great exercise is the side plank, which involves balancing your body on one forearm and lifting your hips off the ground. This exercise not only targets your oblique muscles but also improves your overall core strength.

How to Do Side Abs Exercise: Step-by-Step Guide

Here is a step-by-step guide on how to do side abs exercise:

1. Russian Twist

  1. Start by sitting on the floor with your knees bent and your feet flat on the ground. Hold a weight or medicine ball in front of your chest.
  2. Lean back slightly, keeping your spine straight and your core engaged.
  3. Twist your torso to the right, bringing the weight or medicine ball towards your right hip.
  4. Return to the starting position and twist your torso to the left, bringing the weight or medicine ball towards your left hip.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 reps on each side.

2. Side Plank

  1. Start by lying on your left side with your left forearm on the ground, elbow directly under your shoulder.
  2. Engage your core and lift your hips off the ground, creating a straight line from your head to your feet.
  3. Hold for 30-60 seconds, then switch to the other side and repeat.

3. Bicycle Crunch

  1. Lie on your back with your hands behind your head and your knees bent.
  2. Bring your right elbow towards your left knee while straightening your right leg.
  3. Switch sides, bringing your left elbow towards your right knee while straightening your left leg.
  4. Repeat for 10-12 reps on each side.

4. Woodchopper

  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a weight or medicine ball with both hands.
  2. Twist your torso to the right, bringing the weight or medicine ball towards your right hip.
  3. Engage your core and lift the weight or medicine ball diagonally across your body towards your left shoulder.
  4. Lower the weight or medicine ball back down to your right hip.
  5. Repeat for 10-12 reps on each side.

Question and Answer

Q1. Can side abs exercises help me lose belly fat?

A. While side abs exercises can help strengthen your oblique muscles, they won't directly lead to fat loss. To lose belly fat, you need to focus on overall weight loss through a combination of diet and exercise.

Q2. Are side abs exercises suitable for beginners?

A. Yes, many side abs exercises are beginner-friendly and can be modified to suit your fitness level. Start with exercises like the side plank and bicycle crunch and gradually work your way up to more challenging exercises.

Q3. How often should I do side abs exercises?

A. Aim to do side abs exercises 2-3 times a week as part of your overall fitness routine. It's essential to give your muscles time to rest and recover between workouts.

Q4. Are side abs exercises safe for people with back problems?

A. If you have any pre-existing back problems, it's crucial to consult with a healthcare professional before starting any new exercise routine. Some side abs exercises, like the Russian twist, can put stress on your lower back, so it's essential to use correct form and start with lighter weights.

Conclusion of How to Do Side Abs Exercise

Side abs exercises are a great way to target your oblique muscles and achieve a more defined waistline. By incorporating exercises like the Russian twist, side plank, bicycle crunch, and woodchopper into your workout routine, you can strengthen your core and improve your overall fitness level.

Remember to use correct form, start with lighter weights and consult with a healthcare professional if you have any pre-existing back problems. With consistent effort and the right guidance, you can achieve your fitness goals and get those toned side abs you've been dreaming of.
