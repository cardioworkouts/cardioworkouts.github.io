How Long Should You Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

Written by Christine Sep 11, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you ever wonder how long you should do cardio after weight lifting? Do you feel like you're not making progress despite going to the gym regularly? If you are looking to maximize your workout and get the most out of your time at the gym, then this post is for you.

Many people struggle with the question of how long they should do cardio after weight lifting. It's a common pain point for those who are trying to improve their fitness and lose weight. Doing cardio after weight lifting is a great way to burn extra calories and improve your cardiovascular health, but it's important to do it for the right amount of time.

How Long Should You Do Cardio After Weight Lifting?

The ideal amount of time to do cardio after weight lifting is about 20-30 minutes. This is enough time to get your heart rate up and burn some extra calories, but not so much that you are overexerting yourself. It's important to remember that everyone's body is different, and what works for one person may not work for another.

It's also important to consider your fitness level and goals when determining how long to do cardio after weight lifting. If you are just starting out, you may want to start with 10-15 minutes and gradually increase the time as you get stronger. If your goal is to lose weight, you may want to do more cardio to burn more calories.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Lifting

As someone who loves weight lifting, I used to avoid doing cardio because I thought it would hinder my gains. However, I soon realized that doing cardio after weight lifting actually helps me feel better and more energized. I usually do 20-30 minutes of cardio after my weight lifting sessions, and I have noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness and health.

The Benefits of Cardio After Weight Lifting

Doing cardio after weight lifting has many benefits. It helps to improve your cardiovascular health, increase your endurance, and burn extra calories. It also helps to reduce muscle soreness and improve your recovery time. Overall, doing cardio after weight lifting is a great way to improve your fitness and reach your goals.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Lifting

If you're new to doing cardio after weight lifting, it's important to start slow and gradually increase the time. You can start with 10-15 minutes and gradually work your way up to 20-30 minutes. It's also important to choose a type of cardio that you enjoy, whether it's running, cycling, or using the elliptical machine.

Tips for Making the Most of Your Cardio After Weight Lifting

To get the most out of your cardio after weight lifting, it's important to stay hydrated and fuel your body with the right nutrients. Make sure to drink plenty of water before, during, and after your workout, and eat a balanced meal with protein and carbohydrates within an hour after your workout. It's also important to stretch and cool down after your workout to prevent injury.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Lifting

As someone who loves weight lifting, I used to avoid doing cardio because I thought it would hinder my gains. However, I soon realized that doing cardio after weight lifting actually helps me feel better and more energized. I usually do 20-30 minutes of cardio after my weight lifting sessions, and I have noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness and health.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight lifting?

A: It's generally better to do weight lifting before cardio, as weight lifting requires more energy and strength than cardio. Doing cardio after weight lifting can also help to improve your recovery time and reduce muscle soreness.

Q: How often should you do cardio after weight lifting?

A: It's recommended to do cardio after weight lifting at least 2-3 times a week, or more if your goal is to lose weight or improve your cardiovascular health.

Q: What type of cardio is best after weight lifting?

A: The best type of cardio after weight lifting is one that you enjoy and that gets your heart rate up. This can include running, cycling, using the elliptical machine, or doing a HIIT workout.

Q: Can doing too much cardio after weight lifting be harmful?

A: Yes, doing too much cardio after weight lifting can be harmful and lead to overtraining, injury, and burnout. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard.

Conclusion of How Long Should You Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

In conclusion, doing cardio after weight lifting is a great way to improve your fitness and reach your goals. The ideal amount of time to do cardio after weight lifting is about 20-30 minutes, but it's important to consider your fitness level and goals when determining how long to do cardio. By incorporating cardio after weight lifting into your workout routine and making the most of your time at the gym, you can achieve the results you desire.
