How Many Days Cardio And Weight Training A Guide For Beginners

Written by Aprilia Aug 09, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you a beginner looking to start a fitness routine but wondering how many days cardio and weight training you should do? It can be overwhelming to know where to start, but don't worry, we've got you covered. In this guide, we'll break down everything you need to know about how many days cardio and weight training, so you can start your fitness journey with confidence.

The Pain Points of How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training

Before we dive into the specifics of how many days cardio and weight training, let's take a look at some common pain points beginners face when starting a fitness routine. You might be wondering:

  • How often should I work out?
  • Should I focus on cardio or weight training?
  • How long should my workouts be?
  • What exercises should I do?

These are all valid questions, and we'll be addressing them throughout this guide.

How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training?

Now, onto the main question: how many days cardio and weight training should you do? The answer depends on your fitness goals and current fitness level.

If your goal is to lose weight and improve cardiovascular health, you'll want to focus more on cardio. Aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week, spread out over several days. This can include activities such as brisk walking, cycling, or swimming.

If your goal is to build muscle and increase strength, weight training is key. Aim for at least two to three days of strength training per week, focusing on compound exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench presses.

It's important to note that both cardio and weight training are important for overall health and fitness, so try to incorporate both into your routine if possible.

The Benefits of How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training

So, why is it important to do both cardio and weight training? Let's break down the benefits of each:

Cardiovascular Health

Cardiovascular exercise (such as running, biking, or swimming) strengthens your heart and lungs, improves circulation, and can lower your risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease and diabetes.

Muscle and Bone Health

Weight training helps build and maintain muscle mass, which is important for overall strength and mobility. It can also improve bone density and reduce the risk of osteoporosis.

Weight Loss

Both cardio and weight training can help with weight loss, as they burn calories and boost metabolism. Combining the two can be especially effective for shedding pounds.

Personal Experience with How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training

As a fitness enthusiast, I've found that incorporating both cardio and weight training into my routine has been key for reaching my fitness goals. I aim for at least three days of strength training per week, focusing on compound movements like squats and deadlifts, and I also do cardio (usually running or cycling) four to five days per week.

Not only do I feel stronger and more energized, but I've also seen improvements in my overall health, including lower blood pressure and cholesterol levels.

Tips for How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training

Here are some tips to keep in mind as you design your cardio and weight training routine:

Start Slow

If you're just starting out, don't push yourself too hard too fast. Gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts to avoid injury and burnout.

Mix It Up

Don't be afraid to try different types of cardio and weight training exercises to keep your routine interesting and challenging. This can also help prevent boredom and plateaus.

Rest and Recovery

Rest days are just as important as workout days, as they give your body time to recover and repair. Aim for at least one or two rest days per week.

Question and Answer

Q: How long should a cardio workout be?

A: Aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per session. You can break this up into shorter sessions throughout the day if needed.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It's generally recommended to do weight training before cardio, as weight training requires more energy and focus. However, the most important thing is to find a routine that works for you and stick with it.

Q: Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A: Yes, it's possible to do both cardio and weight training in the same workout. Just make sure to space them out (for example, do weight training first, then cardio) and listen to your body.

Q: How do I know if I'm doing too much or too little cardio and weight training?

A: It's important to listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed. Signs of doing too much include fatigue, soreness, and lack of progress. Signs of doing too little include lack of energy and slow progress.

Conclusion of How Many Days Cardio and Weight Training

Now that you have a better understanding of how many days cardio and weight training to do, it's time to start designing your own fitness routine. Remember to start slow, mix it up, and listen to your body. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your overall health and well-being.
