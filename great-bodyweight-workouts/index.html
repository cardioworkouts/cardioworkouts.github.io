Great Bodyweight Workouts Your Ultimate Guide To Staying Fit Anywhere Anytime

Written by Christine Aug 28, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym but still not seeing any significant results? Do you find it challenging to stick to a strict workout routine because of your busy schedule? If you answered yes to either of these questions, then great bodyweight workouts might be the perfect solution for you.

Many people struggle with finding the time and motivation to work out regularly. Others find gym memberships and personal trainers too expensive. But with bodyweight workouts, you can exercise anytime, anywhere, and without any equipment. Plus, they are a great way to build strength, burn calories, and improve your overall fitness level.

In this guide, we will introduce you to some of the best bodyweight workouts that you can do at home or on the go. We'll cover the target of great bodyweight workouts, the benefits of bodyweight exercises, and how to create a workout plan that works for you. So, let's get started!

The Push-Up

The push-up is a classic bodyweight exercise that works your chest, shoulders, triceps, and core. To perform a push-up, start in a plank position with your hands slightly wider than your shoulders. Lower your body until your chest touches the ground, then push back up to the starting position. You can modify the push-up by placing your knees on the ground or doing the exercise against a wall.

When I first started doing push-ups, I could barely do five in a row. But with practice, I gradually increased my reps and added different variations like diamond push-ups and decline push-ups. Push-ups are a great way to build upper body strength and endurance, and they require no equipment.

The Squat

Squats are another excellent bodyweight exercise that targets your glutes, quads, and hamstrings. To do a squat, stand with your feet shoulder-width apart and your toes pointing forward. Lower your body by bending your knees and pushing your hips back, then return to the starting position by pushing through your heels.

I used to hate squats because I found them challenging and uncomfortable. But as I learned to engage my glutes and maintain proper form, I started to enjoy the exercise and feel the burn in my legs. Squats are a great way to tone your lower body and improve your balance and stability.

The Plank

The plank is a simple yet effective bodyweight exercise that targets your core muscles. To perform a plank, start in a push-up position, but instead of lowering your body, hold the position for as long as you can. Make sure to keep your body in a straight line from head to heels and engage your abs and glutes.

Planks are one of my favorite bodyweight exercises because they are low-impact and can be done anywhere. I love challenging myself to hold the position for longer and adding variations like side planks and plank jacks. Planks are a great way to strengthen your core, improve your posture, and prevent lower back pain.

The Burpee

The burpee is a full-body bodyweight exercise that combines a squat, push-up, and jump. To do a burpee, start in a standing position, then squat down and place your hands on the ground. Kick your feet back into a push-up position, then lower your body and do a push-up. Jump your feet back up to your hands, then explode up into a jump.

I have a love-hate relationship with burpees because they are intense and challenging. But they are also a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. I like to add burpees to my workout routine as a cardio boost or a finisher.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I build muscle with bodyweight workouts?

A: Yes, you can build muscle with bodyweight workouts by increasing the resistance and volume of your exercises. For example, you can do single-leg squats or one-arm push-ups to make the exercises more challenging.

Q: How often should I do bodyweight workouts?

A: It depends on your fitness level and goals, but you can start with 2-3 sessions per week and gradually increase the frequency and duration of your workouts.

Q: Do I need any equipment for bodyweight workouts?

A: No, you don't need any equipment for bodyweight workouts. However, you can use props like resistance bands, yoga blocks, or a pull-up bar to add variety and difficulty to your exercises.

Q: Are bodyweight workouts suitable for beginners?

A: Yes, bodyweight workouts are suitable for beginners because they are low-impact and can be modified to suit your fitness level. Just make sure to start with the basic exercises and focus on proper form and technique.

Conclusion of Great Bodyweight Workouts

Great bodyweight workouts are an excellent way to stay fit and healthy without the need for a gym membership or expensive equipment. By incorporating exercises like push-ups, squats, planks, and burpees into your workout routine, you can improve your strength, flexibility, and endurance. Remember to start slow, focus on proper form, and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. With consistency and dedication, you can achieve your fitness goals and enjoy the benefits of a healthy lifestyle.
