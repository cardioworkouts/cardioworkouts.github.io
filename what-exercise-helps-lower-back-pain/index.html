Reduce Lower Back Pain With These Exercises

Written by Petter Oct 25, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Do you experience lower back pain frequently? It can be a frustrating and debilitating issue that affects your daily life. Many people turn to medication or surgery to address the problem, but exercise can be an effective and natural way to reduce lower back pain. In this blog post, we will explore what exercise helps lower back pain and related keywords.

Lower back pain can be caused by many factors such as poor posture, weak core muscles, or a sedentary lifestyle. It can be a chronic condition that affects your quality of life. However, regular exercise can strengthen the muscles that support your spine, improve flexibility, and reduce pain.

The exercise that helps lower back pain the most is a combination of stretching and strengthening exercises. This type of exercise routine can help reduce pain and prevent future injuries.

Let's summarize the main points related to what exercise helps lower back pain and related keywords. Regular exercise can reduce lower back pain caused by poor posture, weak core muscles, or a sedentary lifestyle. A combination of stretching and strengthening exercises is the most effective way to address lower back pain.

Strengthen Your Core with Planks

One of the best exercises for reducing lower back pain is planks. Planks are a simple exercise that can significantly improve your core strength, which supports your lower back.

Personally, I used to suffer from lower back pain due to sitting at my desk for long hours every day. I started incorporating planks into my exercise routine, and I noticed a significant improvement in my lower back pain.

To perform a plank, start in a push-up position with your arms straight and your hands shoulder-width apart. Keep your body in a straight line from your head to your heels. Hold this position for 30 seconds to a minute, and repeat 2-3 times.

Stretch Your Hamstrings with Forward Folds

Another exercise that helps reduce lower back pain is forward folds. Tight hamstrings can contribute to lower back pain, and stretching them can provide relief.

Personally, I used to experience lower back pain after running. I started incorporating forward folds into my cool-down routine, and I noticed a significant improvement in my lower back pain.

To perform a forward fold, stand with your feet hip-width apart and slowly bend forward from your hips. Reach your arms toward the ground and let your head hang. Hold this position for 30 seconds to a minute, and repeat 2-3 times.

Try Yoga for Lower Back Pain Relief

Yoga is a great exercise for reducing lower back pain. It can improve flexibility, strengthen muscles, and reduce stress. Many yoga poses focus on the lower back and can provide pain relief.

Personally, I have found yoga to be an effective way to manage my lower back pain. I attend a weekly yoga class that focuses on poses that relieve lower back pain, and it has made a significant difference in my pain levels.

How to Perform Cat-cow Stretch

Cat-cow stretch is a gentle yoga pose that can provide lower back pain relief. To perform cat-cow stretch, start on your hands and knees with your wrists directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips. Inhale and arch your back, dropping your belly toward the ground and lifting your head and tailbone. Exhale and round your spine, tucking your chin into your chest and pulling your belly button toward your spine. Repeat for 5-10 breaths.

Conclusion of What Exercise Helps Lower Back Pain

In conclusion, regular exercise is an effective and natural way to reduce lower back pain. A combination of stretching and strengthening exercises can improve flexibility, strengthen muscles, and reduce pain. Planks and forward folds are two exercises that can help reduce lower back pain. Yoga is also an excellent exercise for lower back pain relief. Incorporate these exercises into your routine to experience lower back pain relief.

Question and Answer

Q: Can exercise worsen lower back pain?

A: If you have severe lower back pain, consult with a medical professional before starting an exercise routine. Certain exercises can worsen lower back pain if not performed correctly.

Q: How often should I exercise to reduce lower back pain?

A: Aim to exercise at least three times a week to experience the benefits of exercise for lower back pain.

Q: Are there any other exercises that can help reduce lower back pain?

A: Yes, exercises such as bridges, bird dogs, and lunges can also help reduce lower back pain.

Q: Can I do these exercises if I have a previous lower back injury?

A: If you have a previous lower back injury, consult with a medical professional before starting an exercise routine. They can provide guidance on which exercises are safe for you to perform.
