Beginner Cardio Workout Schedule A Guide To Getting Started

Written by Christine Nov 22, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to cardio workouts and looking to start a beginner cardio workout schedule? If so, you're not alone. Starting a new workout routine can be overwhelming, especially if you're not sure where to begin. But fear not, we've got you covered!

Many people struggle with finding the time and motivation to start a new workout routine. Others may not know how to structure their workouts or which exercises to include. Whatever the reason, starting a beginner cardio workout schedule can be a challenge.

The target of a beginner cardio workout schedule is to help you build endurance and improve cardiovascular health. This type of workout involves exercises that elevate your heart rate and keep it elevated for an extended period of time. Some common types of cardio workouts include running, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope.

In this article, we'll go over some tips and tricks for starting a beginner cardio workout schedule. We'll cover the benefits of cardio workouts, how often to do them, and what types of exercises to include. By the end of this article, you'll have all the information you need to start your own beginner cardio workout schedule.

The Benefits of Cardio Workouts

Before we dive into the specifics of a beginner cardio workout schedule, let's talk about the benefits of cardio workouts. Cardio workouts offer numerous benefits, including:

Improved cardiovascular health: Cardio workouts help improve the health of your heart and lungs, making it easier for your body to transport oxygen throughout your body.

Weight loss: Cardio workouts are great for burning calories and can help you lose weight when combined with a healthy diet.

Reduced stress: Cardio workouts release endorphins, which can help reduce stress and improve your mood.

Increased energy: Regular cardio workouts can help increase your energy levels and reduce fatigue.

How Often to Do Cardio Workouts

When starting a beginner cardio workout schedule, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Aim to do cardio workouts 3-4 times a week, with at least one rest day in between. As you become more comfortable with the workouts, you can gradually increase the number of days per week or the length of your workouts.

Types of Cardio Exercises

There are many different types of cardio exercises to choose from when building your beginner cardio workout schedule. Some popular ones include:

Running: Running is a great way to get your heart rate up and burn calories. You can run outdoors or on a treadmill.

Cycling: Cycling is a low-impact exercise that is easy on your joints. You can cycle outdoors or on a stationary bike.

Swimming: Swimming is a full-body workout that is gentle on your joints. It's also a great way to cool off on hot days.

Jumping rope: Jumping rope is a fun and challenging way to get your heart rate up. It's also a great workout to do at home.

Creating Your Beginner Cardio Workout Schedule

When creating your beginner cardio workout schedule, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Aim to include a variety of different exercises to keep things interesting and challenging. Here's an example of a beginner cardio workout schedule:

Monday: 20 minutes of jogging or running

Tuesday: Rest day

Wednesday: 30 minutes of cycling

Thursday: Rest day

Friday: 20 minutes of swimming

Saturday: 10 minutes of jumping rope, 20 minutes of cycling

Sunday: Rest day

Remember to Warm Up and Cool Down

Before starting any workout, it's important to warm up your muscles and prepare your body for exercise. This can include stretching, light cardio, or a combination of both. Similarly, after your workout, you should cool down and stretch to help prevent injury and reduce muscle soreness.

Final Thoughts

Starting a beginner cardio workout schedule can be a fun and rewarding way to improve your health and fitness. Remember to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts. Aim to include a variety of different exercises to keep things interesting and challenging. By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to achieving your fitness goals.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the best time of day to do cardio workouts?

A: The best time of day to do cardio workouts is whenever you have the most energy and motivation. Some people prefer to workout in the morning, while others prefer to workout in the evening. Find a time that works best for you and stick to it.

Q: How long should a beginner cardio workout be?

A: A beginner cardio workout should be around 20-30 minutes in length. As you become more comfortable with the workouts, you can gradually increase the length of your workouts.

Q: Can I do cardio workouts every day?

A: It's not recommended to do cardio workouts every day, especially when starting a new workout routine. Aim to do cardio workouts 3-4 times a week, with at least one rest day in between.

Q: How long does it take to see results from cardio workouts?

A: It can take several weeks or months to see significant results from cardio workouts. Consistency is key, so aim to stick to your beginner cardio workout schedule for at least a few months before expecting to see significant results.

Conclusion of Beginner Cardio Workout Schedule

Starting a beginner cardio workout schedule can be a challenge, but it's also a fun and rewarding way to improve your health and fitness. Remember to start slowly, gradually increase the intensity of your workouts, and include a variety of different exercises. By following these tips, you'll be well on your way to achieving your fitness goals.
