How To Increase Strength Training Volume A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Christine Sep 25, 2023 · 3 min read

Are you feeling stuck in your strength training routine? Do you find yourself hitting a plateau and not seeing the gains you want? It can be frustrating to put in the time and effort at the gym without seeing the results you want. But fear not, there are ways to increase your strength training volume and get back on track to achieving your fitness goals.

The key to increasing strength training volume is to challenge your muscles in new ways. This means adding weight, increasing reps or sets, or changing up your exercises. By doing so, you will break through plateaus and stimulate muscle growth.

Here are some tips on how to increase strength training volume:

Start Slow

When it comes to increasing strength training volume, it's important to start slow. Don't go from doing three sets of 10 reps to five sets of 15 reps in one day. Gradually increase your volume over time to avoid injury and allow your body to adapt. Start by adding one extra set or a few extra reps to your routine each week.

Vary Your Exercises

Doing the same exercises every day can lead to boredom and a lack of progress. To increase strength training volume, try incorporating new exercises into your routine. This will challenge your muscles in new ways and prevent plateaus. For example, if you usually do bicep curls, try adding in hammer curls or concentration curls.

Increase Weight

One of the most effective ways to increase strength training volume is to lift heavier weights. This will challenge your muscles and stimulate growth. Start by gradually increasing the weight you're lifting. Aim to lift a weight that is heavy enough to make the last few reps of your set challenging, but not so heavy that you sacrifice proper form.

Rest and Recovery

It's important to give your body time to rest and recover between strength training sessions. This will allow your muscles to repair and grow. Make sure to take at least one day off between strength training sessions, and get plenty of sleep and proper nutrition to support muscle recovery.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I increase my strength training volume?

A: It's best to increase your volume gradually over time, adding one extra set or a few extra reps each week. This will allow your body to adapt and prevent injury.

Q: Can I increase my strength training volume every day?

A: No, it's important to give your body time to rest and recover between strength training sessions. Aim to strength train 2-3 times per week, with at least one day off in between sessions.

Q: What if I'm not seeing results after increasing my strength training volume?

A: It's important to make sure you're also getting proper nutrition, rest, and recovery. Make sure you're eating enough protein to support muscle growth, getting plenty of sleep, and taking rest days as needed.

Q: How long does it take to see results after increasing strength training volume?

A: Results will vary depending on your individual fitness level and goals. However, with consistent training and proper nutrition, you should start to see progress within a few weeks.

Conclusion of How to Increase Strength Training Volume

Increasing strength training volume is a key component of building muscle and achieving your fitness goals. By gradually increasing your volume, varying your exercises, lifting heavier weights, and prioritizing rest and recovery, you can break through plateaus and make progress towards your goals. Remember to listen to your body and always prioritize proper form and safety.
