Does It Matter If You Do Strength Or Cardio First

Written by Petter Jul 17, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who enjoys working out but is unsure whether to start with strength or cardio exercises? If yes, then this article is for you. In this blog post, we will discuss whether it matters if you do strength or cardio first and provide you with some insights and personal experiences to help you make an informed decision.

Many people struggle with deciding which type of exercise to start with during their workout routine. Some are concerned that they will tire out before they can complete their entire workout, while others believe that starting with cardio will help them lose more weight. These are common pain points that many individuals face when they are trying to decide whether to do strength or cardio first.

The short answer to whether it matters if you do strength or cardio first is that it depends on your fitness goals. If your goal is to build muscle mass, then it is recommended that you start with strength training. On the other hand, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular endurance, then you should start with cardio.

To summarize, the main points related to does it matter if you do strength or cardio first are that it depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, start with strength training. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, start with cardio.

Personal Experience: Starting with Strength Training

Personally, I always start with strength training because my primary goal is to build muscle mass. I find that when I start with cardio, I am too tired to complete my strength exercises effectively. By starting with strength training, I have more energy and can put more effort into my workouts.

Personal Experience: Starting with Cardio

On the other hand, my friend starts with cardio because her primary goal is to lose weight. She believes that starting with cardio helps her burn more calories and lose weight faster. She still incorporates strength training into her routine but does it after her cardio workout.

More Detailed Explanation

When you start with strength training, your muscles are fresh, and you have more energy to lift weights. This means that you can lift heavier weights and complete more reps, which is essential for building muscle mass. Additionally, strength training helps increase your metabolism, which can help you burn more calories throughout the day.

On the other hand, starting with cardio can help you warm up your body and increase your heart rate. This can help prevent injury during your strength training exercises. Additionally, cardio exercises can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health.

How to Make a Decision

To decide whether to start with strength or cardio, you need to consider your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, start with strength training. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, start with cardio. Additionally, you should consider your personal preferences and what works best for your body.

Personal Experience: Combining Strength and Cardio

Personally, I like to combine strength and cardio exercises into one workout. I start with a five-minute warm-up on the treadmill, then move on to my strength training exercises. After completing my strength exercises, I finish my workout with a 20-minute cardio session on the elliptical. I find that this approach helps me get the best of both worlds.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do strength and cardio on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do strength and cardio on the same day. However, you should start with the exercise that is your primary focus.

Q: How long should I wait between strength and cardio exercises?

A: This depends on your fitness level and how intense your workouts are. Generally, it is recommended to wait at least 30 minutes between strength and cardio exercises.

Q: Should I do strength or cardio first if I want to lose weight?

A: If your goal is to lose weight, it is recommended that you start with cardio. Cardio exercises can help you burn more calories and lose weight faster.

Q: Can I do strength training and cardio on the same machine?

A: Yes, you can combine strength training and cardio exercises on some machines. For example, you can use the elliptical or stationary bike to do both cardio and strength exercises.

Conclusion

In conclusion, whether to do strength or cardio first depends on your fitness goals. If you want to build muscle mass, start with strength training. If you want to improve your cardiovascular endurance, start with cardio. You can also combine these exercises into one workout or do them on separate days. The key is to find what works best for your body and your fitness goals.
