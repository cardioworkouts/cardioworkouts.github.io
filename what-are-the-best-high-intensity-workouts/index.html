Best High Intensity Workouts Get Fit And Stay Active

Written by Bardi Aug 31, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Do you want to get in shape and stay active but don't have a lot of time to spare? High intensity workouts might just be the solution you're looking for. These workouts are designed to provide maximum results in a short amount of time, making them perfect for busy people who want to stay fit. In this article, we'll explore what are the best high intensity workouts and how you can incorporate them into your fitness routine.

Are you tired of spending hours at the gym without seeing any results? Do you struggle to find the motivation to exercise regularly? These are common pain points for many people who are trying to stay fit. High intensity workouts can help you overcome these challenges by providing a quick and effective way to get in shape.

So what are the best high intensity workouts? There are many different types of high intensity workouts, but some of the most popular include interval training, circuit training, and HIIT (high intensity interval training). These workouts involve short periods of intense activity followed by brief periods of rest or active recovery. They are designed to get your heart rate up and maximize calorie burn.

In summary, the best high intensity workouts are those that provide maximum results in a short amount of time. They include interval training, circuit training, and HIIT. By incorporating these workouts into your fitness routine, you can stay active, get fit, and achieve your health goals.

Interval Training

One of the best high intensity workouts is interval training. This involves alternating between periods of high intensity exercise and periods of low intensity exercise or rest. For example, you might sprint for 30 seconds, then walk or jog for 1 minute, and repeat this pattern for several rounds. Interval training can be done with any type of exercise, such as running, cycling, or swimming.

I personally love interval training because it keeps me engaged and challenged throughout the workout. I feel like I'm constantly pushing myself to go harder and faster. Plus, it's a great way to mix up my routine and prevent boredom.

Circuit Training

Another great high intensity workout is circuit training. This involves performing a series of exercises in a specific order with little to no rest in between. For example, you might do a set of push-ups, followed by squats, lunges, and burpees, and repeat this circuit for several rounds. Circuit training can be done with bodyweight exercises or with weights.

Personally, I find circuit training to be a fun and challenging way to work all of my major muscle groups. I love the variety of exercises and the fact that I can get a full-body workout in a short amount of time.

HIIT (High Intensity Interval Training)

HIIT (high intensity interval training) is a type of workout that combines interval training with high intensity exercises. This involves performing short bursts of all-out effort followed by periods of rest or active recovery. For example, you might do a round of jump squats, followed by push-ups, mountain climbers, and sprints, and repeat this pattern for several rounds.

Personally, I find HIIT to be the most challenging of all the high intensity workouts. It requires a lot of mental and physical toughness to push yourself to your limits. However, the results are worth it - HIIT is one of the most effective ways to burn calories and improve cardiovascular health.

Tabata Training

Tabata training is a type of HIIT workout that involves performing 8 rounds of 20 seconds of all-out effort followed by 10 seconds of rest. This pattern is repeated for a total of 4 minutes. Tabata workouts can be done with any type of exercise, such as jump squats, burpees, or push-ups.

I personally love Tabata training because it is a quick and effective way to get in a high intensity workout. I can do a Tabata workout in just 4 minutes and feel like I've worked every muscle in my body.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do high intensity workouts?

A: It is recommended that you do high intensity workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between. This will give your body time to recover and prevent injury.

Q: Can high intensity workouts be modified for beginners?

A: Yes, high intensity workouts can be modified for beginners. You can start with lower intensity exercises and gradually increase the intensity over time. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard too soon.

Q: Do I need any special equipment for high intensity workouts?

A: No, you don't need any special equipment for high intensity workouts. Many high intensity workouts can be done with just your bodyweight or minimal equipment, such as resistance bands or dumbbells.

Q: Are high intensity workouts safe?

A: High intensity workouts can be safe if done correctly and with proper form. It's important to start with lower intensity exercises and gradually increase the intensity over time. It's also important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard too soon. If you have any health concerns or injuries, it's always a good idea to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise program.

Conclusion of Best High Intensity Workouts

High intensity workouts are a great way to stay active and get in shape, even if you don't have a lot of time to spare. By incorporating interval training, circuit training, HIIT, and Tabata training into your fitness routine, you can achieve your health goals and feel great. Remember to start slow, listen to your body, and have fun!
