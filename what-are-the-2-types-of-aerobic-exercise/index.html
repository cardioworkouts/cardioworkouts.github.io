What Are The 2 Types Of Aerobic Exercise

Written by Oliver Jul 12, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to improve your cardiovascular health and overall fitness? Aerobic exercise is a great way to achieve these goals. However, with so many types of aerobic exercise out there, it can be overwhelming to know where to start. In this article, we will discuss the two main types of aerobic exercise to help you make an informed decision and get started on your fitness journey.

The thought of exercise can be intimidating, especially if you're not familiar with the terminology. What are the 2 types of aerobic exercise, and how do they differ? Understanding the differences between the two can help you choose which one is best for you and your fitness goals.

The two types of aerobic exercise are high-impact and low-impact. High-impact exercises are those that involve jumping or other movements that put pressure on your joints. Low-impact exercises, on the other hand, are those that involve less pressure on your joints and are often considered safer for beginners or those with joint issues.

Now that we know the two types of aerobic exercise, let's dive deeper into each one to better understand their benefits and how they differ.

High-Impact Aerobic Exercise

High-impact aerobic exercise involves movements that put pressure on your joints, such as jumping jacks, running, or plyometrics. These exercises are great for improving cardiovascular health, burning calories, and strengthening your bones. However, they may not be suitable for everyone, especially those with joint issues or injuries. It's important to ease into high-impact exercise and gradually increase intensity to avoid injury.

Personally, I enjoy high-impact aerobic exercise because it gets my heart rate up quickly, and I feel energized afterward. However, I always make sure to wear supportive shoes and land softly to avoid putting too much pressure on my joints.

Low-Impact Aerobic Exercise

Low-impact aerobic exercise involves movements that put less pressure on your joints, such as walking, cycling, or swimming. These exercises are also great for improving cardiovascular health and burning calories but are often considered safer for beginners or those with joint issues. Low-impact exercises can be just as effective as high-impact exercises, but it may take longer to achieve the same calorie burn or cardiovascular benefits.

Personally, I enjoy low-impact aerobic exercise because it's gentler on my joints, and I can do it for longer periods without feeling fatigued. I often go for walks or bike rides in nature, which not only improves my physical health but also my mental health.

The Benefits of Aerobic Exercise

Regardless of whether you choose high-impact or low-impact aerobic exercise, both types have numerous benefits for your health. Some of these benefits include:

  • Improved cardiovascular health and circulation
  • Better lung function and oxygen delivery
  • Increased endurance and stamina
  • Burns calories and aids in weight loss
  • Strengthens bones and muscles
  • Reduces the risk of chronic diseases such as diabetes, obesity, and heart disease
  • Improves mood and reduces stress

Tips for Starting Aerobic Exercise

Starting a new exercise routine can be overwhelming, but here are some tips to help you get started:

  • Consult with your doctor before starting any exercise program, especially if you have joint issues or injuries.
  • Start slow and gradually increase the intensity of your workouts.
  • Choose an exercise that you enjoy and fits your lifestyle.
  • Wear proper shoes and clothing for your chosen exercise.
  • Stay hydrated before, during, and after exercise.

Conclusion of What Are the 2 Types of Aerobic Exercise

Now that you know the two types of aerobic exercise, high-impact and low-impact, you can make an informed decision about which one is best for you and your fitness goals. Remember to start slow, consult with your doctor, and choose an exercise that you enjoy to ensure success in your fitness journey.

Question and Answer

Q: What is the difference between high-impact and low-impact aerobic exercise?

A: High-impact aerobic exercise involves movements that put pressure on your joints, while low-impact aerobic exercise involves movements that put less pressure on your joints.

Q: Are high-impact exercises suitable for everyone?

A: No, high-impact exercises may not be suitable for those with joint issues or injuries. It's important to ease into high-impact exercise and gradually increase intensity to avoid injury.

Q: Can low-impact exercises be just as effective as high-impact exercises?

A: Yes, low-impact exercises can be just as effective as high-impact exercises, but it may take longer to achieve the same calorie burn or cardiovascular benefits.

Q: What are some benefits of aerobic exercise?

A: Aerobic exercise can improve cardiovascular health, lung function, endurance, burn calories, strengthen bones and muscles, reduce the risk of chronic diseases, and improve mood and reduce stress.
