How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

Written by Bardi Aug 18, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you someone who has just started lifting weights and is now wondering about the ideal duration for cardio post weight lifting session? Or are you someone who has been following this routine for a while and is unsure if you're doing it right? Whatever be the case, this blog post will help you understand how long should you do cardio after weight lifting and why it is important.

Understanding the Pain Points Related to How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

Many people are often confused about how long they should do cardio after weight lifting. Some people think that doing cardio immediately after lifting weights will negate the effects of weight lifting, while others believe that they should do cardio before weight lifting. This confusion can lead to frustration and may even cause people to give up on their fitness goals.

Answer to How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

The ideal duration for cardio after weight lifting is around 15-20 minutes. This helps in burning the excess calories that were not burnt during weight lifting. Doing cardio after weight lifting also helps in reducing muscle soreness and stiffness.

Summary of Main Points

In this post, we have discussed how long you should do cardio after weight lifting. We have also explained why it is important to do cardio after weight lifting and how it can help you achieve your fitness goals. Additionally, we have shared some personal experiences and tips that can help you optimize your cardio routine.

Why is It Important to Do Cardio After Weight Lifting?

Cardio after weight lifting helps in improving your cardiovascular health, aids in weight loss, and promotes quicker recovery. Doing cardio after weight lifting also helps in improving your endurance and stamina, which is essential for any physical activity.

How to Optimize Your Cardio Routine After Weight Lifting

If you're looking to optimize your cardio routine after weight lifting, then you should consider doing high-intensity interval training (HIIT). HIIT involves short bursts of intense activity followed by periods of rest. This type of cardio is more effective in burning calories and improving cardiovascular health as compared to steady-state cardio.

Factors to Consider When Deciding How Long to Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

The duration of cardio after weight lifting depends on various factors such as your fitness level, diet, and workout goals. If you're a beginner, it's best to start with shorter durations and gradually increase the duration as your fitness level improves. Additionally, your diet also plays a crucial role in determining the duration of cardio after weight lifting. If you're on a low-calorie diet, then it's best to limit the duration of your cardio sessions.

How to Incorporate Cardio After Weight Lifting in Your Fitness Routine

If you're unsure about how to incorporate cardio after weight lifting in your fitness routine, then you should consider hiring a personal trainer. A personal trainer can help you create a customized fitness plan that includes both weight lifting and cardio.

Personal Experience with Cardio After Weight Lifting

Personally, I have found that doing 15-20 minutes of cardio after weight lifting has helped me in achieving my fitness goals. I have also noticed a significant reduction in muscle soreness and stiffness after incorporating cardio after weight lifting in my fitness routine.

Question and Answer

Q. Can I do cardio before weight lifting?
A. While it's not recommended to do cardio before weight lifting, you can do it if you want to. However, doing cardio before weight lifting may tire you out and reduce your stamina for weight lifting.

Q. Can I do cardio on rest days?
A. Yes, you can do cardio on rest days. However, it's best to limit the duration and intensity of your cardio sessions on rest days.

Q. What type of cardio is best after weight lifting?
A. High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is the best type of cardio after weight lifting as it is more effective in burning calories and improving cardiovascular health.

Q. How long should I wait to do cardio after weight lifting?
A. You can start doing cardio immediately after weight lifting or wait for 10-15 minutes to cool down. It's best to listen to your body and do what feels comfortable.

Conclusion of How Long Should I Do Cardio After Weight Lifting

Cardio after weight lifting is an essential part of any fitness routine. It helps in improving cardiovascular health, aids in weight loss, and promotes quicker recovery. The ideal duration for cardio after weight lifting is around 15-20 minutes. However, the duration of cardio after weight lifting may vary depending on various factors such as your fitness level, diet, and workout goals.
