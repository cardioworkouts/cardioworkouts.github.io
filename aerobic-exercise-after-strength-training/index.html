Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training The Benefits And How To Do It

Written by Luffy Jul 12, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you looking to maximize your workout routine? Do you want to know how to get the most out of your strength training? One way to do this is by adding aerobic exercise after strength training. Not only can it help you burn more calories, but it can also improve your overall fitness level. In this post, we'll explore the benefits of aerobic exercise after strength training, how to do it, and answer some common questions about this topic.

The Pain Points of Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

It's not uncommon to feel tired or fatigued after a strength training session. The idea of doing more exercise might seem daunting or exhausting. However, adding aerobic exercise after strength training can actually help with post-workout fatigue. It can also help to improve your cardiovascular fitness, which can have numerous health benefits.

The Target of Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

The target of aerobic exercise after strength training is to improve cardiovascular fitness and burn additional calories. Strength training alone can build muscle and improve strength, but it doesn't always result in high calorie burn. By adding aerobic exercise after strength training, you can continue to burn calories and improve your overall fitness level.

My Personal Experience With Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

As a personal trainer, I often recommend adding aerobic exercise after strength training to my clients. I've seen firsthand how it can improve their fitness level and help them reach their weight loss goals. Personally, I also practice this myself. I like to finish my strength training session with a 20-30 minute jog or bike ride. It helps me to clear my head and finish my workout on a high note.

How to Incorporate Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

If you're new to incorporating aerobic exercise after strength training, start small. Begin with 10-15 minutes of low-intensity cardio, such as walking or light jogging. Gradually increase the time and intensity as you become more comfortable. Aim to incorporate aerobic exercise at least 2-3 times per week after your strength training session.

The Benefits of Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

Adding aerobic exercise after strength training can have numerous benefits, including:

  • Improved cardiovascular fitness
  • Increased calorie burn
  • Improved endurance
  • Reduced risk of certain diseases

Tips for Doing Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

Here are some tips to keep in mind when adding aerobic exercise after strength training:

  • Choose an activity you enjoy
  • Start with low-intensity cardio and gradually increase
  • Hydrate before, during, and after your workout
  • Stretch after your workout to prevent muscle soreness

Common Questions About Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

Q: How long should I wait to do aerobic exercise after strength training?

A: It's best to wait at least 10-15 minutes after your strength training session before beginning aerobic exercise. This allows your body to recover and transition to the next phase of your workout.

Q: Do I have to do aerobic exercise after every strength training session?

A: No, you don't have to do aerobic exercise after every strength training session. Aim to incorporate it at least 2-3 times per week for maximum benefits.

Q: Can I do aerobic exercise before strength training?

A: Yes, you can do aerobic exercise before strength training. However, it's important to warm up properly before beginning strength training to prevent injury.

Q: What are some good aerobic exercises to do after strength training?

A: Some good aerobic exercises to do after strength training include jogging, cycling, rowing, or using an elliptical machine. Choose an activity you enjoy and that fits your fitness level.

Conclusion of Aerobic Exercise After Strength Training

Adding aerobic exercise after strength training can be a great way to improve your overall fitness level and burn more calories. By following these tips and incorporating it into your routine, you can achieve your fitness goals and improve your health.
