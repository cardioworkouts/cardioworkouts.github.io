Should I Do Cardio Or Workout First

Written by Bardi Aug 09, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you confused about whether you should do cardio or workout first? It's a common question among fitness enthusiasts. Some people believe that doing cardio before a workout is more effective, while others prefer to lift weights before hitting the treadmill. In this blog post, we’ll discuss the pros and cons of each approach and help you make an informed decision about what works best for you.

Pain Points

One of the biggest concerns for people is whether they will burn more calories by doing cardio first or lifting weights first. Others worry that doing cardio before weightlifting will tire them out and make it difficult to complete their strength training exercises. With so many myths and opinions out there, it can be hard to know what's true and what's not.

Answering the Target

The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. It really depends on your personal goals and preferences. Here are some things to consider:

Cardio First:

If you're looking to burn fat and lose weight, doing cardio before weightlifting may be the best approach. Studies have shown that cardio can increase your metabolism and help you burn more calories throughout the day. Plus, getting your heart rate up before lifting weights can provide a good warm-up and help you avoid injury.

Weightlifting First:

If your goal is to build muscle and increase strength, lifting weights before cardio may be the way to go. Weightlifting requires a lot of energy and focus, and you'll need all of your strength to lift heavy weights and perform complex movements. Doing cardio first can leave you feeling tired and weak, which can compromise your form and increase your risk of injury.

Combining Cardio and Weightlifting:

Another option is to combine cardio and weightlifting into one workout. This approach can provide a full-body workout that targets both your cardiovascular system and your muscles. If you're short on time, this can be an efficient way to get the most out of your workout. However, it's important to be mindful of your form and avoid overtraining.

How to Optimize Your Workout:

If you decide to do cardio and weightlifting in the same workout, here are some tips to help you optimize your results:

  • Start with a dynamic warm-up to get your heart rate up and your muscles warmed up.
  • Do your weightlifting exercises first, focusing on compound movements that work multiple muscle groups at the same time.
  • Follow up with cardio exercises that get your heart rate up, such as running, cycling, or jumping rope.
  • Finish with a cool-down that includes static stretches to help improve your flexibility and reduce muscle soreness.

Personal Experience

Personally, I prefer to do weightlifting before cardio. I find that I have more energy and focus when I start with weights, and I can push myself harder during my strength training exercises. Plus, I like to save cardio for the end of my workout as a way to cool down and stretch my muscles.

FAQs

Q: Is it bad to do cardio and weights on the same day?

A: No, it's not bad to do cardio and weights on the same day. In fact, combining cardio and weightlifting can provide a full-body workout and help you achieve your fitness goals more efficiently.

Q: Should I do cardio or weights first if I want to build muscle?

A: If your goal is to build muscle, you should do weights first. Lifting weights requires a lot of energy and focus, and you'll need all of your strength to lift heavy weights and perform complex movements.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after weights?

A: You should wait at least 10-15 minutes after your weightlifting session before doing cardio. This will give your muscles time to recover and reduce your risk of injury.

Q: Can I do cardio and weights on the same day if I'm a beginner?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weights on the same day if you're a beginner. However, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity over time to avoid overtraining and injury.

Conclusion

So, should you do cardio or workout first? Ultimately, the decision is up to you and depends on your personal goals and preferences. Whether you choose to do cardio first, weights first, or a combination of both, make sure to listen to your body, stay hydrated, and fuel your body with healthy foods to optimize your workout.
