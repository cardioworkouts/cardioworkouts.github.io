Is Full Body Workout Better Than Split For Fat Loss

Written by Aprilia Nov 03, 2023 · 5 min read

Are you trying to lose weight and wondering whether a full body workout or a split workout is the best option? Perhaps you've been doing a split workout but not seeing the results you want, or you're new to working out and not sure where to start. Whatever your situation, choosing between a full body workout and a split workout can be confusing. In this article, we will discuss whether a full body workout is better than a split workout for fat loss.

When it comes to fat loss, the most important thing is creating a calorie deficit, which means burning more calories than you consume. Both full body and split workouts can help you achieve this goal, but they work in different ways. A full body workout involves working all major muscle groups in one session, while a split workout involves working one or two muscle groups per session.

So, is a full body workout better than a split workout for fat loss? The answer is that it depends on your goals and preferences. A full body workout can be more efficient because you're working all major muscle groups in one session, which can help you burn more calories overall. On the other hand, a split workout can be more effective if you want to focus on building muscle in specific areas.

To summarize, choosing between a full body workout and a split workout for fat loss depends on your goals and preferences. Both can be effective, but they work in different ways.

Personal Experience with Full Body Workouts

I have been doing full body workouts for the past year, and I have seen great results in terms of fat loss. I was initially skeptical about whether working all major muscle groups in one session would be effective, but I found that it was a great way to burn more calories and build endurance. I also appreciated the efficiency of full body workouts, as I could get a great workout in a shorter amount of time.

When it comes to fat loss, full body workouts can be effective because they work all major muscle groups, which can help you burn more calories overall. Additionally, because you're working all major muscle groups in one session, you can create a greater calorie deficit than you would with a split workout.

Personal Experience with Split Workouts

While I prefer full body workouts for fat loss, I have also done split workouts in the past. I found that split workouts were more effective for building muscle in specific areas, such as my legs or arms. However, I didn't see as much fat loss with split workouts as I did with full body workouts.

If your goal is to build muscle in specific areas, a split workout can be effective. However, if your primary goal is fat loss, a full body workout may be a better choice.

The Science Behind Full Body Workouts for Fat Loss

There is scientific evidence to support the effectiveness of full body workouts for fat loss. A study published in the Journal of Strength and Conditioning Research found that full body workouts led to a greater reduction in body fat percentage compared to split workouts. Additionally, because full body workouts work all major muscle groups, they can help increase your metabolism, which can lead to greater fat loss over time.

How to Incorporate Full Body Workouts into Your Routine

If you're interested in trying full body workouts for fat loss, there are a few things to keep in mind. First, make sure to vary your exercises so that you're working all major muscle groups. Second, incorporate cardio into your routine to help burn even more calories. Finally, make sure to give yourself enough rest days between workouts to avoid overtraining.

FAQs About Full Body Workouts for Fat Loss

Q: Can I do full body workouts every day?

A: No, it's important to give your muscles time to rest and recover between workouts. Aim to do full body workouts 2-3 times per week.

Q: Can full body workouts help me lose belly fat?

A: Yes, full body workouts can help you lose fat all over your body, including your belly.

Q: Do I need equipment to do full body workouts?

A: No, there are many bodyweight exercises that you can do at home without any equipment. However, incorporating weights into your full body workouts can help you build muscle and burn more calories.

Q: How long should a full body workout be?

A: A full body workout should typically last between 30-60 minutes, depending on your fitness level and the intensity of your workout.

Conclusion of Is Full Body Workout Better Than Split for Fat Loss

In conclusion, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to whether a full body workout or a split workout is better for fat loss. It depends on your goals and preferences. However, if your primary goal is fat loss, a full body workout may be more effective because it works all major muscle groups in one session, which can help you burn more calories overall. Incorporating full body workouts into your routine can be a great way to achieve your weight loss goals and improve your overall fitness and health.
