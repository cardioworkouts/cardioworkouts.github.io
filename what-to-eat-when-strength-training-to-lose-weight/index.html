What To Eat When Strength Training To Lose Weight A Comprehensive Guide

Written by Petter Sep 16, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you struggling to lose weight despite hitting the gym regularly for strength training? Do you feel like your diet is hindering your progress? You're not alone. What you eat plays a crucial role in your weight loss journey, especially when you're strength training. Keep reading to learn what to eat when strength training to lose weight and how it can help you reach your goals.

Why What You Eat Matters

When it comes to weight loss, what you eat is just as important as how much you exercise. Strength training is an effective way to build muscle and burn fat, but it's not enough on its own. Your diet needs to support your fitness goals, providing the necessary nutrients to fuel your workouts and aid in recovery.

Protein

Protein is an essential nutrient for building and repairing muscle tissue. It's also satiating, which can help curb cravings and keep you feeling full for longer. Aim to consume a source of protein with every meal, such as chicken, fish, lean beef, tofu, or lentils.

How Much Protein Do You Need?

The amount of protein you need depends on your body weight and activity level. A general guideline is to consume 0.8 grams of protein per kilogram of body weight. So if you weigh 68 kg, you should aim for around 55 grams of protein per day. If you're strength training, you may need more protein to support muscle growth and repair. Speak to a registered dietitian to determine your protein needs.

When Should You Eat Protein?

Eating protein after a strength training workout can aid in muscle recovery and growth. Aim to consume a protein-rich snack or meal within 30 minutes of finishing your workout.

Complex Carbohydrates

Complex carbohydrates are a great source of energy for your workouts. They provide a steady stream of glucose to your muscles, helping you power through your strength training sessions. Examples of complex carbohydrates include brown rice, quinoa, sweet potatoes, and whole-grain bread.

How Much Carbohydrates Do You Need?

Carbohydrate needs depend on your activity level and body weight. Generally, aim to consume 2-3 grams of carbohydrates per kilogram of body weight. So if you weigh 68 kg, you should aim for around 136-204 grams of carbohydrates per day.

When Should You Eat Carbohydrates?

Eating carbohydrates before a workout can help fuel your exercise. Aim to consume a small meal or snack containing carbohydrates 1-2 hours before your strength training session.

Healthy Fats

Healthy fats are important for overall health and fitness. They provide energy, support cell growth, and help regulate hormones. Good sources of healthy fats include nuts, seeds, avocado, and fatty fish.

How Much Fat Do You Need?

The amount of fat you need depends on your body weight and calorie needs. Generally, aim for 20-35% of your daily calories to come from fat sources.

When Should You Eat Fat?

There's no specific time to eat fat, but it's important to include healthy fats in your overall diet. Sprinkle nuts on top of your oatmeal or add avocado to your sandwich for a boost of healthy fats.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I eat carbs and still lose weight?

A: Yes, you can still eat carbohydrates and lose weight. It's important to focus on complex carbohydrates and monitor your portion sizes.

Q: Should I avoid fat altogether?

A: No, you shouldn't avoid fat altogether. Healthy fats are important for overall health and fitness. Just be sure to monitor your portion sizes and focus on sources of healthy fats.

Q: Can I still eat dessert while strength training?

A: Yes, you can still enjoy dessert while strength training. Just be sure to monitor your portion sizes and choose healthier options, such as fruit or Greek yogurt with honey.

Q: How do I know if I'm eating enough?

A: Speak to a registered dietitian to determine your individual calorie and nutrient needs. They can help you create a personalized meal plan that supports your fitness goals.

Conclusion of What to Eat When Strength Training to Lose Weight

What you eat plays a crucial role in your weight loss journey, especially when you're strength training. Focus on consuming sources of protein, complex carbohydrates, and healthy fats to fuel your workouts and aid in recovery. Monitor your portion sizes and speak to a registered dietitian to determine your individual nutrient needs. With the right diet and exercise plan, you can achieve your weight loss goals and improve your overall health and fitness.
