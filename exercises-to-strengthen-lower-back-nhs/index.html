What Are Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back NHS?

Exercises to strengthen lower back NHS are exercises that target the muscles in your lower back. How to Do Exercises to Strengthen Lower Back NHS

Here are step-by-step instructions for some of the most effective exercises to strengthen lower back NHS:

Bridges
  1. Lie on your back with your knees bent and feet flat on the ground.
  2. Engage your core and glutes, and lift your hips off the ground.
  3. Hold for a few seconds, then lower back down.
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps.
Bird Dogs
  1. Get on all fours with your hands directly under your shoulders and your knees directly under your hips.
  2. Engage your core and lift your right arm and left leg off the ground.
  3. Hold for a few seconds, then lower back down.
  4. Repeat on the opposite side for 10-15 reps.
Supermans
  1. Lie face down with your arms and legs extended.
  2. Engage your glutes and lower back, and lift your arms and legs off the ground.
  3. Hold for a few seconds, then lower back down.
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps.
Deadlifts
  1. Stand with your feet shoulder-width apart, holding a dumbbell or kettlebell in each hand.
  2. Hinge at your hips and lower the weights towards the ground.
  3. Engage your glutes and lower back, and lift the weights back up to standing.
  4. Repeat for 10-15 reps.
Planks
  1. Get into a push-up position with your hands directly under your shoulders and your toes on the ground.
  2. Engage your core and hold for 30-60 seconds.
  3. Lower back down to the ground.
  4. Repeat for 3-5 reps.
