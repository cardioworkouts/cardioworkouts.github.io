Cardio Workout After Lifting A Beginner s Guide

Written by Robby Jun 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those gym-goers who spends hours pumping iron but skips cardio workouts altogether? If yes, then you're not alone. Most people feel that lifting weights is enough to achieve their fitness goals, but that's far from the truth. In this article, we'll discuss the importance of cardio workout after lifting and how it can help you achieve your fitness goals.

The Pain Points of Skipping Cardio Workout After Lifting

Skipping cardio workouts after lifting can lead to a host of problems. Firstly, it can hamper your weight loss goals. Cardio exercises help you burn calories faster and lose weight more efficiently. Secondly, it can hinder your muscle growth. Cardio workouts increase your heart rate and blood flow, which helps in the recovery and growth of muscles. Lastly, it can lead to a lack of stamina and endurance, which can affect your overall fitness.

The Target of Cardio Workout After Lifting

The target of cardio workout after lifting is to complement your weight training with cardiovascular exercises that increase your heart rate and blood flow. This helps in burning calories, improving your endurance and stamina, and aiding in muscle recovery and growth.

It's essential to maintain a balance between cardio and weight training to achieve your fitness goals.

Personal Experience with Cardio Workout After Lifting

As a fitness enthusiast, I used to focus solely on weight training and ignore cardio exercises altogether. However, I soon realized that this approach wasn't helping me achieve my fitness goals. I started incorporating cardio workouts after lifting, and the results were remarkable. Not only did I lose weight faster, but I also felt more energetic and had better stamina during my workouts.

The Benefits of Cardio Workout After Lifting

The benefits of cardio workout after lifting are numerous. Firstly, it helps in burning calories faster and aids in weight loss. Secondly, it improves your cardiovascular health by increasing your heart rate and blood flow. Thirdly, it aids in muscle recovery and growth by increasing blood flow to the muscles. Lastly, it boosts your endurance and stamina, allowing you to perform better during your workouts.

The Best Cardio Exercises After Lifting

The best cardio exercises after lifting are those that increase your heart rate and blood flow without affecting your muscle recovery. Some of the best cardio exercises include running, cycling, swimming, rowing, and jumping rope. You can choose any cardio exercise that you enjoy and fits your fitness level.

How to Incorporate Cardio Workout After Lifting into Your Fitness Routine

It's essential to incorporate cardio workout after lifting into your fitness routine gradually. Start by adding 10-15 minutes of cardio exercises after every weight training session and gradually increase the duration and intensity. It's also essential to choose cardio exercises that you enjoy and fit your fitness level.

Personal Experience with Cardio Workout After Lifting

After incorporating cardio workout after lifting into my fitness routine, I noticed a significant improvement in my overall fitness. I lost weight faster, had better endurance and stamina, and felt more energetic throughout the day. Cardio workouts have become an essential part of my fitness routine, and I cannot imagine skipping them anymore.

Question and Answer Section

Q. Can I do cardio before lifting?

A. Yes, you can do cardio before lifting, but it's essential to warm-up before doing any exercise. A 5-10 minute warm-up before cardio or weight training can help to prevent injuries and prepare your body for the workout.

Q. How often should I do cardio after lifting?

A. It's recommended to do cardio after lifting at least 2-3 times a week. However, the frequency and duration of cardio exercises depend on your fitness level and goals.

Q. Is it okay to do cardio on rest days?

A. Yes, it's okay to do cardio on rest days, but it's essential to give your body enough rest and recovery time.

Q. Can I do cardio and weight training on the same day?

A. Yes, you can do cardio and weight training on the same day, but it's important to prioritize your workouts based on your fitness goals. If you're focusing on weight loss, it's recommended to do cardio before lifting, and if you're focusing on muscle gain, it's recommended to do weight training before cardio.

Conclusion of Cardio Workout After Lifting

Cardio workout after lifting is essential for achieving your fitness goals. It helps in burning calories faster, improving your cardiovascular health, aiding in muscle recovery and growth, and boosting your stamina and endurance. It's essential to incorporate cardio exercises gradually into your fitness routine and choose exercises that you enjoy and fit your fitness level. So, the next time you hit the gym, don't forget to add some cardio workout after lifting to your routine.
