Do You Do Cardio Before Or After Working Out

Written by Robby Oct 26, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you confused about whether to do cardio before or after your workout? You're not alone. Many people struggle with this decision, and it can be challenging to figure out what works best for you. In this blog post, we'll explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio before or after your workout, and help you make an informed decision.

If you're like most people, you want to get the most out of your workout. You want to burn the most calories, build the most muscle, and see the best results. But when it comes to cardio, it's not always clear whether you should do it before or after your workout. There are pros and cons to both approaches, and the best choice depends on your goals, fitness level, and personal preferences.

The answer to the question of whether to do cardio before or after your workout is: it depends. There is no one-size-fits-all approach to this question. The best choice for you will depend on your goals, fitness level, and personal preferences. Let's take a closer look at the benefits and drawbacks of each approach.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Before Your Workout

Personally, I prefer to do cardio before my workout. I find that it helps me warm up and get my heart rate up before I start lifting weights. Plus, it gives me an energy boost and helps me feel more focused and alert during my workout.

However, there are some drawbacks to doing cardio before your workout. For one thing, it can be tiring and make it harder to lift weights effectively. It can also increase your risk of injury if you overdo it or don't warm up properly. Additionally, if you do too much cardio before your workout, you may not have enough energy left to lift weights effectively.

Benefits and Drawbacks of Doing Cardio After Your Workout

On the other hand, some people prefer to do cardio after their workout. They find that it helps them cool down and recover after lifting weights. It can also be a good way to burn extra calories and increase your endurance.

However, there are also some drawbacks to doing cardio after your workout. For one thing, you may not have as much energy left to do cardio after lifting weights. You may also be more likely to skip your cardio workout if you're tired or don't feel like doing it after lifting weights.

How to Decide Whether to Do Cardio Before or After Your Workout

So, how do you decide whether to do cardio before or after your workout? Here are some factors to consider:

  • Your goals: If your goal is to build muscle, you may want to do cardio after your workout to avoid tiring yourself out before lifting weights. If your goal is to burn fat, you may want to do cardio before your workout to burn more calories.
  • Your fitness level: If you're new to working out, you may want to start with cardio before your workout to warm up and get your heart rate up. If you're more experienced, you may want to do cardio after your workout to avoid tiring yourself out before lifting weights.
  • Your personal preferences: Ultimately, the best choice for you will depend on your personal preferences. Try both approaches and see which one works best for you.

How to Incorporate Cardio into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate cardio into your workout routine, there are several ways to do it. Here are some ideas:

  • Do cardio before your workout to warm up and get your heart rate up. Start with a few minutes of cardio and gradually increase the time as you get more comfortable.
  • Do cardio after your workout to cool down and recover. Start with a few minutes of cardio and gradually increase the time as you get more comfortable.
  • Do cardio on separate days from your weightlifting workouts. This can be a good way to avoid tiring yourself out before lifting weights.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I do cardio every day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio every day, but it's important to vary the type and intensity of your workouts to avoid overuse injuries and burnout.

Q: How long should I do cardio for?

A: The amount of cardio you should do depends on your fitness level and goals. As a general rule, aim for at least 30 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio most days of the week.

Q: Can I do cardio and weightlifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and weightlifting on the same day, but it's important to prioritize your goals and not overdo it. Start with a few minutes of cardio before lifting weights, or do cardio after lifting weights to cool down and recover.

Q: What type of cardio is best?

A: The best type of cardio is the one you enjoy and can stick to. Try different types of cardio, such as running, cycling, swimming, or dancing, and see what works best for you.

Conclusion of Do You Do Cardio Before or After Working Out

So, whether you do cardio before or after your workout depends on your goals, fitness level, and personal preferences. There are benefits and drawbacks to both approaches, and the best choice for you will depend on your individual needs. Experiment with different approaches and see what works best for you.
