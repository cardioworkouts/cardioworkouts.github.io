What Burns More Calories Lifting Weights Or Cardio

Written by Eveline Sep 29, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you trying to lose weight and wondering if lifting weights or cardio burns more calories? You're not alone! Many people struggle with this question when starting a fitness routine. In this article, we'll explore the topic of what burns more calories lifting weights or cardio and help you understand which one is right for you.

The Pain Points of What Burns More Calories Lifting Weights or Cardio

When it comes to weight loss, there are a lot of factors to consider. It can be overwhelming to decide which exercises to do, how often to do them, and how to properly fuel your body. Additionally, many people have busy schedules and limited time to dedicate to exercise, so they want to make sure they're getting the most bang for their buck.

What Burns More Calories Lifting Weights or Cardio?

The answer to this question is not straightforward. Both lifting weights and cardio can burn calories and contribute to weight loss. However, the number of calories burned during each activity depends on several factors, such as intensity, duration, and individual body composition.

Cardio exercises, such as running, cycling, or swimming, typically burn more calories in a shorter amount of time than lifting weights. For example, a 30-minute run can burn around 300 calories, while a 30-minute weightlifting session can burn around 100-200 calories. However, weightlifting can help build muscle mass, which can increase your metabolism and help you burn more calories at rest.

The Benefits of Lifting Weights

While cardio exercises may burn more calories during the workout, lifting weights has its own set of benefits. Weightlifting can help build strength, increase bone density, and improve overall body composition. Additionally, building muscle mass can help your body burn more calories at rest, so you can continue to lose weight even when you're not actively exercising.

Personally, I found that incorporating weightlifting into my fitness routine helped me lose weight and tone my body. I noticed that my metabolism increased, and I was able to burn more calories throughout the day, even when I wasn't exercising.

The Benefits of Cardio

Cardio exercises can also contribute to weight loss and overall health. Cardiovascular exercise can improve heart health, increase endurance, and reduce stress levels. Additionally, many people find that cardio is a great way to clear their mind and boost their mood.

Personally, I enjoy going for a run or a bike ride to clear my head and get some fresh air. I find that cardio helps me feel more energized and focused throughout the day.

Combining Lifting Weights and Cardio

If you're looking to maximize your weight loss and overall health, consider incorporating both lifting weights and cardio into your fitness routine. By combining these two types of exercises, you can build muscle mass, increase your metabolism, and burn calories both during and after your workout.

Tips for Combining Lifting Weights and Cardio

If you're new to lifting weights or cardio, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity and duration. Additionally, make sure to properly fuel your body before and after your workout, and stay hydrated throughout the day. Finally, don't forget to listen to your body and rest when you need to.

My Personal Experience with Lifting Weights vs. Cardio

Personally, I've found that combining lifting weights and cardio has been the most effective way for me to lose weight and improve my overall health. I enjoy going for a run or a bike ride a few times a week, and I also make sure to lift weights a few times a week to build muscle mass and increase my metabolism.

Question and Answer

Q: Is it better to do cardio or weights first?

A: It's best to start with a warm-up, whether that's cardio or a dynamic stretching routine, and then alternate between lifting weights and cardio exercises. This will help you maximize your calorie burn and prevent injury.

Q: How often should I do cardio or lift weights?

A: It's recommended to aim for at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio per week and two strength-training sessions per week. However, the amount of exercise you need may vary depending on your individual goals and fitness level.

Q: Can lifting weights help me lose belly fat?

A: Yes, lifting weights can help you lose belly fat by building muscle mass and increasing your metabolism. Additionally, reducing your overall body fat through a combination of diet and exercise can help reduce belly fat.

Q: What's the best time of day to do cardio or lift weights?

A: The best time of day to exercise is the time that works best for you and your schedule. Some people prefer to exercise in the morning to start their day off on the right foot, while others prefer to exercise in the evening to relieve stress and wind down after a busy day.

Conclusion of What Burns More Calories Lifting Weights or Cardio

When it comes to weight loss and overall health, both lifting weights and cardio have their benefits. By incorporating both types of exercises into your fitness routine, you can maximize your calorie burn, build muscle mass, increase your metabolism, and improve your overall health and wellbeing. Remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and make sure to fuel and hydrate properly before and after your workout.
