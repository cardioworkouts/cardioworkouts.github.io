What Should You Eat After Training

Written by Daniel Aug 17, 2023 Â· 3 min read

Are you someone that exercises regularly but struggles with what to eat afterwards? You're not alone. Many people find themselves in the same predicament when it comes to post-workout meals. But the truth is, what you eat after training is just as important as the workout itself. So, let's dive into what you should eat after training and why it's important.

After a workout, your body is in a vulnerable state. Your muscles are torn and need to be repaired and your energy levels need to be replenished. That's why it's important to consume the right nutrients to aid in the recovery process.

The target of what you should eat after training is to provide your body with a combination of carbohydrates and protein. Carbohydrates will help replenish your energy levels while protein will aid in muscle recovery and growth. Additionally, it's important to consume some fats as well to help your body absorb the nutrients.

In summary, the main points to keep in mind when it comes to post-workout meals are to consume a combination of carbohydrates, protein, and fats. Now, let's take a deeper look at each of these macronutrients and what kinds of foods you should be consuming.

Carbohydrates

One of my favorite post-workout meals is a smoothie bowl. I like to blend frozen fruits like berries, bananas, and mangoes with a scoop of protein powder and some almond milk. I then top it off with some granola, shredded coconut, and chia seeds for some added crunch and healthy fats. Other great carb options include sweet potatoes, quinoa, and rice.

Protein

My go-to protein source after a workout is usually chicken or fish. However, if you're vegetarian or vegan, there are plenty of plant-based options such as tofu, tempeh, lentils, and chickpeas. Greek yogurt, cottage cheese, and eggs are also great sources of protein.

Fats

Healthy fats are essential for your body to absorb the nutrients from your post-workout meal. Some great sources of healthy fats include avocado, nuts, seeds, and nut butter.

How Much Should You Eat?

The amount of food you should consume after a workout depends on the intensity and duration of your workout. A general rule of thumb is to aim for a meal that's around 20-30% protein and 50-60% carbohydrates. Additionally, it's important to listen to your body and eat until you feel satisfied.

Conclusion of What Should You Eat After Training

Remember, what you eat after training is just as important as the workout itself. Consuming a combination of carbohydrates, protein, and fats will aid in muscle recovery, growth, and energy replenishment. Don't be afraid to get creative with your post-workout meals and find what works best for you and your body.

Question and Answer

Q: Can I eat a candy bar after a workout?

A: While a candy bar may provide some quick energy, it's not the best option for post-workout recovery. Instead, opt for a combination of carbohydrates, protein, and fats.

Q: Should I eat immediately after my workout?

A: Ideally, it's best to consume your post-workout meal within 30 minutes to an hour after your workout for optimal recovery.

Q: Is it necessary to eat after a low-intensity workout?

A: While it may not be as crucial to consume a post-workout meal after a low-intensity workout, it's still important to refuel and replenish your body with nutrients.

Q: Can I drink a protein shake instead of eating after a workout?

A: Yes, a protein shake can be a great option for post-workout recovery. Just make sure to include some carbohydrates and healthy fats in your shake as well.
