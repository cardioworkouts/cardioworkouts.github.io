Is Cardio Important When Weight Training

Written by Oliver Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Cardio is an essential component of any fitness regimen, but is it necessary when weight training? This is a common question that many people ask, especially those who are just starting out in the gym. In this blog post, we will explore the importance of cardio when weight training and how it can benefit you in achieving your fitness goals.

Many people think that weight lifting alone is enough to build muscle, burn fat, and improve overall fitness. However, this is not entirely true. While weight training is an excellent way to build strength and muscle mass, it does not provide the same cardiovascular benefits as aerobic exercise. This is where cardio comes in.

The short answer is yes, cardio is important when weight training. Cardiovascular exercise helps improve heart health, increases endurance, and aids in weight loss. When combined with weight training, cardio can also help improve overall fitness and performance in the gym.

In summary, the main benefits of incorporating cardio into your weight training routine are:

  • Improved heart health
  • Increased endurance
  • Weight loss
  • Improved overall fitness and performance in the gym

How Does Cardio Help with Weight Training?

When you do cardio, you increase your heart rate and breathing rate, which helps improve cardiovascular health. This, in turn, increases your endurance, allowing you to work out harder and longer during weight training sessions. Cardio also helps burn calories, which can aid in weight loss and fat loss.

Another way cardio helps with weight training is by improving overall fitness and performance in the gym. When you have better endurance and cardiovascular health, you can perform exercises with more intensity and for longer periods of time. This can lead to increased muscle growth and strength gains.

How Much Cardio Should You Do When Weight Training?

The amount of cardio you should do when weight training varies depending on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to build muscle mass and strength, you should focus more on weight training and do cardio as a secondary activity. Aim for 2-3 days of cardio per week, with each session lasting 20-30 minutes.

If your main goal is weight loss or improving cardiovascular health, you should aim for 3-5 days of cardio per week, with each session lasting 30-60 minutes.

The Best Types of Cardio for Weight Training

When it comes to cardio, there are many different types to choose from. The best types of cardio for weight training are those that are low impact and won't interfere with your recovery from weight training sessions. Some examples include:

  • Walking
  • Running
  • Cycling
  • Swimming
  • Elliptical

Tips for Combining Cardio and Weight Training

If you're looking to combine cardio and weight training, here are some tips to get you started:

  • Start with shorter cardio sessions and gradually increase the duration and intensity
  • Do cardio after weight training to avoid interfering with muscle recovery
  • Alternate between different types of cardio to keep it interesting and challenging
  • Listen to your body and adjust your routine as needed

Question and Answer

Q: Does cardio burn muscle?

A: No, cardio does not burn muscle. In fact, it can help improve overall fitness and performance in the gym, leading to increased muscle growth and strength gains.

Q: Is it better to do cardio before or after weight training?

A: It is generally better to do weight training before cardio to avoid interfering with muscle recovery. However, the most important thing is to make sure you are doing both types of exercise regularly.

Q: Can you lose weight with just weight training?

A: While weight training can help you build muscle and burn fat, it is not as effective for weight loss as cardio. To lose weight, you need to create a calorie deficit, which can be achieved through a combination of diet and exercise.

Q: How often should you do cardio when weight training?

A: The amount of cardio you should do when weight training depends on your fitness goals. If your main goal is to build muscle, aim for 2-3 days of cardio per week. If your main goal is weight loss or improved cardiovascular health, aim for 3-5 days of cardio per week.

Conclusion of Is Cardio Important When Weight Training

In conclusion, cardio is an essential component of any fitness regimen, including weight training. Cardio helps improve heart health, increases endurance, aids in weight loss, and improves overall fitness and performance in the gym. By incorporating cardio into your weight training routine, you can achieve your fitness goals more effectively and efficiently.
