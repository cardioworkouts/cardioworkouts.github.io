What Is Low Intensity Cardio Heart Rate A Beginner s Guide

Written by Christine Jun 08, 2023 Â· 5 min read

Are you new to the world of fitness and wondering what low intensity cardio heart rate is all about? Do you want to learn how to improve your cardiovascular health without pushing yourself too hard? If so, this guide is for you.

Many people struggle with cardiovascular exercise because they don't know their target heart rate. They often push themselves too hard, which can lead to injury and burnout. Others simply don't understand how to measure their heart rate during exercise. This is where low intensity cardio heart rate comes in.

Low intensity cardio heart rate is the heart rate range you should aim for during low intensity cardiovascular exercise. This range is typically between 60-70% of your maximum heart rate. This level of intensity is suitable for beginners, those recovering from injury or illness, and those who want to improve their cardiovascular health without putting too much stress on their body.

To summarize, low intensity cardio heart rate is the range of heart rate you should aim for during low intensity cardio exercise. It is a safe and effective way to improve your cardiovascular health without pushing your body too hard.

How Do You Calculate Your Low Intensity Cardio Heart Rate?

Personal Experience:

To calculate my low intensity cardio heart rate, I first had to calculate my maximum heart rate. To do this, I subtracted my age from 220. For example, if you are 30 years old, your maximum heart rate would be 190 (220-30=190). To find your low intensity cardio heart rate, you then multiply your maximum heart rate by 0.6 and 0.7. Using the same example, the low intensity cardio heart rate range would be 114-133 beats per minute (190 x 0.6=114 and 190 x 0.7=133).

To calculate your low intensity cardio heart rate, you first need to calculate your maximum heart rate. This can be done by subtracting your age from 220. Once you have your maximum heart rate, you can then multiply it by 0.6 and 0.7 to find your target heart rate range.
