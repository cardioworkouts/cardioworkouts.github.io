Best Cardio Workout For Beginners A Guide To Starting Your Fitness Journey

Written by Aprilia Oct 02, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you looking to start your fitness journey but don't know where to begin? Cardio workouts are a great way to get your heart pumping and burn calories, but it can be overwhelming to choose the best one for beginners. In this guide, we'll walk you through the best cardio workouts for beginners, their benefits, and how to get started.

Starting a fitness routine can be intimidating, especially if you're new to it. You may be worried about not knowing the proper form, feeling self-conscious at the gym, or not seeing results fast enough. But the benefits of starting a fitness routine far outweigh any fears or doubts you may have.

What is the Target of Best Cardio Workout for Beginners?

The target of the best cardio workout for beginners is to get your heart rate up and burn calories. Cardiovascular exercise strengthens your heart and lungs, improves circulation, and can help with weight loss. It's a great way to improve your overall health and fitness levels, and it doesn't require any expensive equipment or gym memberships.

Here are some of the best cardio workouts for beginners:

1. Walking

Walking is a low-impact cardio workout that's great for beginners. All you need is a pair of comfortable shoes and a safe place to walk. You can start by taking short walks around your neighborhood and gradually increase your distance and speed as you get more comfortable.

Personally, I started my fitness journey with walking. I would take a 30-minute walk every day, and it helped me build up my endurance and confidence. I also loved being outside and getting some fresh air while exercising.

2. Cycling

Cycling is another low-impact cardio workout that's easy on your joints. You can ride a stationary bike at home or join a cycling class at your gym. Cycling can be a fun way to explore your city or town and get some fresh air.

I love cycling because it's a great way to get a full-body workout while also enjoying the outdoors. I feel energized and refreshed after a good cycling session.

3. Swimming

Swimming is a full-body workout that's easy on your joints. It's a great way to improve your cardiovascular health and build muscle strength. You can swim laps at your local pool or take a water aerobics class.

I started swimming a few years ago, and it's now one of my favorite cardio workouts. It's a great way to cool off during the summer months and get a full-body workout at the same time.

4. Jumping Rope

Jumping rope is a high-intensity cardio workout that can burn a lot of calories in a short amount of time. It's a great way to improve your coordination and agility, and it doesn't require any expensive equipment.

If you're new to jumping rope, start with short intervals and gradually increase your time. You can also alternate between jumping rope and other cardio workouts to keep things interesting.

Conclusion of Best Cardio Workout for Beginners

Starting a fitness routine can be intimidating, but it doesn't have to be. The best cardio workouts for beginners are walking, cycling, swimming, and jumping rope. These workouts are easy on your joints, don't require any expensive equipment, and can help improve your overall health and fitness levels.

Question and Answer

Q: How often should I do cardio workouts as a beginner?

A: It's recommended to do at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity cardio exercise per week, or 75 minutes of vigorous-intensity cardio exercise per week. As a beginner, start with 3-4 cardio workouts per week and gradually increase your time and intensity.

Q: Should I do cardio workouts before or after strength training?

A: It's generally recommended to do cardio workouts after strength training. This is because strength training can deplete your glycogen stores, which your body needs for energy during cardio workouts. However, it's important to listen to your body and do what works best for you.

Q: Do I need any equipment for these cardio workouts?

A: Walking and cycling can be done with just a pair of comfortable shoes. Swimming requires access to a pool, and jumping rope requires a jump rope. You can also use a stationary bike for cycling or a treadmill for walking if you have access to a gym.

Q: How can I make my cardio workouts more challenging?

A: You can make your cardio workouts more challenging by increasing your speed, distance, or resistance. You can also try different types of cardio workouts or add intervals of high-intensity exercise.
