Should You Do Cardio Before Lifting The Ultimate Guide

Written by Petter Jun 15, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you wondering whether to do cardio before lifting? There are different opinions on this topic, and it can be confusing to know what to do. This guide will provide you with all the information you need to make an informed decision on whether to do cardio before lifting.

If you're new to the gym, you might not know whether to do cardio before or after lifting weights. It's a common question that many people ask. Some people believe that doing cardio before lifting can help you warm up and prevent injuries. Others think that doing cardio after lifting is better for building muscle mass.

The answer to the question of should you do cardio before lifting depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories. However, if you're trying to build muscle, doing cardio after lifting is better because it allows you to focus on lifting weights without getting tired from the cardio.

In summary, the best time to do cardio depends on your goals. If you're trying to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories. However, if you're trying to build muscle, doing cardio after lifting is better because it allows you to focus on lifting weights without getting tired from the cardio.

My Personal Experience with Cardio Before Lifting

When I started my fitness journey, I used to do cardio before lifting. I thought that it would help me warm up and prevent injuries. However, I found that I was getting tired quickly during my weight lifting sessions, and I wasn't able to lift as heavy as I wanted to.

After doing some research, I realized that doing cardio after lifting was better for building muscle. So, I switched my routine and started doing cardio after lifting weights. I found that I was able to focus more on lifting weights and lifting heavier without getting tired from the cardio.

The Benefits of Cardio Before Lifting

Doing cardio before lifting has some benefits. It can help you warm up and prevent injuries by increasing blood flow to your muscles. It can also help you burn more calories during your workout. If your goal is to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can be beneficial.

The Benefits of Cardio After Lifting

Doing cardio after lifting also has some benefits. It allows you to focus more on lifting weights without getting tired from the cardio. This can help you lift heavier weights and build more muscle mass. It also helps your body recover by flushing out lactic acid and increasing blood flow to your muscles.

What Happens if You Do Cardio Before Lifting?

If you do cardio before lifting, you might get tired quickly during your weight lifting session. This can prevent you from lifting as heavy as you want to and building muscle mass. It can also increase the risk of injury if you're not properly warmed up.

What Happens if You Do Cardio After Lifting?

If you do cardio after lifting, you'll be able to focus more on lifting weights and lifting heavier without getting tired from the cardio. This can help you build more muscle mass and strength. It also helps your body recover by flushing out lactic acid and increasing blood flow to your muscles.

Conclusion of Should You Do Cardio Before Lifting

In conclusion, whether you should do cardio before or after lifting depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories. However, if you're trying to build muscle, doing cardio after lifting is better because it allows you to focus on lifting weights without getting tired from the cardio.

Question and Answer

Q: Should you do cardio before or after lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness goals. If you're trying to lose weight, doing cardio before lifting can help you burn more calories. However, if you're trying to build muscle, doing cardio after lifting is better.

Q: Does doing cardio before lifting prevent injuries?

A: Doing cardio before lifting can help you warm up and increase blood flow to your muscles, which can prevent injuries. However, doing too much cardio before lifting can make you tired, which can increase the risk of injury.

Q: Can you build muscle doing cardio?

A: Cardio can help you burn fat and improve your cardiovascular health, but it's not the best way to build muscle. Lifting weights is the best way to build muscle mass and strength.

Q: How long should you do cardio before lifting?

A: It depends on your fitness level and the intensity of your workout. Generally, 10-20 minutes of cardio before lifting is enough to warm up and prevent injuries.
