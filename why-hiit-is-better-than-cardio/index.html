Why Hiit Is Better Than Cardio

Written by Luffy Oct 10, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you tired of spending long hours on the treadmill or stationary bike without seeing the results you want? Do you want to maximize your workout and burn more calories in less time? Then it's time to give HIIT a try. HIIT, or High-Intensity Interval Training, is a form of exercise that has been gaining popularity in recent years for its many benefits over traditional cardio workouts. In this post, we'll explore why HIIT is better than cardio and how you can incorporate it into your fitness routine.

Pain Points

Many people find traditional cardio workouts boring and time-consuming. They may spend hours on the treadmill or elliptical machine without seeing the results they want. Cardio workouts can also be hard on the joints and cause injuries if not done properly. Additionally, cardio workouts only burn calories during the workout, whereas HIIT workouts continue to burn calories long after the workout is over. These pain points are why HIIT is becoming a popular alternative to traditional cardio workouts.

Why HIIT is better than Cardio

HIIT involves short bursts of high-intensity exercise followed by periods of rest or low-intensity exercise. This type of workout has been shown to be more effective at burning fat and building muscle than traditional cardio workouts. HIIT workouts also increase your metabolism and continue to burn calories long after the workout is over. Additionally, HIIT workouts are shorter in duration than traditional cardio workouts, making them more time-efficient. Finally, HIIT workouts can be modified to suit any fitness level, from beginners to advanced athletes.

Personal Experience

As someone who used to spend hours on the treadmill without seeing results, I was hesitant to try HIIT at first. But once I started incorporating HIIT workouts into my routine, I noticed a significant difference in my fitness level and body composition. I was able to burn more calories in less time and see visible results in a shorter period. HIIT workouts also challenged me in a way that traditional cardio workouts never did, making my workouts more enjoyable and motivating.

How to Incorporate HIIT into Your Fitness Routine

There are many ways to incorporate HIIT into your fitness routine. One popular method is to do a Tabata-style workout, which involves 20 seconds of high-intensity exercise followed by 10 seconds of rest, repeated for 4 minutes. You can also do a circuit-style workout, where you perform a series of exercises back-to-back with little to no rest in between. Another option is to do a pyramid-style workout, where you start with a short interval and gradually increase the duration and intensity of each interval. There are many free resources online to find HIIT workouts that suit your fitness level and goals.

The Science Behind HIIT

HIIT has been shown to be more effective at burning fat and building muscle than traditional cardio workouts because of the way it affects the body's metabolism. HIIT workouts cause the body to enter a state of EPOC, or Excess Post-Exercise Oxygen Consumption, which means the body continues to burn calories long after the workout is over. This is because HIIT workouts cause a metabolic disturbance in the body, which in turn leads to an increase in metabolism and calorie burn. Additionally, HIIT workouts have been shown to improve cardiovascular health and insulin sensitivity, making them a great option for overall health and fitness.

Tips for Getting Started with HIIT

If you're new to HIIT, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase the intensity and duration of your workouts. It's also important to listen to your body and rest when needed. Proper form is also important to prevent injury, so be sure to research proper technique for each exercise you plan to do. Finally, make sure to fuel your body with the proper nutrients before and after your workouts to maximize your results.

FAQs about HIIT

Q: How often should I do HIIT workouts?

A: It's recommended to do HIIT workouts 2-3 times per week, with rest days in between to allow for recovery.

Q: Can anyone do HIIT workouts?

A: Yes, HIIT workouts can be modified to suit any fitness level, from beginners to advanced athletes.

Q: How long should a typical HIIT workout be?

A: A typical HIIT workout can range from 10-30 minutes, depending on the intensity and duration of each interval.

Q: What are some examples of exercises to include in a HIIT workout?

A: Some examples of exercises to include in a HIIT workout are burpees, jumping jacks, mountain climbers, high knees, and squat jumps.

Conclusion

Overall, HIIT is a highly effective and efficient form of exercise that has many benefits over traditional cardio workouts. By incorporating HIIT into your fitness routine, you can maximize your workouts and see visible results in a shorter period of time. Whether you're a beginner or an advanced athlete, HIIT can be modified to suit your fitness level and goals, making it a great option for anyone looking to improve their health and fitness.
