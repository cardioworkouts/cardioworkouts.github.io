Is Strength Training Anaerobic

Written by Robby Oct 22, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you a fitness enthusiast who is interested in strength training? Do you want to know if strength training is anaerobic? If yes, then you have come to the right place.

Strength training is one of the most popular forms of exercise, but it can be confusing to know whether or not it is anaerobic. People often have questions about the type of training they should do to achieve their fitness goals. This is where understanding the concept of anaerobic exercise comes into play.

In this article, we will discuss whether strength training is anaerobic or not and answer some related questions.

What is Anaerobic Exercise?

Before we answer the main question, let's first understand what anaerobic exercise is. Anaerobic exercise is a type of exercise that involves short bursts of high-intensity activity. During such activities, the body's demand for oxygen is greater than the oxygen supply available, forcing the body to use other energy sources to fuel the activity.

Examples of anaerobic exercises include sprinting, weightlifting, and high-intensity interval training (HIIT).

Is Strength Training Anaerobic?

Yes, strength training is considered anaerobic because it typically involves short bursts of high-intensity activity. When you lift weights, you are performing short, intense sets of exercises that require a lot of energy. In these moments, your body relies on stored energy sources, such as creatine phosphate and glycogen, to fuel the activity.

However, it's important to note that strength training can also be aerobic if you perform it for longer durations with less intensity. For example, performing a high number of repetitions with lighter weights will cause your heart rate to increase and your body to use oxygen as a fuel source.

Benefits of Anaerobic Exercise

Now that we know that strength training is anaerobic, let's look at some of the benefits of this type of exercise:

1. Builds Muscle Mass: Anaerobic exercise, such as strength training, helps build muscle mass and increase strength.

2. Increases Metabolic Rate: Anaerobic exercise increases the metabolic rate, leading to more calories burned throughout the day.

3. Improves Bone Density: Anaerobic exercise, such as weightlifting, can improve bone density and reduce the risk of osteoporosis.

4. Boosts Endurance: Anaerobic exercise helps improve endurance and stamina by increasing the body's ability to perform high-intensity activities for longer durations.

How to Incorporate Anaerobic Exercise into Your Workout Routine

If you want to incorporate anaerobic exercise into your workout routine, here are some tips:

1. Lift Weights: Strength training is a great way to perform anaerobic exercise. You can incorporate weightlifting into your routine by performing exercises such as squats, deadlifts, and bench press.

2. HIIT: High-intensity interval training (HIIT) is another effective way to perform anaerobic exercise. You can incorporate HIIT into your routine by performing exercises such as sprints, burpees, and jump squats.

3. Plyometrics: Plyometric exercises involve explosive movements that require a lot of energy. You can incorporate plyometrics into your routine by performing exercises such as box jumps, jump lunges, and clap push-ups.

Question and Answer

Q1. Can you perform anaerobic exercise without weights?

A. Yes, you can perform anaerobic exercise without weights. Examples include sprinting, plyometrics, and bodyweight exercises such as push-ups and pull-ups.

Q2. How often should you perform anaerobic exercise?

A. It is recommended to perform anaerobic exercise 2-3 times per week, with at least 48 hours between sessions to allow for proper recovery.

Q3. How long should anaerobic exercise sessions last?

A. Anaerobic exercise sessions should last between 20-45 minutes, depending on the intensity of the activity.

Q4. Is anaerobic exercise safe for everyone?

A. No, anaerobic exercise is not safe for everyone. People with certain medical conditions, such as heart disease, should consult with their doctor before performing anaerobic exercise.

Conclusion of Is Strength Training Anaerobic

In conclusion, strength training is considered anaerobic because it involves short bursts of high-intensity activity. Anaerobic exercise has many benefits, including building muscle mass, increasing metabolic rate, improving bone density, and boosting endurance. If you want to incorporate anaerobic exercise into your routine, consider lifting weights, performing HIIT, or doing plyometric exercises. However, it's important to consult with your doctor before starting any new exercise routine, especially if you have any medical conditions.
