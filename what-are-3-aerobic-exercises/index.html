What Are 3 Aerobic Exercises A Beginner s Guide

Written by Oliver Aug 24, 2023 · 4 min read

Are you looking for a way to improve your cardiovascular health and burn some calories? Aerobic exercises are a great way to achieve both! However, if you're new to exercising, you may be wondering what are 3 aerobic exercises that you can add to your routine. In this post, we'll explore the benefits of aerobic exercises and provide you with three exercises to get you started.

A lot of people struggle with finding an exercise routine that they enjoy and can stick to. One of the biggest pain points is feeling like exercise is a chore. However, aerobic exercises can be a fun way to get your heart pumping and release endorphins. Plus, they have numerous health benefits, including improving cardiovascular health, increasing lung capacity, and reducing the risk of chronic diseases.

So, what are 3 aerobic exercises that you can try? Three great exercises to get started with are running, cycling, and swimming.

Running

Running is a great aerobic exercise that requires no equipment besides a good pair of running shoes. You can run outdoors or on a treadmill. It's a high-impact exercise, so it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity to avoid injury. Running is great for improving cardiovascular health, burning calories, and reducing stress.

Personally, I started running by doing a run-walk interval program. I started with running for one minute and walking for two minutes and gradually increased my running time until I could run for 30 minutes straight. It's important to listen to your body and not push yourself too hard too fast.

Cycling

Cycling is another great aerobic exercise that can be done indoors or outdoors. You can use a stationary bike or a regular bike. Cycling is a low-impact exercise, so it's a great option for people with joint pain. It's also great for improving cardiovascular health, building leg muscles, and burning calories.

When I started cycling, I joined a spin class at my gym. It was a great way to learn proper form and get a great workout in. If you're new to cycling, it may be helpful to take a class or watch some instructional videos to make sure you're doing it correctly.

Swimming

Swimming is a low-impact exercise that is great for people of all ages and fitness levels. It's a full-body workout that improves cardiovascular health, builds muscle, and improves flexibility. You can swim in a pool or in open water if you're an experienced swimmer.

Personally, I learned how to swim as an adult and found it to be a great way to get a workout in without feeling like I was exercising. It's a great option for people who don't enjoy traditional forms of exercise.

Benefits of Aerobic Exercise

In addition to the specific benefits of running, cycling, and swimming, there are numerous benefits to aerobic exercise in general. Aerobic exercise can improve your mood, reduce anxiety and depression, and improve cognitive function. It can also reduce the risk of chronic diseases such as heart disease, diabetes, and certain types of cancer.

Tips for Getting Started

If you're new to aerobic exercise, it's important to start slowly and gradually increase your intensity. It's also important to listen to your body and rest if you're feeling tired or sore. Here are some tips for getting started:

  1. Choose an exercise that you enjoy
  2. Start slowly and gradually increase your intensity
  3. Set realistic goals
  4. Listen to your body and rest when needed
  5. Stay hydrated
  6. Be consistent

FAQs

Q: How often should I do aerobic exercise?

A: It's recommended that adults get at least 150 minutes of moderate-intensity aerobic exercise per week. This can be broken down into 30 minutes a day, five days a week.

Q: Can I do aerobic exercise if I have joint pain?

A: Yes, there are low-impact options such as cycling and swimming that are great for people with joint pain.

Q: Do I need any special equipment for aerobic exercise?

A: It depends on the exercise. Running requires a good pair of running shoes, cycling requires a bike (either stationary or regular), and swimming requires access to a pool or open water.

Q: How long does it take to see the benefits of aerobic exercise?

A: You may start to feel the benefits of aerobic exercise after just a few weeks. However, it may take several months to see significant improvements in cardiovascular health and other health markers.

Conclusion of What Are 3 Aerobic Exercises

Aerobic exercise is a great way to improve your cardiovascular health, burn calories, and reduce the risk of chronic diseases. Running, cycling, and swimming are three great options to get started with. Remember to start slowly, listen to your body, and stay consistent. With time, you'll start to see the benefits of aerobic exercise.
