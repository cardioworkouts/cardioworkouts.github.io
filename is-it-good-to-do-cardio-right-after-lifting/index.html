Is It Good To Do Cardio Right After Lifting

Written by Frank Oct 31, 2023 Â· 4 min read

Are you one of those people who hit the gym for both weightlifting and cardio exercises? Have you ever wondered whether it's okay to do cardio right after lifting weights? Well, you're not alone. This post will explore the benefits and drawbacks of doing cardio right after lifting weights and help you decide whether it's good for you.

Pain Points Related to Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

Many people wonder whether doing cardio right after lifting is a good idea. This is because some fitness enthusiasts believe that doing cardio after lifting can interfere with muscle growth and recovery. Others believe that doing cardio before lifting can drain your energy, making it difficult to lift heavy weights.

Answering the Target of Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

The truth is, there is no one-size-fits-all answer to this question. Whether it's good to do cardio right after lifting depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. However, there are some benefits and drawbacks to doing cardio after lifting that you should be aware of.

Summary of Main Points

In summary, doing cardio after lifting can help you burn more calories, improve your cardiovascular health, and increase your endurance. However, it can also interfere with muscle growth and recovery and make it difficult to lift heavy weights. Ultimately, the decision to do cardio after lifting depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences.

The Benefits and Drawbacks of Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

When I first started going to the gym, I used to do cardio before lifting weights. However, I soon realized that doing cardio before lifting drained my energy, making it difficult to lift heavy weights. I then started doing cardio after lifting, and I noticed that it helped me burn more calories and improve my cardiovascular health.

However, I also noticed that doing cardio after lifting sometimes made me feel tired and sore the next day. I soon realized that this was because doing cardio after lifting can interfere with muscle growth and recovery. So, I started doing cardio on separate days from lifting weights, and I found that this worked better for me.

The Science Behind Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

Research has shown that doing cardio after lifting can interfere with muscle growth and recovery by reducing the amount of glycogen available in your muscles. Glycogen is the primary fuel source for your muscles, and when you do cardio after lifting, your body uses up some of this glycogen, making it harder for your muscles to recover and grow.

However, doing cardio after lifting can also improve your cardiovascular health and endurance by increasing your heart rate and oxygen consumption. This can help you burn more calories and improve your overall fitness.

How to Decide Whether to Do Cardio Right After Lifting

The decision to do cardio right after lifting ultimately depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. If your goal is to build muscle mass, it's best to avoid doing cardio right after lifting and instead focus on lifting heavy weights with proper form. However, if your goal is to improve your cardiovascular health and endurance, doing cardio after lifting can be beneficial.

Personal Experience with Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

When I first started going to the gym, I used to do cardio after lifting weights. However, I soon realized that this made me feel tired and sore the next day, and it interfered with my muscle growth and recovery. So, I started doing cardio on separate days from lifting weights, and I found that this worked better for me.

Question and Answer Section

Q: Does doing cardio after lifting burn more fat?

A: Yes, doing cardio after lifting can help you burn more fat by increasing your heart rate and oxygen consumption.

Q: Will doing cardio after lifting make me lose muscle?

A: Doing cardio after lifting can interfere with muscle growth and recovery by reducing the amount of glycogen available in your muscles. However, if you eat enough protein and carbs and give your muscles time to recover, you can still build muscle while doing cardio.

Q: How long should I wait to do cardio after lifting?

A: It's best to wait at least 30 minutes to an hour after lifting weights before doing cardio. This will give your muscles time to recover and replenish glycogen.

Q: Can I do cardio and lifting on the same day?

A: Yes, you can do cardio and lifting on the same day, but it's best to do them at separate times to avoid interference with muscle growth and recovery.

Conclusion of Doing Cardio Right After Lifting

In conclusion, whether it's good to do cardio right after lifting depends on your fitness goals and personal preferences. While doing cardio after lifting can help you burn more calories and improve your cardiovascular health, it can also interfere with muscle growth and recovery. So, it's best to experiment with different routines and find what works best for you.
